Mean free path calculator

The present tool evaluates the mean free path of one or more species in a gas mixture from the VSS collision parameters of each species, and reports the DSMC numerical bounds \(\Delta x \lesssim \lambda/3\) and \(\Delta t \lesssim \tau_c/3\) that the mesh and time-step must respect (see Kinetic foundations and the DSMC algorithm). A short derivation and the VSS parameter convention used by the tool are given at the end of the page.

The calculator runs entirely in the browser: no input is sent to any server. A dozen monatomic, diatomic, and polyatomic species are preloaded with Bird’s reference VSS parameters [Bird1994m]; custom parameters may be entered directly in the table and propagate through the calculation on the next update.

The mean free path of species s in a mixture is evaluated through Bird's VSS mixture formula:

λs = 1 / Σt   nt · π · dst2 · (Tref / T)ωst − 1/2 · (1 + ms/mt)1/2

with cross-pair averages dst = (ds + dt) / 2 and ωst = (ωs + ωt) / 2, the reference temperature Tref = 273 K, and the per-species number density nt = xt · p / (kB T). The mean thermal speed c̄s = √(8 kB T / π ms) gives the mean collision time τc,s = λs / c̄s.

Background

Derivation

For a single species at temperature \(T\) and number density \(n\), the mean free path in the VHS/VSS model follows from the average collision rate per molecule,

\[\nu_{\mathrm{coll}} = n\,\langle \sigma_T\, g \rangle = n\,\sigma_{\mathrm{ref}}\, \left(\tfrac{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}{T}\right)^{\omega - 1/2}\, \sqrt{\tfrac{8 k_B T}{\pi\,m}}\,\sqrt{2},\]

by dividing the mean thermal speed \(\bar{c} = \sqrt{8 k_B T / \pi m}\) by the collision rate and simplifying. The result is

\[\lambda = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}\,\pi\,n\,d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}\, (T_{\mathrm{ref}}/T)^{\omega - 1/2}}.\]

For a mixture, the path length that a given species \(s\) can travel before colliding with any species is the reciprocal of the sum of the per-pair collision frequencies. In Bird’s VSS mixture formula [Bird1994m],

\[\lambda_s = \frac{1} {\displaystyle\sum_{t} n_t\,\pi\,d_{st}^{2}\, \left(\tfrac{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}{T}\right)^{\omega_{st} - 1/2}\, \left(1 + \tfrac{m_s}{m_t}\right)^{1/2}},\]

with pair averages \(d_{st} = (d_s + d_t)/2\) and \(\omega_{st} = (\omega_s + \omega_t)/2\). This is the formula used by the calculator.

DSMC numerical constraints

The DSMC convergence conditions of Kinetic foundations and the DSMC algorithm require that the spatial and temporal resolution respect the local kinetic scales:

\[\Delta x \lesssim \frac{\lambda}{3}, \qquad \Delta t \lesssim \frac{\tau_c}{3},\]

with \(\tau_c = \lambda / \bar{c}\) the mean collision time. Violating either bound introduces a bias in the collision statistics that is difficult to diagnose a posteriori. In a multi-species mixture, the binding constraint is the smallest \(\lambda_s\) across all species present — typically the heaviest, slowest species at the highest local pressure. The calculator identifies this binding species and reports the corresponding \(\Delta x\) and \(\Delta t\) bounds.

On the sub-cell machinery

When the flow spans several decades in pressure or temperature, a fixed mesh cannot satisfy \(\Delta x < \lambda/3\) everywhere simultaneously. The solver’s sub-cell adaptation layer (dsmcDynamicAdapter — sub-cell and weight adaptation) is the operational fix: each cell is logically subdivided, on the fly, into a number of sub-cells per direction that keeps the sub-cell size below the local mean free path. The calculator’s \(\Delta x\) recommendation therefore applies either to the mesh cell size (in non-adaptive runs) or to the effective sub-cell size (in adaptive runs).

Notes and caveats

  • The preloaded VSS parameters are Bird’s reference values at \(T_{\mathrm{ref}} = 273~\mathrm{K}\). More recent fits exist in the literature for individual species; users are encouraged to override the d_ref and ω entries directly in the table if they have more appropriate values.

  • The calculator assumes a well-mixed, single-temperature gas. Under thermal non-equilibrium (Tt ≠ Tint) the mean free path depends only on the translational temperature and on the ground-state populations through \(n_s\); the numbers above therefore remain the right first-order estimate provided \(T\) is understood as the translational temperature.

  • The Knudsen classification is the standard textbook one (continuum < 0.01, slip < 0.1, transitional < 10, free molecular otherwise); it should be treated as indicative rather than sharp.

[Bird1994m] (1,2)

G. A. Bird, Molecular Gas Dynamics and the Direct Simulation of Gas Flows, Oxford University Press (1994).
