Kinetic foundations and the DSMC algorithm

The present section establishes the kinetic-theoretic foundations on which the solver rests, states the regime of validity in terms of dimensionless numbers, and describes the DSMC algorithm at the level of detail required to interpret the input parameters and the numerical diagnostics produced by the code.

The Boltzmann equation

For a dilute monatomic gas, the single-particle phase-space distribution \(f(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}, t)\) satisfies

\[\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} + \mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} f + \mathbf{F}\cdot\nabla_{\mathbf{v}} f = \mathcal{C}[f, f],\]

with \(\mathbf{F}\) an external body force (per unit mass) and the binary-collision operator

\[\mathcal{C}[f, f](\mathbf{v}) = \int\!\mathrm{d}\mathbf{v}_{\star}\int\!\mathrm{d}\Omega\; |\mathbf{v} - \mathbf{v}_{\star}|\, \sigma(g, \chi)\, \bigl[f'\,f'_{\star} - f\,f_{\star}\bigr],\]

where \(g = |\mathbf{v} - \mathbf{v}_{\star}|\), \(\sigma(g, \chi)\) is the differential cross section at relative speed \(g\) and centre-of-mass scattering angle \(\chi\), and primes denote post-collision velocities. For a polyatomic mixture with internal states indexed by \(\mathrm{s}\), one carries one distribution \(f_{\mathrm{s}}\) per state and the collision operator becomes a sum over reactant states of the corresponding state-to-state cross sections.

Three assumptions underpin this form and are carried into DSMC:

  • Molecular chaos — the joint distribution of two colliding molecules factorises, \(f^{(2)}(\mathbf{v}, \mathbf{v}_{\star}) = f(\mathbf{v})\,f(\mathbf{v}_{\star})\).

  • Binary collisions only — three- and higher-body collisions are negligible, which holds for \(n d^{3} \ll 1\) with \(n\) the number density and \(d\) the collision diameter.

  • Point collisions — the collision event is localised at a single point despite the finite collision diameter.

Detailed balance and the H-theorem

Detailed balance is the microscopic statement that, at thermodynamic equilibrium, the forward and reverse rates of every elementary event are equal. Combined with time-reversal symmetry of the collision kernel, it implies Boltzmann’s H-theorem,

\[\frac{\mathrm{d}H}{\mathrm{d}t} \le 0, \qquad H = \int f\,\ln f\;\mathrm{d}\mathbf{v},\]

with equality if and only if \(f\) is Maxwell-Boltzmann. The theorem is the formal justification for interpreting the equilibrium distribution of a DSMC simulation — once transients have decayed — as the correct physical one.

Knudsen number and flow regimes

The regime of validity is characterised by the Knudsen number

\[\mathrm{Kn} = \frac{\lambda}{L},\]

with \(\lambda\) the mean free path and \(L\) a characteristic flow length. Standard classification:

Regime

Description

\(\mathrm{Kn} < 10^{-2}\)

Continuum: Navier-Stokes with no-slip boundary conditions is adequate.

\(10^{-2} < \mathrm{Kn} < 10^{-1}\)

Slip: Navier-Stokes with velocity-slip and temperature-jump conditions on walls.

\(10^{-1} < \mathrm{Kn} < 10\)

Transitional: neither continuum nor free-molecular; DSMC is the method of choice.

\(\mathrm{Kn} > 10\)

Free-molecular: particles collide almost exclusively with walls; collisionless kinetic-theory expressions apply.

The mean free path in a mixture of species with VHS cross sections is

\[\lambda = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}\,\pi\,n\,d^{2}}\, \left(\frac{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}{T}\right)^{\omega - 1/2},\]

where \(n\) is the total number density, \(d\) is the reference VHS diameter, and \(\omega\) is the viscosity exponent. The mean collision time \(\tau_c\) is \(\lambda / \bar{c}\) with \(\bar{c}\) the mean thermal speed.

The DSMC time-step

DSMC does not solve the Boltzmann equation directly. Instead it follows a statistically equivalent particle system, decoupling motion and collisions on a sub-mean-collision-time step. One global time-step \(\Delta t\) of the cloud executes, in order:

  1. Advection. Each simulator particle is advected ballistically over \(\Delta t\) under the current body force. Parcel-boundary interactions are processed as they arise (walls, cyclic patches, inflow/outflow, processor patches); rovibronic-aware walls resample the internal state from the wall temperature, other patches leave it unchanged.

  2. Collisions. In each computational cell of volume \(V_c\), collision candidates are generated through the selected partner-selection scheme (Collision sampling) and accepted with probability \(\sigma_T\,g / (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\). On acceptance, the binary collision model (Collision cross sections) rotates the relative velocity and redistributes internal energy through Larsen-Borgnakke sampling (Larsen-Borgnakke energy redistribution); reactive channels (Neutral chemistry) are tested in the same pass.

  3. Controllers. Configured flow-state controllers (see Adaptation and controllers) sample their target quantities and, if due, act on the parcel population.

  4. Measurement. Per-cell accumulators update the primitive moments from which macroscopic fields are later built (Macroscopic properties); per-patch accumulators do the same on boundary faces.

  5. Adaptation. If the adaptive machinery (Adaptation and controllers) is enabled and the configured adaptation interval has elapsed, sub-cell levels and/or parcel weights are recomputed and \(\Delta t\) may be revised.

Equivalent-particles factor

Each simulator particle represents \(F_N\) real molecules (nEquivalentParticles in the input dictionary). The physical collision rate scales as \(n^{2}\), so the simulated collision candidates must scale as \(F_N \cdot N^{2}\); the No-Time- Counter scheme absorbs this scaling into its expected-candidates formula (see Collision sampling). Equivalently, \(F_N\) converts between simulator and physical cross sections at every step and is the single knob that trades statistical resolution against cost: halving \(F_N\) doubles the particle count per cell and halves the statistical variance.

Numerical convergence conditions

Three conditions must be satisfied jointly for DSMC to reproduce the Boltzmann solution to leading order:

\[\Delta x < \frac{\lambda}{3}, \qquad \Delta t < \frac{\tau_c}{3}, \qquad N_{\mathrm{cell}} \gtrsim 10\text{–}20.\]

The first two are conditions on the discretisation and are the reason sub-cell refinement is valuable in regions of steep gradients (Adaptation and controllers); the third is a statistical condition whose violation merely amplifies noise. Bird [Bird1994f] gives a thorough discussion of the bias introduced by violating each condition, which we do not repeat here.

Statistical convergence and errors

A cell-level macroscopic field \(\langle Q\rangle\) is estimated from \(N\) independent samples as

\[\overline{Q} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} Q_i, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(\overline{Q}) = \frac{\mathrm{Var}(Q)}{N},\]

so that the relative statistical error on a macroscopic quantity decays as \(1/\sqrt{N}\). Resolving a 1% macroscopic quantity requires of the order of \(10^{4}\) independent samples per cell; in steady flows this cost is absorbed through temporal averaging after the transient phase has decayed; in unsteady flows it is absorbed through ensemble averaging over independent runs. The measureErrors switch on dsmcVolFields (see Measurements) writes the per-cell standard deviations alongside the averaged fields so that the statistical confidence intervals can be reconstructed post-hoc.

Motivating the rovibronic extension

The stock DSMC internal-energy model carries, on each particle, a continuous rotational and vibrational energy, and exchanges energy with the translational mode through the Larsen-Borgnakke model. Under thermodynamic equilibrium, this reproduces the Boltzmann distribution correctly. Under strong non-equilibrium, however, two pathologies emerge:

  • The tail of the internal-energy distribution deviates substantially from the Boltzmann form in strong shocks and rapid expansions, yet it is precisely the tail that drives dissociation and radiation.

  • The electronic mode is altogether absent in the continuous formulation, or at best represented by a single average energy, making any coupling with radiation transport solvers semi-phenomenological at best.

The rovibronic extension replaces the continuous internal-energy variable by an indexed triple \((n, v, J)\) that selects one level in a pre-tabulated three-dimensional database (Rovibronic level structure). Energies, degeneracies, and reaction thresholds are then evaluated from Dunham-fitted coefficients derived from ab-initio potential-energy surfaces. Concrete benefits include non-Boltzmann-tail-resolved chemistry [Civrais2024ChemI_f], [Civrais2024ChemII_f], and the direct coupling with radiation transport solvers [Civrais2024Electronic_f].
