Neutral chemistry

The present section derives the reactive channels of neutral-species chemistry as implemented in the solver: the Quantum-Kinetic (QK) model, its Total-Collision-Energy (TCE) ancestor, and the post-reaction redistribution of internal energy. Charged-species reactions, which inherit the same statistical machinery but require additional care around the electron population, are treated separately in Charged-species chemistry.

Total-Collision-Energy (TCE) model (Not implemented yet)

Warning

The TCE model is not implemented in the current build. It is described here for completeness and as the historical baseline against which the rovibronic QK model of the next section is compared; the solver reads state-resolved QK parameters only, and a case with reactionModel set to a TCE-style class will not run. The section below may therefore be skipped on first reading.

Bird’s TCE model [Bird1994ch] computes the reaction probability from the collision energy \(E_c\), the activation energy \(E_a\), and a fit to an experimental Arrhenius rate \(k(T) = A\, T^{b}\,\exp(-E_a/k_B T)\). Starting from the equilibrium rate constant

\[k^{\mathrm{eq}}(T) = \int_{E_a}^{\infty} P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{TCE}}(E_c)\, \sigma_T(E_c)\, g(E_c)\, f^{\mathrm{eq}}(E_c; T)\, \mathrm{d}E_c,\]

and matching to the Arrhenius form for a VHS gas yields the closed-form reaction probability

\[P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{TCE}}(E_c) = C_1\,\left(\frac{E_c - E_a}{E_c}\right)^{C_2} \left(\frac{E_c}{k_B T_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)^{C_3} \Theta(E_c - E_a),\]

where \(\Theta\) is the Heaviside step and the three coefficients \(C_1, C_2, C_3\) are derived analytically from \(A, b, \omega\):

\[C_1 = \frac{A\,(\omega - 1/2)!\,T_{\mathrm{ref}}^{1 - \omega}} {(b + 3/2 - \omega)!\,(m_{12}/2k_B)^{1/2}},\]
\[C_2 = b + \tfrac{1}{2} - \omega, \qquad C_3 = 1 - \omega + b.\]

The TCE model is a global reaction model: every collision above \(E_a\) has the same reaction probability regardless of the reactants’ internal states. This is its virtue (no per-state data required) and its limitation (it cannot describe the influence of excited-state populations on reaction rates).

Quantum-Kinetic (QK) model — full derivation

The QK model of Bird [Bird2011qk], in its rovibronic extension adopted here [Civrais2024ChemI_ch], replaces the TCE activation energy by a state-dependent threshold for each reactant \((n, v, J)\) triple. The derivation proceeds in four steps.

Step 1 — microscopic reaction probability. We start from the principle that the reaction can only proceed when the collision energy exceeds the state-specific dissociation energy \(E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}\). For a VHS cross section the post-threshold reaction probability derives from the classical line-of-centres model:

\[P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{QK}}(E_c; n, v, J) = \left(1 - \frac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}}{E_c}\right)^{3/2 - \omega} \Theta\!\bigl(E_c - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}\bigr),\]

where \(E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n, v, J)} = D_0 - E(n, v, J)\) is the energy needed to break the bond given that the molecule already carries internal energy \(E(n, v, J)\).

Step 2 — state-resolved reaction rate. Integrating the above against the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution of collision energies for a VHS gas,

\[f^{\mathrm{eq}}(E_c; T) = \frac{E_c^{3/2 - \omega}} {(k_B T)^{5/2 - \omega}\,\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)}\, \exp\!\left(-\frac{E_c}{k_B T}\right),\]

yields the state-resolved rate

\[k^{\mathrm{QK}}_{nvJ}(T) = H(T)\, \Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{5}{2} - \omega,\; \frac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n, v, J)}}{k_B T}\right),\]

where \(H(T)\) is the VHS binary-rate kernel

\[H(T) = \sigma_{\mathrm{ref}}^{\mathrm{VHS}}\, \sqrt{\frac{8\,k_B T}{\pi\,\mu_{12}}}\, \left(\frac{T}{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)^{1/2 - \omega} \frac{1}{\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)},\]

and \(\Gamma(s, x)\) is the upper incomplete gamma function.

Step 3 — Boltzmann-weighted total rate. Averaging over the reactant’s internal-state population yields the macroscopic rate

\[k^{\mathrm{QK}}(T) = \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{n, v, J} g(n, v, J)\, e^{-E(n,v,J)/k_B T}\, H(T)\,\Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{5}{2} - \omega,\; \tfrac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}}{k_B T}\right)} {Q_{\mathrm{int}}(T)},\]

with \(Q_{\mathrm{int}}(T)\) the internal partition function (see Rovibronic level structure). The summand makes manifest that excited states contribute disproportionately to the rate, because their dissociation threshold \(D_0 - E(n, v, J)\) is smaller than the ground-state value \(D_0\).

Step 4 — acceptance-rejection at the collision. At run-time, each collision is tested against the QK probability

\[P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{QK}}(E_c; n_P, v_P, J_P) = \left(1 - \frac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n_P, v_P, J_P)}}{E_c}\right)^{3/2 - \omega} \Theta\!\bigl(E_c - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n_P, v_P, J_P)}\bigr),\]

drawn for the dissociating parcel \(P\) only; the partner parcel \(Q\) merely supplies its share of the collision energy. If the probability exceeds the uniform random draw, the reaction fires and the post-reaction energy redistribution begins.

Post-reaction redistribution of internal energy

Once a reaction has fired, the pooled energy available to the products is

\[E_{\mathrm{avail}} = E_c + \Delta H - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n_P, v_P, J_P)},\]

where \(E_c = E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(P) + E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q)\) is the collision energy pool, \(\Delta H\) is the heat of reaction (automatically read from the database for dissociation, recombination, and exchange; cf. Neutral chemistry). The redistribution proceeds in the order below.

Product-state enumeration. For a dissociation \(\mathrm{AB}(n, v, J) + \mathrm{M} \to \mathrm{A}(n_1) + \mathrm{B}(n_2) + \mathrm{M}\), the product atoms’ electronic states \((n_1, n_2)\) are determined at initialisation by the asymptote-matching algorithm below. For an exchange reaction, two molecular / atomic products must be sampled.

Larsen-Borgnakke sampling for the first product. The first product’s state \((n', v', J')_{\mathrm{AC}}\) is sampled by acceptance-rejection,

\[f_{1}(E_{\mathrm{AC}}') = g(E_{\mathrm{AC}}')\, \left(\frac{E_{\mathrm{avail}} - E_{\mathrm{AC}}'}{E_{\mathrm{avail}}}\right)^{\alpha_{1}},\]

with exponent

\[\alpha_{1} = \frac{\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \xi_{\mathrm{AC}}^{\mathrm{int}} + \xi_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{int}}}{2} - 1,\]

where the exponent pools the translational DOF and the internal DOF of every product whose state has not yet been sampled.

Energy update. The remaining pool is

\[E_{\mathrm{avail}}' = E_{\mathrm{avail}} - E_{\mathrm{AC}}'.\]

Second-product state. The second product’s state is sampled in the same way, with exponent

\[\alpha_{2} = \frac{\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \xi_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{int}}}{2} - 1,\]

and energy \(E_{\mathrm{B}}'\) drawn against \(E_{\mathrm{avail}}'\).

Post-reaction translational energy. The remaining pool,

\[E_{\mathrm{rel}}' = E_{\mathrm{avail}}' - E_{\mathrm{B}}',\]

becomes the translational kinetic energy of the products’ relative motion. The post-reaction relative speed is \(g' = \sqrt{2 E_{\mathrm{rel}}'/\mu'}\), with \(\mu'\) the reduced mass of the products, and the product velocities follow from the conservation laws applied to the product pair.

Detailed balance. The sequential acceptance-rejection above reproduces the thermodynamically-weighted product-state distribution at equilibrium, and the forward and backward rates of any channel are connected through the equilibrium constant. This is the key property that allows the recombination rate to be derived parameter-free from the dissociation rate (below).

Dissociation with potential-energy-curve asymptote matching

When the molecule AB dissociates from electronic state \(n\), the electronic states of the product atoms are determined by matching the PEC asymptote. Define the asymptote energy

\[\mathrm{asymptote}(n) = T_e^{(n)} + E_d^{(n)} - E^{(0)}_{000},\]

and the excess asymptote

\[\Delta E(n) = \mathrm{asymptote}(n) - \mathrm{asymptote}(0).\]

For each molecular electronic state, we search over all feasible pairs \((n_1, n_2)\) of product-atom electronic states for the combination that minimises

\[\mathrm{err}(n_1, n_2) = \bigl|\,E_{A}(n_1) + E_{B}(n_2) - \Delta E(n)\,\bigr|.\]

The mapping \(n \mapsto (n_1^{\star}, n_2^{\star})\) is built at initialisation, stored in a look-up table, and printed to the terminal together with its residual errors (typically smaller than \(1~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}\)). The match guarantees that the Wigner-Witmer correlation rules are obeyed at the solver level, without any user input.

Recombination from detailed balance

The three-body recombination rate \(\mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B} + \mathrm{M} \to \mathrm{AB} + \mathrm{M}\) is not a free parameter. It is derived from the dissociation rate through the equilibrium constant,

\[k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T) = \frac{k^{D}(T)}{K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)},\]

with

\[K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T) = \frac{(2\pi m_A k_B T)^{3/2}\,(2\pi m_B k_B T)^{3/2}\,h^{3}} {(2\pi m_{\mathrm{AB}} k_B T)^{3/2}}\, \frac{Q_A(T)\,Q_B(T)}{Q_{\mathrm{AB}}(T)}\, \exp\!\left(-\frac{D_0}{k_B T}\right).\]

The partition functions \(Q_i(T)\) are built at initialisation from the rovibronic database, and \(D_0\) is read from the molecule’s database entry. The user supplies neither the recombination rate nor the heat of reaction; both are computed internally. Run-time, a collision between \(\mathrm{A}\) and \(\mathrm{B}\) in the presence of a third body \(\mathrm{M}\) is tested with probability \(P_{\mathrm{rec}} = n_M k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T) / \nu_{\mathrm{coll}}(\mathrm{A},\mathrm{B})\).

Atom-exchange (Zeldovich-type) reactions

For \(\mathrm{AB} + \mathrm{C} \to \mathrm{AC} + \mathrm{B}\), the heat of reaction is computed automatically from the database as

\[\Delta H_{\mathrm{exch}} = D_0(\mathrm{AC}) - D_0(\mathrm{AB}).\]

The QK reaction probability,

\[P_{\mathrm{exch}}^{\mathrm{QK}}(E_c; n, v, J) = \frac{\bigl[1 - E_{\mathrm{act}}^{(n,v,J)}/E_c\bigr]^{3/2 - \omega}} {\displaystyle\sum_{n',v',J'}^{\mathrm{BC}} g(n', v', J')\, \bigl[1 - E(n', v', J')/E_c\bigr]^{3/2 - \omega}},\]

normalises the state-resolved threshold by the Boltzmann-averaged denominator over the product-molecule level space, and fires through the two-stage LB sampling described above. Start-up diagnostics print the computed heat against literature values: \(\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}\) returns \(\Delta H \approx -37\,732~\mathrm{K}\), within 1% of the tabulated value.
