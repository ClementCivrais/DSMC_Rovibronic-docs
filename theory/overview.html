Method Overview

The present section aims to describe the Direct Simulation Monte Carlo (DSMC) method as implemented in this solver, and to motivate the rovibronic extension that constitutes the novelty of the present work.

The DSMC method [Bird1994t] resolves a rarefied or transitional gas flow by following a population of simulator particles, each representing a large number of real molecules. Motion and collisions are decoupled on a sub-mean-collision-time step: particles are first advected ballistically, and then binary collisions are sampled, cell by cell, according to the selected collision model — the Variable Soft Sphere (VSS) model by default. The DSMC method relies on considerations from kinetic theory, imposing that the gas be dilute, i.e. the mean molecular diameter must be significantly smaller than the mean free path and Boltzmann’s molecular chaos assumption must hold.

The novelty of the rovibronic extension lies in the replacement of the usual continuous (or multi-temperature) internal-energy model by explicit quantum-state tracking. Every molecular particle carries three integers:

  • the electronic level index \(n\),

  • the vibrational level index \(v\) (within electronic level \(n\)), and

  • the rotational level index \(J\) (within the \((n, v)\) manifold).

Energies, degeneracies, and transition rates are evaluated from pre-tabulated level tables, with Dunham-fitted coefficients derived from ab-initio potential-energy surfaces where available.

What the rovibronic description buys

The state-resolved description is more expensive than a bulk temperature representation, but delivers three benefits that matter in applications of interest:

  • Correct non-Boltzmann tail populations in strong shocks and rapid expansions, where multi-temperature assumptions fail.

  • Direct coupling between excited-state populations and state-specific dissociation, recombination, and charge-exchange channels [Civrais2024ChemI_t], [Civrais2024ChemII_t].

  • Predictive chemistry without having to fit global rate constants to a particular temperature range, because the rate integrals are constructed directly from the spectroscopic database.

In particular, coupling between the vibrational and electronic modes of molecular species is essential when DSMC is coupled with radiation transport solvers [Civrais2024Electronic_t], or in flows in which state-selective dissociation channels (e.g. from electronically excited O2 in re-entry plasmas) alter the overall chemistry.

Organisation of the present guide

The remainder of the Theory Guide develops, in turn:

  • Rovibronic Level Structure — the spectroscopic database and the construction of the \((n, v, J)\) energy tables;

  • Binary Collisions — the VSS collision model and the no-time-counter (NTC) scheme;

  • Chemistry — dissociation, recombination, exchange, and ionisation, with particular emphasis on the parameter-free computation of heats of reaction from the database;

  • Energy Relaxation — the parallel-marginal cascade that drives the internal modes toward equilibrium, and its validation against the multi-exponential Landau-Teller solution;

  • Macroscopic Properties — the inversion from state-resolved populations to effective temperatures.
