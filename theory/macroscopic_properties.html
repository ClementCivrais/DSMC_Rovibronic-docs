Macroscopic Properties

Volume-averaged fields are accumulated from the simulator particles in each computational cell. Below we summarise how the common diagnostics are defined in the rovibronic setting, and we emphasise the status of the per-mode “temperatures” as effective scalars extracted from population distributions rather than as primary thermodynamic variables.

Number density and species fractions

For a cell of volume \(V_{\mathrm{cell}}\) containing \(N_s\) simulator particles of species \(s\), each representing \(w = \mathtt{nEquivalentParticles}\) real molecules,

\[n_s = \frac{N_s\, w}{V_{\mathrm{cell}}}, \qquad x_s = \frac{n_s}{\displaystyle\sum_{s'} n_{s'}}.\]

Translational temperature

The translational temperature is extracted, as in standard DSMC, from the second moment of the peculiar velocity,

\[\frac{3}{2}\, k_B T_T = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \tfrac{1}{2}\, m_i\,(\mathbf{v}_i - \bar{\mathbf{v}})^{2}.\]

Rotational, vibrational, and electronic temperatures

For each composite internal mode \(k \in \{R,\,V,\,E\}\), we first average the mode-specific energy over the cell, \(\langle E_k\rangle = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i E_k^{(i)}\), and then invert the (pre-tabulated) equilibrium relation \(\langle E_k\rangle(T_k)\) by bisection to obtain the effective temperature \(T_k\). The associated effective DOF is

\[\xi_k(T_k) = \frac{2\,\langle E_k\rangle(T_k)}{k_B\,T_k},\]

which matches the definition used in the Landau-Teller cascade (see Energy Relaxation).

Status of effective temperatures under non-equilibrium

It is important to emphasise that, under strong non-equilibrium — for instance in the early stages of a shock-heated flow, or in a rapidly expanding nozzle — the quantities \(T_R\), \(T_V\), \(T_E\) are cell-level effective temperatures rather than thermodynamic temperatures. They correctly describe the first moment of the population distribution but do not characterise the distribution as a whole, which in general is strongly non-Boltzmann. The physical solution resides in the state-resolved population vector \(P(n, v, J)\) itself, and any downstream coupling that assumes a Boltzmann shape (e.g. coupling with a radiation transport solver) must be revisited accordingly [Civrais2024Electronic_m].

Population diagnostics

Per-cell histograms over \((n, v, J)\) are available from the post-processing utilities under applications/utilities/. Typical use cases include:

  • the vibrational population \(P(v)\) at fixed electronic state \(n\), plotted against the Boltzmann distribution at \(T_T\) (a direct diagnostic of vibrational non-equilibrium);

  • the rotational population \(P(J)\) at fixed \((n, v)\) to assess the assumption of rotational equilibrium;

  • the electronic population \(P(n)\) as a function of time in shock-heated flows, to monitor the rate at which the excited electronic manifolds become populated.

[Civrais2024Electronic_m]

C. H. B. Civrais, M. Pfeiffer, C. White, and R. Steijl, “Modelling of the electronic excited states in high-temperature flows,” Physics of Fluids (2024).
