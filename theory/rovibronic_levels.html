Rovibronic Level Structure

The rovibronic extension replaces the continuous internal energy usually carried on a DSMC particle by an indexed triple \((n, v, J)\) that selects one level out of a three-dimensional table. The present section describes how that table is built, which symbols are involved, and which assumptions underpin each step.

Energy expansion

We expand the total internal energy of a diatomic molecule as

\[E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J) = T_e^{(n)} + E_v^{(n)}(v) + E_r^{(n, v)}(J),\]

where \(T_e^{(n)}\) is the electronic term energy of level \(n\), \(E_v^{(n)}\) is the vibrational contribution in electronic level \(n\), and \(E_r^{(n,v)}\) is the rotational contribution in the \((n, v)\) manifold. The three contributions are modelled as follows.

Vibrational ladder (Morse oscillator)

Following the Morse oscillator description, the vibrational term energy in electronic level \(n\) reads

\[E_v^{(n)}(v) = hc\left[\omega_e^{(n)}\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right) - \omega_e x_e^{(n)}\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{2}\right],\]

with the harmonic frequency \(\omega_e^{(n)}\) and the anharmonicity \(\omega_e x_e^{(n)}\) given in \(\mathrm{cm}^{-1}\). The ladder is truncated at the dissociation limit: the maximum bound vibrational quantum number \(v_{\max}^{(n)}\) is determined as the largest \(v\) for which the fitted series still lies below the dissociation energy \(E_d^{(n)}\) of electronic state \(n\).

Rotational sub-ladder (rigid rotor + distortion)

For each \((n, v)\), the rotational ladder is built from

\[E_r^{(n, v)}(J) = B_v\, J(J+1) - D_v\,[J(J+1)]^{2},\]

with vibration-dependent rotational and centrifugal-distortion constants

\[B_v = B_e - \alpha_e\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right), \qquad D_v = D_e - \beta_e\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right).\]

The maximum rotational quantum number \(J_{\max}^{(n,v)}\) is obtained from a centrifugal-barrier analysis, i.e. as the largest \(J\) for which the effective potential (including the centrifugal contribution) still supports a bound state.

Degeneracy

The statistical weight of a level is

\[g(n, v, J) = (2J+1)\, g_{\mathrm{elec}}^{(n)},\]

where \(g_{\mathrm{elec}}^{(n)}\) accounts for the electronic degeneracy (e.g. 3 for a \({}^{3}\Sigma\) state, 4 for \({}^{2}\Pi\)). For homonuclear molecules the parity constraint of the nuclear spin further suppresses either even- or odd-\(J\) sub-ladders, which is enforced in the sampling routines.

Ground-state normalisation and provenance

It is important to emphasise that every energy in the database is stored relative to the molecular ground state \((n, v, J) = (0, 0, 0)\), i.e.

\[E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J) \leftarrow E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J) - E_{\mathrm{int}}(0, 0, 0),\]

so that the ground state carries zero internal energy by convention. This choice has no physical content but ensures that the dissociation energy \(D_0\) that the chemistry module extracts from the database is the physical bond energy, rather than the fitted potential minimum.

The Dunham coefficients are stored in constant/speciesCache/<species>.dat together with the \((n, v, J)\) level tables and the associated degeneracies. Fit quality and ab-initio provenance are documented in docs/Database/dunham_coefficients_from_ab_initio_PES.md.

Typical state-space sizes

For a diatomic species in air chemistry, the level space is dense. As a representative example, O2 carries six electronic states, of the order of 60 vibrational levels per electronic state, and on average some 15 rotational levels per vibrational level — altogether \(\sim 3 \times 10^{5}\) rovibronic levels. The tables are built once, at solver start-up, and cached on disk so that subsequent runs with the same species list incur no rebuild cost.
