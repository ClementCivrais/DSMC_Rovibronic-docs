Rovibronic Level Structure

The molecular internal energy is expanded as

\[E_{\text{int}}(e, v, j) = E_e(e) + E_v(e, v) + E_r(e, v, j),\]

where \(E_e\) is the electronic term energy, \(E_v\) is the vibrational term in electronic level \(e\), and \(E_r\) is the rotational term in the \((e, v)\) manifold. Each contribution is obtained from a Dunham expansion,

\[E_v(e, v) = \sum_k Y_{k,0}(e)\,(v + 1/2)^k,\]
\[E_r(e, v, j) = \sum_{k,\ell} Y_{k,\ell}(e)\,(v + 1/2)^k\,[j(j+1)]^\ell,\]

fitted to ab-initio potential-energy surfaces. See docs/Database/dunham_coefficients_from_ab_initio_PES.md for the fit procedure and quality metrics.

Level enumeration and cut-offs

  • Dissociation cut-off: maximum bound \(v\) for each electronic level is set where the fitted Dunham series diverges from the physical PES.

  • Rotational cut-off: \(j_{\max}(e, v)\) is enforced from a centrifugal-barrier criterion.

  • Electronic levels: populated up to the highest state included in the species database.

Degeneracies

Nuclear spin, electronic angular momentum, and rotational degeneracy \((2j + 1)\) are carried per level so that equilibrium (Boltzmann) populations are reproduced exactly in the absence of departures from equilibrium.
