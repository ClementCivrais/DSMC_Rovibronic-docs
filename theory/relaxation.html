Energy Relaxation

Internal relaxation drives the rovibronic population of each molecular species toward equilibrium with the local translational temperature. In the present solver this is achieved through a parallel-marginal cascade in which rotational, rovibrational, and rovibronic events are mutually exclusive at the single-collision level, yet reproduce the correct multi-exponential Landau-Teller relaxation of the composite internal energy in the macroscopic limit [CivraisThesis_r].

Landau-Teller rate per mode

Following the Landau-Teller framework, the mean internal energy in mode \(k\) approaches its equilibrium value with time constant \(\tau_k\):

\[\frac{\mathrm{d}\,\langle E_k\rangle}{\mathrm{d}t} = \frac{\langle E_k\rangle(T_T) - \langle E_k\rangle(t)} {Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}\,\tau_c},\]

where \(T_T\) is the translational temperature, \(Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}\) is the continuum collision number for mode \(k\), and \(\tau_c = 1/\nu_{\mathrm{coll}}\) is the mean collision time. Under isothermal conditions the solution is a simple exponential decay; in the multi-mode case, with three composite modes relaxing on well-separated timescales, the approach is the sum of three exponentials

\[\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t) = \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star} - \Delta E_R\, e^{-t/\tau_R} - \Delta E_V\, e^{-t/\tau_{RV}} - \Delta E_E\, e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}}.\]

Equivalently,

\[\frac{\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star} - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t)} {\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star} - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(0)} = A_R e^{-t/\tau_R} + A_V e^{-t/\tau_{RV}} + A_E e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}},\]

with mode amplitudes \(A_k = \Delta E_k / \Delta E_{\mathrm{total}}\) satisfying \(A_R + A_V + A_E = 1\).

Composite modes and effective DOF

We identify three composite modes, each characterised by which quantum numbers are sampled and by the energy floor below which the mode’s contribution vanishes:

  • mode \(R\) (rotational only) — samples \(J\) at fixed \((n, v)\); floor \(E_R^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, v, 0)\);

  • mode \(RV\) (rovibrational) — samples \((v, J)\) at fixed \(n\); floor \(E_{RV}^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, 0, 0)\);

  • mode \(RVE\) (rovibronic) — samples \((n, v, J)\) over the full table; floor \(E_{RVE}^{\mathrm{floor}} = 0\).

The effective degrees of freedom of each composite mode are computed on the fly from the Boltzmann average

\[\xi_k(T) = \frac{2 \langle E_k\rangle(T)}{k_B T},\]

evaluated from the tabulated partition functions.

Parallel-marginal cascade

On each accepted collision we draw a single uniform \(R \sim \mathcal{U}(0, 1)\) and select one of four mutually exclusive events \(\{E_0, E_1, E_2, E_3\}\) with probabilities

\[P_1 = \frac{1}{Z_{r}\,C_{r}}, \quad P_2 = \frac{1}{Z_{rv}\,C_{rv}}, \quad P_3 = \frac{1}{Z_{rve}\,C_{rve}},\]

and \(P_0 = 1 - P_1 - P_2 - P_3\). Events \(E_1,\,E_2,\,E_3\) correspond to rotational, rovibrational, and rovibronic sampling on their respective subspaces, with Larsen-Borgnakke acceptance and the exponent

\[\alpha_k = \tfrac{1}{2}(\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \xi_k^{\mathrm{pooled}}) - 1\]

where \(\xi_k^{\mathrm{pooled}}\) is the effective DOF of the mode plus any lower modes that re-equilibrate at the same event. Event \(E_0\) leaves the internal state unchanged (elastic-like collision from the internal-energy standpoint).

The factors \(C_k\) are the Nizenkov correction factors

\[C_k = \frac{\xi_T\,(T_T - T'_k)} {\xi_k(T_T)\,T_T - \xi_k(T_k)\,T_k},\]

where \(T'_k\) is the pool temperature satisfying local energy conservation. Their role is to recover the target Landau-Teller rate per mode from the cell-level acceptance statistics; without them, the macroscopic relaxation drifts systematically from the analytical LT solution.

When the sum \(P_1 + P_2 + P_3 > 1\), the probabilities are rescaled to saturate the collision; this effectively raises the operational \(Z_k\) seen by the solver, and is flagged in the diagnostic output.

Validation protocol (phase-by-phase)

The cascade is validated in three phases, each isolating one composite mode by sending the collision numbers of the others to infinity (i.e. setting them to very large values in the input dictionary):

  • Phase 1 — rotational only: \(Z_{rv} = Z_{rve} = \infty\); initial condition \(T_T = 10^{4}~\mathrm{K}\), \(T_R(0) = 10^{3}~\mathrm{K}\). Expected: single-exponential decay with time constant \(\tau_R = Z_r^{\mathrm{cont}}\,\tau_c\).

  • Phase 2 — rovibrational: \(Z_r = \infty\), \(Z_{rve} = \infty\). Expected: double-exponential decay with a fast rotational equilibration followed by vibrational relaxation.

  • Phase 3 — all modes active: finite \(Z_r,\,Z_{rv},\,Z_{rve}\). Expected: triple-exponential with the hierarchy \(\tau_R \ll \tau_{RV} \ll \tau_{RVE}\).

Success is declared when the fitted collision number satisfies \(|Z_{k,\mathrm{fit}}/Z_k - 1| < 5\%\). At the time of writing, Phase 1 passes comfortably (\(Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z \approx 1.07\)), Phase 2 overcorrects (\(\approx 0.83\)), and Phase 3 is still being diagnosed.

On the absence of the classic ZCorr

A separate study of the rovibronic Larsen-Borgnakke path showed that the ratio \(Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z_{\mathrm{input}}\) sits at approximately 0.99 without any additional Z-correction factor [Civrais2024Vib_r]. The classic ZCorr used in continuous- internal-energy models is therefore not required on the rovibronic LB path, and its prior inclusion was traced to a compensating bug that has since been fixed; the interested reader will find the full analysis in docs/Rovibronic Article/DSMC_Bugs_Report.pdf.
