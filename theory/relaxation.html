Energy Relaxation

Internal relaxation drives the molecular internal state toward equilibrium with the local translational temperature.

Landau–Teller enforcement

In Landau–Teller mode, relaxation events are sampled with probability

\[P = \frac{\Delta t}{\tau(T)},\]

where \(\tau(T)\) is the species-pair relaxation time (Millikan–White or curve-fitted to state-to-state master-equation solutions). The implementation correctly accounts for:

  • Multi-mode relaxation when multiple internal degrees of freedom are active (see doc/LT_Correction_MultiMode.pdf).

  • Multi-exponential \(\tau(T)\) fits (doc/LandauTeller_MultiExponential_Derivation.pdf).

  • The ZCorr correction where applicable — the \(Z_{\text{fit}}/Z_{\text{input}}\) ratio was measured to \(\approx 0.99\) for rovibronic LB without the correction, confirming that ZCorr is not needed in the rovibronic path.

State-to-state relaxation

When relaxationScheme selects the state-to-state model, transitions are sampled directly from per-pair rate tables rather than through an effective \(\tau\). This is the most expensive but most faithful choice for strong non-equilibrium.

Coupled relaxation matrix

The coupled vibrational–rotational relaxation operator is derived in doc/CoupledRelaxationMatrix_Derivation.pdf.
