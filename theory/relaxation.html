Internal-energy relaxation

We now document the relaxation of the rotational, vibrational, and electronic internal modes of the molecular species toward equilibrium with the translational mode. The present section derives the Landau-Teller rate equation from first principles, describes the Millikan-White and Parker empirical correlations from which the relaxation collision numbers are read, introduces the Nizenkov correction that guarantees rate-matching in a multi-mode cascade, and documents the parallel-marginal cascade by which the solver turns the analytic rate equations into an event-by-event sampler on the discrete rovibronic state space.

Landau-Teller rate equation

Under the assumption of an exponential decay of correlations, the mean energy \(\langle E_k\rangle\) of internal mode \(k\) approaches its equilibrium value \(\langle E_k\rangle(T_T)\) at the translational temperature \(T_T\) according to

\[\frac{\mathrm{d}\,\langle E_k\rangle}{\mathrm{d}t} = \frac{\langle E_k\rangle(T_T) - \langle E_k\rangle(t)} {\tau_k},\]

where \(\tau_k\) is the relaxation time of mode \(k\). The Landau-Teller form

\[\tau_k = Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}\,\tau_c,\]

with \(\tau_c = 1/\nu_{\mathrm{coll}}\) the mean collision time, relates the continuum-level relaxation time to a collision number \(Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}\), i.e. the mean number of collisions required to equilibrate the mode. Under isothermal heat-bath conditions the equation admits the explicit solution

\[\langle E_k\rangle(t) = \langle E_k\rangle(T_T) - \bigl[\langle E_k\rangle(T_T) - \langle E_k\rangle(0)\bigr]\, \exp\!\left(-\frac{t}{\tau_k}\right),\]

which is the reference curve against which the solver is validated (Landau-Teller heat-bath validation).

Multi-mode decomposition

When several internal modes relax on well-separated timescales \(\tau_R \ll \tau_{RV} \ll \tau_{RVE}\), the total internal energy approaches equilibrium as a sum of exponentials:

\[\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t) = \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star} - \Delta E_R\, e^{-t/\tau_R} - \Delta E_V\, e^{-t/\tau_{RV}} - \Delta E_E\, e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}},\]

where \(\Delta E_k\) is the initial energy deficit of mode \(k\). In normalised form,

\[\frac{\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star} - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t)} {\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star} - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(0)} = A_R\, e^{-t/\tau_R} + A_V\, e^{-t/\tau_{RV}} + A_E\, e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}},\]

with \(A_k = \Delta E_k / \Delta E_{\mathrm{tot}}\) and \(A_R + A_V + A_E = 1\).

Millikan-White and Parker correlations

The rotational collision number \(Z_r(T)\) is fitted, in the manner of Parker [Parker1959rel], to measured rotational- translational thermalisation data:

\[Z_r(T) = \frac{Z_r^{\infty}}{1 + (\pi^{3/2}/2)\,\sqrt{\theta_r^{\star}/T} + (\pi + \pi^{2}/4)\,(\theta_r^{\star}/T)},\]

with \(\theta_r^{\star}\) a reference rotational temperature and \(Z_r^{\infty}\) an asymptotic value that is typically fitted to 5–10 for air diatomics. In the DSMC implementation the low-temperature asymptote is tabulated directly as rotationalRelaxationCollisionNumber in the <Model>Coeffs sub-dictionary.

The vibrational relaxation time follows the Millikan-White correlation [MillikanWhite1963rel]:

\[p\,\tau_v^{\mathrm{MW}}(T) = \exp\!\left[A\,\bigl(T^{-1/3} - B\bigr) - 18.42\right], \qquad \text{[atm}\cdot\text{s]},\]

with \(A = 1.16 \times 10^{-3}\,\mu^{1/2}\,\theta_v^{4/3}\) and \(B = 0.015\,\mu^{1/4}\), the reduced mass \(\mu\) in atomic units, and the characteristic vibrational temperature \(\theta_v\) in kelvin. Park’s high-temperature correction, \(\tau_v^{\mathrm{Park}} = \tau_v^{\mathrm{MW}} + 1/(\sigma_v\,c_s\,n_s)\), brings the relaxation rate under control above \(10^{4}~\mathrm{K}\), where the Millikan-White exponential extrapolation would otherwise produce unphysically fast relaxation. In the DSMC implementation, the effective vibrational collision number rovibrationalRelaxationCollisionNumber absorbs the combined Millikan-White/Park relation, and the database-derived \(\tau_v(T)\) is checked against the input at solver initialisation.

Electronic relaxation times are less well characterised experimentally than rotational or vibrational ones, and the effective rovibronicRelaxationCollisionNumber in <Model>Coeffs is conventionally set to a large value (\(10^{3}\text{–}10^{4}\)) that reflects the slow equilibration typical of atomic emission observations. Setting it to \(\infty\) amounts to freezing the electronic populations — useful diagnostically, but physically justified only in short transient cases.

Composite modes in the parallel-marginal cascade

Rather than redistributing energy independently between each internal mode and the translational pool, the solver groups the relaxation events into three composite modes organised by the sub-space of the rovibronic state that they sample:

  • mode \(R\) (rotational-only) samples \(J\) at fixed \((n, v)\); the mode energy is measured relative to the floor \(E_R^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, v, 0)\);

  • mode \(RV\) (rovibrational) samples \((v, J)\) at fixed \(n\); floor \(E_{RV}^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, 0, 0)\);

  • mode \(RVE\) (rovibronic) samples \((n, v, J)\) on the full table; floor \(E_{RVE}^{\mathrm{floor}} = 0\).

Each composite mode carries its own effective degrees of freedom, computed from the sub-space partition function,

\[\xi_k(T) = \frac{2\,\langle E_k\rangle(T)}{k_B\,T},\]

and these \(\xi_k(T)\) feed the Larsen-Borgnakke exponent (Larsen-Borgnakke energy redistribution).

Event selection

On each accepted collision the solver draws a single uniform \(R \sim \mathcal{U}(0, 1)\) and selects one of four mutually exclusive events:

\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} P_1 &= \frac{1}{Z_r\,C_r}, \\ P_2 &= \frac{1}{Z_{rv}\,C_{rv}}, \\ P_3 &= \frac{1}{Z_{rve}\,C_{rve}}, \\ P_0 &= 1 - P_1 - P_2 - P_3, \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

where event \(E_k\) (for \(k = 1, 2, 3\)) triggers Larsen-Borgnakke sampling in the corresponding composite mode, and \(E_0\) leaves the internal state unchanged (elastic-like event). When \(P_1 + P_2 + P_3 > 1\) the probabilities are rescaled to saturate, and a warning is emitted in the solver log: the effective collision numbers seen by the simulation then differ from the input values.

Nizenkov correction

The collision numbers \(Z_k\) are defined at the continuum level, while the cascade acts at the event level. The two are related by a temperature-dependent correction factor derived by Nizenkov [Pfeiffer2016rel]:

\[C_k = \frac{\xi_T\,(T_T - T'_k)} {\xi_k(T_T)\,T_T - \xi_k(T_k)\,T_k},\]

where \(\xi_T\) is the translational DOF entering the exchange, and \(T'_k\) is the pool temperature that satisfies local energy conservation in the cascade event. Without the \(C_k\) factors, the macroscopic relaxation observed by the solver drifts systematically from the analytical Landau-Teller solution; with them in place, the ratio \(Z_{k,\mathrm{fit}}/Z_k\) recovers the input to within a few percent on the validation cases. A derivation of \(C_k\) is given in doc/LT_Correction_MultiMode.pdf.

Phase-by-phase validation protocol

The cascade is validated phase by phase by sending the collision numbers of all but one mode to a very large value, \(Z_j \to 10^{9}\) for \(j \ne k\), and comparing the observed relaxation curve against the analytical single-mode LT solution.

  • Phase 1 — rotational only: freeze \(Z_{rv}, Z_{rve}\). Initial state \(T_T = 10^{4}~\mathrm{K}\), \(T_R(0) = 10^{3}~\mathrm{K}\). The fitted \(Z_r\) should match the input within 5%. At the time of writing the ratio is \(Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z \approx 1.07\), acceptable.

  • Phase 2 — rovibrational: freeze \(Z_r, Z_{rve}\). Expected: rotational sub-ladder equilibrates near instantaneously (because rotational events are frozen, the cascade has only one active mode), followed by vibrational relaxation. Current ratio \(\approx 0.83\); the phase overcorrects and is under investigation.

  • Phase 3 — all modes active. Expected triple-exponential decay; ratio pending diagnosis.

On the absence of the classic ZCorr

The continuous-variable implementations of Larsen-Borgnakke require the so-called ZCorr correction to match the macroscopic Landau-Teller rate (since, in the continuous formulation, the effective DOF enter the exponent while the sampled population is unphysically concentrated near the mean). In the rovibronic extension the sampled population is the actual quantum distribution, and a direct measurement [Civrais2024Vib_rel] of \(Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z_{\mathrm{input}}\) returns \(\approx 0.99\) without any correction factor, confirming that ZCorr is not required on the rovibronic Larsen-Borgnakke path. The relevant diagnostic history is tracked in the in-repo bug-audit report.
