Collision sampling

Given a chosen cross-section model, the second half of the DSMC collision step is to decide, in each cell, how many candidate pairs to draw and which of them to accept. Four sampling schemes are implemented in the solver. They are mathematically equivalent in the limit of infinite particle count but differ in statistical efficiency and in the way they handle collision-rate disparities across species pairs. The scheme is selected via the collisionPartnerSelectionModel key in constant/dsmcProperties.

No-Time-Counter (NTC)

The No-Time-Counter scheme, introduced by Bird, is the default. The per-cell candidate count over \(\Delta t\) is

\[N_{\mathrm{cand}} = \frac{1}{2}\, \frac{N(N - 1)\, F_N\, (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\,\Delta t} {V_c},\]

where \(N\) is the number of particles in the cell, \(F_N\) is the equivalent-molecules factor, \(V_c\) is the cell volume, and \((\sigma_T g)_{\max}\) is a running estimate of the maximum of \(\sigma_T(g_{\mathrm{ref}})\, g\). Each candidate pair \((P, Q)\) is accepted with probability

\[P_{\mathrm{acc}} = \frac{\sigma_T(g)\, g}{(\sigma_T g)_{\max}},\]

which, combined with the candidate-generation formula above, recovers the target collision rate

\[\nu_{\mathrm{coll}} = \frac{N(N - 1)\, F_N\,\langle\sigma_T g\rangle} {2\,V_c}\]

on expectation, with \(\langle \cdot \rangle\) the ensemble average over the cell distribution. The running maximum \((\sigma_T g)_{\max}\) is monotonically updated whenever a candidate exceeds the current estimate, and is therefore self-correcting.

Validity. NTC is unbiased in the limit of infinite particle count per cell and in the limit of infinite time-steps; the leading correction is \(O(1/N)\) and is addressed by keeping \(N \gtrsim 10\text{–}20\) per cell. Its main limitation is that the acceptance probability \(\sigma_T g / (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\) becomes small whenever the cross-section distribution across species pairs spans several decades, which the alternatives below are designed to address.

No-Time-Counter with sub-cycling

When charged species are present, electron-electron and electron- neutral collision rates routinely exceed heavy-species collision rates by several orders of magnitude. A single DSMC time-step is then either too coarse to resolve electron kinetics or too fine to be practical for the rest of the flow. The noTimeCounterSubCycled variant performs \(n_{\mathrm{sc}}\) internal NTC sub-cycles on the electron population within each global DSMC step:

\[\Delta t_{\mathrm{sc}} = \frac{\Delta t}{n_{\mathrm{sc}}}, \qquad N_{\mathrm{cand}}^{\mathrm{sc}} = \frac{1}{2}\, \frac{N_e(N_e - 1)\, F_N\, (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\, \Delta t_{\mathrm{sc}}}{V_c} \times n_{\mathrm{sc}},\]

so that the total number of sampled electron collisions remains unchanged but they are now distributed over \(n_{\mathrm{sc}}\) smaller steps. The key nSubCycles in the noTimeCounterSubCycledProperties sub-dictionary sets \(n_{\mathrm{sc}}\).

Majorant Collision Frequency (MCF)

The majorant collision frequency method addresses the acceptance- rate inefficiency of NTC by replacing the global \((\sigma_T g)_{\max}\) bound by per-pair majorants. For each candidate pair, an analytical upper bound \(\hat{\sigma}_T(g)\) is available that is tighter than the global maximum; the acceptance probability then becomes

\[P_{\mathrm{acc}}^{\mathrm{MCF}} = \frac{\sigma_T(g)\, g}{\hat{\sigma}_T(g_{\max})\, g_{\max}}.\]

The method is particularly effective in multi-species flows in which a handful of pairs dominate the collision rate. The implementation follows the particle-based formulation of Pfeiffer et al. [Pfeiffer2019cs].

Simplified Bernoulli Trials (SBT)

The SBT scheme of Stefanov replaces the NTC acceptance step by a sequence of Bernoulli trials with per-pair probability

\[P_{\mathrm{coll}}^{\mathrm{SBT}} = \frac{\sigma_T(g)\, g\, F_N\,\Delta t}{V_c / (N - 1)}.\]

Each particle is tested exactly once against a randomly chosen partner, and the global estimate \((\sigma_T g)_{\max}\) is not needed. SBT is closer in spirit to the original Nanbu algorithm and is occasionally preferable in low-particle-per-cell regimes, at the cost of a slightly different statistical bias profile at small \(N\).

Sub-cell resolution of collision partners

Whichever sampling scheme is chosen, accepted pairs may be restricted to particles within the same sub-cell of the parent cell when the adaptation machinery (Adaptation and controllers) is enabled. This is the solver’s principal mechanism for tightening the \(\Delta x < \lambda/3\) condition in regions of locally steep gradients, and it is applied transparently to the scheme above: the partner pool in the formulae is reduced to the sub-cell inventory, while the sampling logic is otherwise unchanged.

Choosing between schemes

In practice:

  • use noTimeCounter for general-purpose DSMC work; it has been exercised on every validation case in the project;

  • use noTimeCounterSubCycled when chargedParticles is enabled and electron-electron or electron-neutral collision rates are expected to dominate the global DSMC time-step;

  • consider majorantCollisionFrequency when the cross-section distribution across species pairs spans several decades;

  • simplifiedBernoulliTrials is primarily of pedagogical interest in this implementation and is not recommended as a default.
