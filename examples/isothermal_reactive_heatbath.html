Isothermal reactive heat-bath

Directory: tutorials/isothermalReactiveHeatbath/.

A zero-dimensional isothermal heat-bath with dissociation, exchange, and recombination all active (neutral chemistry only). The conditions are chosen to be representative of a post-normal-shock re-entry layer: \(T = 10\,000~\mathrm{K}\) and \(p = 100~\mathrm{Pa}\), with an initial air-like mixture dominated by N2 / O2 and a small NO seed.

The diagnostic of interest is the time history of the species populations: dissociation progressively depletes the diatomic species and enriches the atom pool, until chemical equilibrium is reached when the forward dissociation and the detailed-balance recombination rates match.

Theoretical basis

The case exercises four reactive channels in parallel, each derived in Neutral chemistry:

  • Dissociation via the Quantum-Kinetic (QK) model. For a reactant molecule in rovibronic state \((n, v, J)\), the state-dependent dissociation threshold is \(E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)} = D_0 - E(n, v, J)\), so that excited states contribute disproportionately to the dissociation rate. The reaction probability at collision energy \(E_c\) is \(P^{\mathrm{QK}} = (1 - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)} / E_c)^{3/2 - \omega}\) above threshold; see the full derivation in Neutral chemistry.

  • Post-reaction Larsen-Borgnakke redistribution of the available energy \(E_{\mathrm{avail}} = E_c - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}\) between the products’ internal and translational modes, following Larsen-Borgnakke energy redistribution. Because the sampler acts on the discrete \((n, v, J)\) state space, detailed balance is satisfied by construction.

  • Zeldovich exchange \(\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}\) with the heat of reaction \(\Delta H_{\mathrm{exch}} = D_0(\mathrm{NO}) - D_0(\mathrm{N}_2)\) computed automatically from the database ground-state dissociation energies (no user input required).

  • Three-body recombination from detailed balance \(\mathrm{A} + \mathrm{A} + \mathrm{M} \to \mathrm{A}_2 + \mathrm{M}\), with rate \(k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T) = k^{D}(T) / K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)\) where \(K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)\) is built from the pre-tabulated rovibronic partition functions of Rovibronic level structure. Again, no rate parameter is supplied by the user; the recombination rate is entirely database-driven.

The implementation of each reaction class is catalogued in Reaction classes; the species-level data (ground-state \(D_0\), level tables, partition functions) is read from dsmcSpeciesDatabase — spectroscopic & VSS data.

Validation goals

The case targets three quantitative checks:

  1. Element conservation. The total number density of nitrogen atoms (\(2\,n_{\mathrm{N}_2} + n_\mathrm{N} + n_\mathrm{NO}\)) and of oxygen atoms (\(2\,n_{\mathrm{O}_2} + n_\mathrm{O} + n_\mathrm{NO}\)) must each be conserved to within statistical noise. A drift in either sum indicates a stoichiometric bug in one of the registered reactions and is the quickest useful test of any change to the chemistry module.

  2. Database-consistent heats of reaction. At start-up the solver prints the computed \(\Delta H = D_0(\mathrm{products}) - D_0(\mathrm{reactants})\) for the exchange and recombination channels. For \(\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}\), the expected value is \(\approx -37\,700~\mathrm{K}\) and must agree with the literature (cf. Validation, Heats of reaction) to within 1 %.

  3. Equilibrium composition. Running the case for long enough to reach chemical equilibrium, the product ratio \(n_\mathrm{A}^{2} / n_{\mathrm{A}_2}\) must match \(K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)/n_\mathrm{M}\) evaluated from the same rovibronic database; this is the end-to-end check of the detailed-balance construction of the recombination rate.

Reactions included

Five reactions are registered in system/chemReactDict:

  • O2 + O2 → O + O + O2 (dissociation, same species);

  • N2 + N2 → N + N + N2 (dissociation, same species);

  • N2 + O → NO + N (Zeldovich exchange, with \(\Delta H\) from the database);

  • O + O + M → O2 + M (three-body recombination from detailed balance, \(M \in \{\)O2, O, N2, N, NO\(\}\));

  • N + N + M → N2 + M (three-body recombination, same third bodies).

Case parameters

  • Species: N2, O2, NO, N, O.

  • Number densities at \(t = 0\) (air-like): \(n_{\mathrm{N}_2} = 5.65 \times 10^{19}\;\mathrm{m}^{-3}\), \(n_{\mathrm{O}_2} = 1.52 \times 10^{19}\;\mathrm{m}^{-3}\), \(n_{\mathrm{NO}} = 7.24 \times 10^{17}\;\mathrm{m}^{-3}\).

  • Translational and internal temperatures: both at \(10\,000~\mathrm{K}\) (internal / translational equilibrium at \(t = 0\)).

  • Mesh: \(1 \times 1 \times 1\) cell, fully periodic.

  • Time-step: \(5 \times 10^{-11}~\mathrm{s}\), total integration \(10^{-4}~\mathrm{s}\).

Running the case

cd tutorials/isothermalReactiveHeatbath
blockMesh
dsmcInitialise
dsmcFoam

Expected outcome

At the specified conditions, the diatomic populations decay by roughly an order of magnitude over the simulation time as dissociation dominates; the atomic O and N populations climb correspondingly, and NO equilibrates through the Zeldovich exchange channel. Running the case for long enough brings the system to an equilibrium set by \(k^{\mathrm{diss}}(T) / k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T)\), which the calculator at Mean free path calculator uses (implicitly, via the equilibrium constant) to estimate the equilibrium composition at any \((T, p)\).
