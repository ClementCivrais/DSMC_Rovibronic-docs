Validation
==========

We collect in the present page the canonical checks that are
exercised whenever the rovibronic machinery is modified. The scope
is deliberately lightweight —  the goal is to assemble a coherent
baseline against which regressions can be detected (see the
planned :doc:`../guide/regression_tests`), rather than to
revisit every published validation case.

Heat-bath relaxation (Landau-Teller)
------------------------------------

The four-phase isothermal heat-bath protocol is the primary
validation of the parallel-marginal relaxation cascade with the
Nizenkov Z-correction. For each phase only one channel is active
(the other relaxation collision numbers are set to :math:`10^{6}`),
so the DSMC relaxation of the active mode can be compared directly
against the analytical Landau-Teller (LT) curve — no fit is used;
the reference equilibrium :math:`E_{\mathrm{eq}}` is taken from the
rovibronic database restricted to the state space actually reachable
by the sampler.

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 10 30 25 20 15

   * - Phase
     - Active channel
     - Build flag
     - :math:`E_{\mathrm{eq}}`
     - DSMC vs LT
   * - Phase 1
     - rotational :math:`(Z_r = 5)`
     - ``allowSpinForbidden=false``
     - J-Boltzmann per :math:`(n_p, v_p)`
     - On curve
   * - Phase 2
     - rovibrational :math:`(Z_{rv} = 50)`
     - ``allowSpinForbidden=false``
     - :math:`(v, J)` at :math:`n_p`
     - On curve
   * - Phase 3
     - rovibronic :math:`(Z_{rve} = 500)` spin-conserving
     - ``allowSpinForbidden=false``
     - Triplet-only Boltzmann
     - On curve \*
   * - Phase 4
     - rovibronic :math:`(Z_{rve} = 500)` full manifold
     - ``allowSpinForbidden=true``
     - Full :math:`(n, v, J)`
     - On curve

\* Phase 3 shows a small mid-decay residual relative to the single-
:math:`\tau` LT curve; this is the bi-exponential physics signature
of spin-conserving relaxation — fast :math:`(v,J)` equilibration
inside :math:`n=0` followed by slow access to the A'³Δ\ :sub:`u`
and A³Σ\ :sub:`u`:sup:`+` triplet states at
:math:`\sim\!50\,000`\ K. Phase 4 does not show this because the
low-lying singlets a¹Δ\ :sub:`g` (∼11\,400\ K) and
b¹Σ\ :sub:`g`:sup:`+` (∼19\,000\ K) equilibrate on the same
timescale as :math:`(v,J)` inside :math:`n=0`.

**Key ingredients** that make the validation succeed:

* **Window-select dispatch** in
  ``dsmcCloud::postCollisionInternalEnergy`` replaces the earlier
  nested conditional cascade. A single uniform :math:`R` selects
  exactly one of :math:`\{E_0,\,E_1,\,E_2,\,E_3\}` with marginal
  probabilities :math:`P_k = 1/(Z_k \cdot C_k)`. This is what
  enables clean phase-by-phase isolation.
* **State-consistent pre-tabulation** in
  ``dsmcCloud::buildZCorrTables``:

  - ``meanRotEnergyTable_[tid][n][v][iT]`` — J-Boltzmann per
    :math:`(n,v)` ladder;
  - ``meanRovibEnergyTable_[tid][n][iT]`` — :math:`(v,J)`-Boltzmann
    at each electronic state;
  - ``meanIntEnergyTable_[tid][\mathrm{spinIdx}][iT]`` —
    :math:`(n,v,J)`-Boltzmann restricted to each spin class of
    the species.

  Each parcel looks up its own :math:`(n_p, v_p)` / :math:`n_p` /
  spin class in the relevant table — the DOF used in the LB
  exponent is therefore consistent with the sub-manifold being
  sampled.
* **Species-general cell reference** in
  ``dsmcCloud::updateZCorrections``: cell-level
  :math:`\langle E_k\rangle(T)` is a parcel-count-weighted mixture
  over every molecular species in the cell; no "first molecular
  species" shortcut.

The derivation of :math:`C_k` per composite mode and the protocol
itself are documented in ``doc/LT_Correction_MultiMode.pdf`` in
the source tree.

Two-temperature recombination and three-body channel
----------------------------------------------------

The 2-T recombination path, together with the three-body
``recombinePQ`` channel, has been exercised in zero-dimensional
and one-dimensional canonical cases. Two historical failure modes
have been isolated and fixed in the course of validation:

* a Larsen-Borgnakke NaN, traced to unbounded inelastic energy
  sampling under a specific configuration of reaction exponents;
* a dissociation double-count bug associated with the wrong
  branch of the collision index in the early charge-exchange
  implementation.

The full list of bug-audit findings, together with the
corresponding fixes and short justifications, is collected in
``docs/Rovibronic Article/DSMC_Bugs_Report.pdf`` in the source
tree.

Heats of reaction
-----------------

Startup diagnostics print the automatically-computed heats of
reaction :math:`\Delta H = D_0(\mathrm{products}) -
D_0(\mathrm{reactants})` for each registered exchange and
recombination channel. Representative values for air chemistry
agree with the literature to within 1%:

* :math:`\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}`:
  :math:`\Delta H \approx -37\,700~\mathrm{K}` (lit.) vs.
  :math:`-37\,732~\mathrm{K}` (database).
* :math:`\mathrm{O}_2 + \mathrm{N} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{O}`:
  :math:`\Delta H \approx +16\,000~\mathrm{K}` (lit.) vs.
  :math:`+16\,150~\mathrm{K}` (database).

Shock profiles
--------------

One-dimensional normal-shock profile benchmarks against the
Park and Macheret reference data, as published in
[Civrais2024Shock_v]_, are tracked as separate cases under
``run/``. Re-running these after a substantive change to the
collision or chemistry modules is the recommended next-level
validation beyond the heat-bath test.
