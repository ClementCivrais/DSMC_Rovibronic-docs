About
=====

Scope
-----

DSMC Rovibronic is a custom research version of **dsmcFoam+**, itself
a modification of the legacy OpenFOAM Direct Simulation Monte Carlo
(DSMC) solver ``dsmcFoam``. The rovibronic extension introduces
state-to-state tracking of the electronic, vibrational, and
rotational quantum numbers on each simulator particle, and is aimed
at hypersonic non-equilibrium flows in which a multi-temperature
description is inadequate, and at regimes in which DSMC is coupled
with radiation transport solvers.

Lineage
-------

The solver inherits a three-generation lineage:

* ``dsmcFoam`` — the legacy OpenFOAM DSMC solver, developed within
  the OpenFOAM Foundation.
* ``dsmcFoam+`` — a re-engineered and extended DSMC solver
  introduced by C. White and co-workers at the University of
  Glasgow [White2018_a]_, which has since served as the basis for
  a range of hypersonic non-equilibrium studies.
* **DSMC Rovibronic** (the present solver) — a custom rovibronic
  extension developed on top of dsmcFoam+, adding state-resolved
  :math:`(n, v, J)` internal-energy tracking, automatic database-
  driven heats of reaction, and a multi-mode Landau-Teller
  cascade.

Target applications
-------------------

* DSMC in the rarefied and transitional regimes, with an emphasis
  on hypersonic re-entry and high-altitude flight.
* Diatomic species of air chemistry —  N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`,
  NO, CO, CO\ :sub:`2` —  together with their dissociation,
  exchange, and ionisation products.
* Shock-layer chemistry with explicit electronic-state populations,
  as required by coupling with radiation transport solvers.

Development team
----------------

The current development team is:

* **C. White** (Principal Investigator) — University of Glasgow,
  James Watt School of Engineering. Original developer of
  dsmcFoam+ [White2018_a]_, thesis adviser for the rovibronic
  extension.
* **C. H. B. Civrais** (Lead developer) — University of California,
  Irvine. Developer of the rovibronic extension, author of the
  associated peer-reviewed publications
  [Civrais2024Electronic_a]_, [Civrais2024ChemI_a]_,
  [Civrais2024ChemII_a]_, [Civrais2024Vib_a]_, and of the underlying
  doctoral thesis [CivraisThesis_a]_.

Licence and access
------------------

The source repository is private; licensing is handled on a
per-collaboration basis. The documentation site, on the other hand,
is public, and is intended to support collaborators, reviewers, and
prospective users seeking an overview of the method. Please
contact the development team for access to the source.
