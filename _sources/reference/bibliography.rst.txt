Bibliography
============

The present section collects the principal references cited in the
documentation, organised in three groups: foundational DSMC
literature, the dsmcFoam+ lineage, and the peer-reviewed
publications describing the rovibronic extension of the present
solver. Copies of most of the external references are distributed
under ``docs/References/`` in the source tree.

Foundational DSMC literature
----------------------------

* G. A. Bird, *Molecular Gas Dynamics and the Direct Simulation of
  Gas Flows*, Oxford University Press (1994).
* C. Borgnakke and P. S. Larsen, "Statistical collision model for
  Monte Carlo simulation of polyatomic gas mixture," *Journal of
  Computational Physics*, **18** (4), 405–420 (1975).
* I. D. Boyd, "Rotational and vibrational non-equilibrium effects in
  rarefied hypersonic flow," *Journal of Thermophysics and Heat
  Transfer*, **4** (4), 478–484 (1990).
* I. D. Boyd, "Modeling backward chemical rate processes in the
  direct simulation Monte Carlo method," *Physics of Fluids*,
  **19**, 126110 (2007).
* R. C. Millikan and D. R. White, "Systematics of vibrational
  relaxation," *Journal of Chemical Physics*, **39** (12),
  3209–3213 (1963).
* M. Pfeiffer and P. Nizenkov, "Extension of particle-based BGK
  models to polyatomic species," *Physics of Fluids*, **28**,
  027103 (2016).

The dsmcFoam+ lineage
---------------------

* C. White, M. K. Borg, T. J. Scanlon, S. M. Longshaw,
  R. C. Palharini, D. R. Emerson, and J. M. Reese,
  "dsmcFoam+: An OpenFOAM based direct simulation Monte Carlo
  solver," *Computer Physics Communications*, **224**, 22–43
  (2018).
* C. White, *Benchmarking, Development and Applications of an Open
  Source DSMC Solver*, PhD thesis, University of Glasgow.
  Copy: ``docs/References/White C. - (Thesis) - Benchmarking,
  Development and Applications of an Open Source DSMC Solver.pdf``.
