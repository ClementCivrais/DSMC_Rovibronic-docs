Orion capsule at 107 km (non-reacting)
======================================

Directory: ``tutorials/orion107kmNonReactingFlow/``.

Flow over the Orion crew capsule forebody at 107 km altitude
during re-entry, with chemistry disabled. The case is
representative of the upper-atmosphere portion of a lunar-return
trajectory, where the Knudsen number is large enough that DSMC
is essential (a continuum description fails) and the number
density is small enough that ionisation is not yet significant.

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2` and O\ :sub:`2`.
* Altitude: 107 km — representative free-stream density
  :math:`\sim 10^{16}\;\mathrm{m}^{-3}`; use the
  :doc:`../tools/mean_free_path` calculator to confirm the mean
  free path and Knudsen number at the case's reference length.
* Triangulated capsule geometry: ``constant/triSurface/``.
* Free-stream velocity and temperature in
  ``system/boundariesDict``.
* Walls: diffuse, at the specified :math:`T_w`.

Diagnostics
-----------

* Volume fields (density, translational and internal
  temperatures, stress tensor, heat flux, Mach number) via
  ``dsmcVolFields``.
* Wall heat flux and friction drag via
  ``boundaryMeasurements``; the integrated drag over the
  forebody is directly comparable to the Apollo Command Module
  and Orion flight data referenced in the literature.

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/orion107kmNonReactingFlow
   # Mesh generation — use the usual OpenFOAM pipeline appropriate
   # for the triangulated surface:
   blockMesh
   surfaceFeatureExtract   # if used
   snappyHexMesh -overwrite
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam
