Isothermal reactive heat-bath
=============================

Directory: ``tutorials/isothermalReactiveHeatbath/``.

A zero-dimensional isothermal heat-bath with dissociation,
exchange, and recombination **all active** (neutral chemistry
only). The conditions are chosen to be representative of a
post-normal-shock re-entry layer: :math:`T = 10\,000~\mathrm{K}`
and :math:`p = 100~\mathrm{Pa}`, with an initial air-like mixture
dominated by N\ :sub:`2` / O\ :sub:`2` and a small NO seed.

The diagnostic of interest is the time history of the species
populations: dissociation progressively depletes the diatomic
species and enriches the atom pool, until chemical equilibrium is
reached when the forward dissociation and the detailed-balance
recombination rates match.

Theoretical basis
-----------------

The case exercises four reactive channels in parallel, each
derived in :doc:`../theory/chemistry`:

* **Dissociation via the Quantum-Kinetic (QK) model.** For a
  reactant molecule in rovibronic state :math:`(n, v, J)`, the
  state-dependent dissociation threshold is
  :math:`E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)} = D_0 - E(n, v, J)`, so
  that excited states contribute disproportionately to the
  dissociation rate. The reaction probability at collision
  energy :math:`E_c` is
  :math:`P^{\mathrm{QK}} = (1 - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}
  / E_c)^{3/2 - \omega}` above threshold; see the full
  derivation in :doc:`../theory/chemistry`.
* **Post-reaction Larsen-Borgnakke redistribution** of the
  available energy :math:`E_{\mathrm{avail}} = E_c -
  E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}` between the products'
  internal and translational modes, following
  :doc:`../theory/larsen_borgnakke`. Because the sampler acts on
  the discrete :math:`(n, v, J)` state space, detailed balance
  is satisfied by construction.
* **Zeldovich exchange**
  :math:`\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}`
  with the heat of reaction
  :math:`\Delta H_{\mathrm{exch}} = D_0(\mathrm{NO}) -
  D_0(\mathrm{N}_2)` **computed automatically** from the
  database ground-state dissociation energies (no user input
  required).
* **Three-body recombination from detailed balance**
  :math:`\mathrm{A} + \mathrm{A} + \mathrm{M} \to \mathrm{A}_2
  + \mathrm{M}`, with rate
  :math:`k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T) = k^{D}(T) / K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)`
  where :math:`K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)` is built from the pre-tabulated
  rovibronic partition functions of
  :doc:`../theory/rovibronic_levels`. Again, no rate parameter
  is supplied by the user; the recombination rate is entirely
  database-driven.

The implementation of each reaction class is catalogued in
:doc:`../classes/reactions`; the species-level data (ground-state
:math:`D_0`, level tables, partition functions) is read from
:doc:`../classes/species_database`.

Validation goals
----------------

The case targets three quantitative checks:

#. **Element conservation.** The total number density of
   nitrogen atoms (:math:`2\,n_{\mathrm{N}_2} + n_\mathrm{N}
   + n_\mathrm{NO}`) and of oxygen atoms
   (:math:`2\,n_{\mathrm{O}_2} + n_\mathrm{O} + n_\mathrm{NO}`)
   must each be conserved to within statistical noise. A drift
   in either sum indicates a stoichiometric bug in one of the
   registered reactions and is the quickest useful test of any
   change to the chemistry module.
#. **Database-consistent heats of reaction.** At start-up the
   solver prints the computed
   :math:`\Delta H = D_0(\mathrm{products}) - D_0(\mathrm{reactants})`
   for the exchange and recombination channels. For
   :math:`\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}`,
   the expected value is :math:`\approx -37\,700~\mathrm{K}` and
   must agree with the literature (cf.
   :doc:`../reference/validation`, *Heats of reaction*) to
   within 1 %.
#. **Equilibrium composition.** Running the case for long enough
   to reach chemical equilibrium, the product ratio
   :math:`n_\mathrm{A}^{2} / n_{\mathrm{A}_2}` must match
   :math:`K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)/n_\mathrm{M}` evaluated from the
   same rovibronic database; this is the end-to-end check of
   the detailed-balance construction of the recombination rate.

Reactions included
------------------

Five reactions are registered in ``system/chemReactDict``:

* ``O2 + O2 → O + O + O2`` (dissociation, same species);
* ``N2 + N2 → N + N + N2`` (dissociation, same species);
* ``N2 + O → NO + N`` (Zeldovich exchange, with
  :math:`\Delta H` from the database);
* ``O + O + M → O2 + M`` (three-body recombination from detailed
  balance, :math:`M \in \{`\ O\ :sub:`2`\ , O, N\ :sub:`2`\ , N,
  NO\ :math:`\}`);
* ``N + N + M → N2 + M`` (three-body recombination, same third
  bodies).

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`, NO, N, O.
* Number densities at :math:`t = 0` (air-like):
  :math:`n_{\mathrm{N}_2} = 5.65 \times 10^{19}\;\mathrm{m}^{-3}`,
  :math:`n_{\mathrm{O}_2} = 1.52 \times 10^{19}\;\mathrm{m}^{-3}`,
  :math:`n_{\mathrm{NO}}  = 7.24 \times 10^{17}\;\mathrm{m}^{-3}`.
* Translational and internal temperatures: both at
  :math:`10\,000~\mathrm{K}` (internal / translational
  equilibrium at :math:`t = 0`).
* Mesh: :math:`1 \times 1 \times 1` cell, fully periodic.
* Time-step: :math:`5 \times 10^{-11}~\mathrm{s}`, total
  integration :math:`10^{-4}~\mathrm{s}`.

Running the case
----------------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/isothermalReactiveHeatbath
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam

Expected outcome
----------------

At the specified conditions, the diatomic populations decay by
roughly an order of magnitude over the simulation time as
dissociation dominates; the atomic O and N populations climb
correspondingly, and NO equilibrates through the Zeldovich
exchange channel. Running the case for long enough brings the
system to an equilibrium set by
:math:`k^{\mathrm{diss}}(T) / k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T)`, which the
calculator at :doc:`../tools/mean_free_path` uses (implicitly, via
the equilibrium constant) to estimate the equilibrium composition
at any :math:`(T, p)`.
