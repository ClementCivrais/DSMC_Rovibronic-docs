Micro-scale channel flow
========================

Directory: ``tutorials/microScaleTestCase/``.

A pressure-driven micro-channel flow of N\ :sub:`2` in the
transitional regime. The case exercises the pressure-inlet /
deletion-outlet combination on a rectangular channel geometry
and is designed to reproduce the measurements of Liou & Fang
[LiouFang2006mc]_ on mass-flow rate versus Knudsen number, which
served as one of the original benchmarks of the dsmcFoam+ solver
[White2013mc]_.

Because the channel height sits at
:math:`\mathrm{Kn} \sim 0.2`, the flow is firmly in the regime
where DSMC is the method of choice (see
:doc:`../theory/foundations`), and neither a pure continuum nor a
pure free-molecular description would be adequate.

Theoretical basis
-----------------

The case touches several theory pages at once:

* :doc:`../theory/foundations` — at
  :math:`\mathrm{Kn} \sim 0.2` the flow is in the
  **transitional regime**: the mean free path is comparable to
  the channel height, so continuum slip-flow models break down.
  DSMC is the natural method of choice.
* :doc:`../theory/wall_models` — the channel walls are
  **fully-diffuse thermal walls**
  (``dsmcDiffuseWallPatch``) at a prescribed :math:`T_w`; the
  velocity is redrawn from the half-range Maxwellian at
  :math:`T_w` on every reflection, and the resulting gas-wall
  temperature jump and velocity slip are captured automatically
  from the particle dynamics.
* :doc:`../theory/wall_models` also documents the
  **pressure-driven inlet** (``dsmcWangPressureInlet`` or the
  Liou-Fang variant) that sets the upstream reservoir
  condition, and the deletion-patch outlet that caps the
  downstream end.
* :doc:`../theory/macroscopic_properties` — the cell-level
  pressure and mean-velocity fields that appear in the
  post-processing are the ingredients of Poiseuille-flow
  theory, from which the analytical continuum solution can be
  derived for comparison with the DSMC output.

The underlying physics is the **Knudsen minimum** of the
normalised mass-flow rate :math:`Q(\delta_m)` discovered by
Knudsen and widely reproduced since: between the continuum and
free-molecular limits, :math:`Q` has a pronounced minimum near
:math:`\mathrm{Kn} \sim 1`, which no continuum theory reproduces
and which is therefore a strong discriminator of a DSMC
implementation.

Validation goals
----------------

#. **Knudsen minimum** — the normalised mass flow
   :math:`Q(\delta_m)` measured across a Knudsen-number sweep
   must exhibit the canonical minimum near
   :math:`\mathrm{Kn} \sim 1`. At the case's nominal
   :math:`\mathrm{Kn} \sim 0.2` the flow sits on the continuum
   side of the minimum.
#. **Quantitative comparison** against the reference
   measurements of Liou & Fang [LiouFang2006mc]_ and
   Ewart et al. — the centreline velocity and the mass-flow
   rate should agree with the published values to within
   plotting accuracy once the Knudsen number is matched.

Diagnostics
-----------

* The ``dsmcBinsMethod`` field model in
  ``system/fieldPropertiesDict`` samples a 1-D bin stack along
  the channel axis (see :doc:`../classes/measurements`); each
  bin returns translational temperature, density, and mean
  velocity.
* A ``dsmcMassFluxSurface`` records the mass flow rate through
  the inlet and outlet patches for direct comparison against
  [LiouFang2006mc]_.

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/microScaleTestCase
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam

.. [LiouFang2006mc] W. W. Liou and Y. Fang,
   *Microfluid Mechanics: Principles and Modeling*, McGraw-Hill
   (2006).
.. [White2013mc] C. White, *Benchmarking, Development and
   Applications of an Open Source DSMC Solver*, PhD thesis,
   University of Glasgow (2013).
