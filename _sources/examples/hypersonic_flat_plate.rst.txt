Hypersonic flat plate
=====================

Directory: ``tutorials/hypersonicFlatPlate/``.

A classic external hypersonic test case: supersonic N\ :sub:`2`
flow over a sharp-leading-edge flat plate. The leading-edge
shock and the flat-plate boundary layer are both captured, and
the case is the natural exercise of the full wall-heat-flux /
skin-friction pipeline through ``boundaryMeasurements`` (see
:doc:`../classes/measurements`).

Theoretical basis
-----------------

The case stacks several physical regimes in a single domain:

* :doc:`../theory/foundations` — near the leading edge the
  Knudsen number based on the local shock thickness is large
  enough for the continuum description to fail, and DSMC is
  required. Far downstream of the plate the boundary layer
  thickens, the local :math:`\mathrm{Kn}` drops, and DSMC
  overlaps with the Navier-Stokes solution.
* :doc:`../theory/wall_models` — the plate surface is a
  **fully-diffuse wall** at :math:`T_w`, with full thermal
  accommodation (:math:`\alpha_T = 1`) by default. The
  reflected velocity is drawn from the half-range Maxwellian
  described in that page, and the accumulated per-face momentum
  and kinetic-energy moments feed the surface heat flux and
  skin-friction diagnostics.
* :doc:`../theory/collision_cross_sections` and
  :doc:`../theory/relaxation` — the VHS cross section governs
  the collision rate in the shock, and the parallel-marginal
  cascade redistributes energy between translational and
  internal modes through the post-shock region.
* :doc:`../theory/macroscopic_properties` — the wall heat flux
  is constructed from the per-face
  :math:`\mathbf{q} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{n}}_w` accumulator
  of :doc:`../classes/measurements`; the skin friction is the
  in-plane component of the per-face momentum moment.

Validation goals
----------------

Three quantitative targets:

#. **Shock stand-off and post-shock state** — the shock
   stand-off distance from the leading edge and the
   post-shock translational temperature must follow the
   Rankine-Hugoniot relations at the free-stream Mach number.
#. **Wall heat-flux profile** — for a laminar boundary layer
   at moderate Reynolds number, the wall heat flux along the
   plate should show the expected :math:`x^{-1/2}` decay; its
   absolute magnitude should match either the Van Driest II
   correlation or a high-resolution CFD solution at the same
   conditions to within plotting accuracy.
#. **Boundary-layer thickness** — the 99 % edge of the
   translational-temperature profile should thicken as
   :math:`x^{1/2}` along the plate.

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2`.
* Free-stream: hypersonic Mach number, prescribed in
  ``system/boundariesDict`` through the
  ``dsmcFreeStreamInflowPatch`` entry.
* Wall: diffuse at :math:`T_w`, specified in the patch
  properties.
* 2-D mesh generated through ``blockMesh``.

Diagnostics
-----------

* Volume fields via ``dsmcVolFields`` —  density, translational
  and internal temperatures, stress tensor, heat flux,
  Mach number.
* Wall heat flux and friction drag via
  ``boundaryMeasurements`` on the plate patch.

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/hypersonicFlatPlate
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam
