Hypersonic flat plate
=====================

Directory: ``tutorials/hypersonicFlatPlate/``.

A classic external hypersonic test case: supersonic N\ :sub:`2`
flow over a sharp-leading-edge flat plate. The leading-edge
shock and the flat-plate boundary layer are both captured, and
the case is the natural exercise of the full wall-heat-flux /
skin-friction pipeline through ``boundaryMeasurements`` (see
:doc:`../classes/measurements`).

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2`.
* Free-stream: hypersonic Mach number, prescribed in
  ``system/boundariesDict`` through the
  ``dsmcFreeStreamInflowPatch`` entry.
* Wall: diffuse at :math:`T_w`, specified in the patch
  properties.
* 2-D mesh generated through ``blockMesh``.

Diagnostics
-----------

* Volume fields via ``dsmcVolFields`` —  density, translational
  and internal temperatures, stress tensor, heat flux,
  Mach number.
* Wall heat flux and friction drag via
  ``boundaryMeasurements`` on the plate patch.

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/hypersonicFlatPlate
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam

Useful validations
------------------

* The shock stand-off distance and the post-shock conditions
  should follow the Rankine-Hugoniot relations at this Mach
  number.
* The wall heat flux profile should show the expected
  :math:`x^{-1/2}`-like decay along the plate for a laminar
  boundary layer at moderate Reynolds number.
