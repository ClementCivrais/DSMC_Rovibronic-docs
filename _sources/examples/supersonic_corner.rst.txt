Supersonic corner
=================

Directory: ``tutorials/supersonicCorner/``.

A supersonic flow of N\ :sub:`2` over a 2-D compression corner.
The case exercises the interaction of an oblique shock with a
boundary layer and a compression fan —  the "shock-boundary-
layer interaction" (SBLI) problem that is central to inlet and
control-surface aerodynamics at hypersonic speeds.

.. note::

   The original tutorial used argon, which is not present in
   the rovibronic speciesCache. The case has been switched to
   N\ :sub:`2` while preserving the geometry; this adds
   internal-degrees-of-freedom relaxation to the flow and is
   therefore a slightly different physical problem than the
   monatomic baseline, but a better match for the solver's
   intended applications.

Theoretical basis
-----------------

The case combines three distinct physical ingredients:

* :doc:`../theory/foundations` — the flow is supersonic,
  compressible, and partly rarefied in the shock structure.
  DSMC captures the shock as a finite-thickness transition
  region of a few mean free paths rather than a jump
  discontinuity.
* :doc:`../theory/collision_cross_sections` and
  :doc:`../theory/collision_sampling` — the collision kernel
  and the NTC sampler determine the shock thickness; any
  change to the cross section or to the acceptance rule shows
  up here as a shift in the shock profile.
* :doc:`../theory/wall_models` — the walls downstream of the
  corner are fully-diffuse (``dsmcDiffuseWallPatch``) at the
  specified :math:`T_w`, which drives the growth of the
  recompression boundary layer after the corner and the
  associated wall heat-flux peak at the reattachment point.
* :doc:`../theory/relaxation` — the post-shock state is not
  in internal equilibrium; the rotational-vibrational cascade
  drives :math:`T_{\mathrm{int}}` toward :math:`T_t` over a
  few mean collision times, and the resulting internal
  temperature gradient feeds into the heat-flux vector
  measured by :doc:`../classes/measurements`.

Validation goals
----------------

#. **Oblique-shock angle** — the observed shock angle must
   follow the oblique-shock relation
   :math:`\tan\theta_w = 2\cot\beta\,(M^{2}\sin^{2}\beta - 1)/
   (M^{2}(\gamma + \cos 2\beta) + 2)` at the free-stream Mach
   number, within the finite-thickness uncertainty of a DSMC
   shock.
#. **Separation / reattachment** — if the corner angle is
   large enough to induce separation, the observed separation
   and reattachment locations should agree with the published
   SBLI literature for similar conditions.
#. **Wall heat-flux peak** — the heat-flux peak at the
   reattachment point should match the Edney-type scaling laws
   for SBLI interactions at hypersonic speeds.

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2`.
* Free-stream Mach number: supersonic (see
  ``system/boundariesDict``).
* 2-D corner geometry generated through ``blockMesh``.
* Diffuse walls at the specified temperature.

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/supersonicCorner
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam
