Free-space periodic equilibrium
===============================

Directory: ``tutorials/freeSpacePeriodic/``.

A periodic cube of N\ :sub:`2` / O\ :sub:`2` in thermodynamic
equilibrium. The case has no walls, no body forces, no
chemistry; the gas is initialised at the target state and the
solver is run for long enough to confirm that the sampled
statistics (temperature, density, stress tensor, mean free path)
remain within statistical bounds of the initial state.

Theoretical basis
-----------------

This is the simplest configuration in which the core DSMC
machinery can be exercised end-to-end:

* :doc:`../theory/foundations` —  the case sits squarely in the
  regime where the Boltzmann equation applies: dilute gas,
  molecular chaos, binary collisions. The time-averaged
  single-particle distribution is expected to be Maxwell-
  Boltzmann, and the H-theorem guarantees that any departure
  from equilibrium would decay monotonically if introduced;
  here nothing perturbs equilibrium so the distribution simply
  stays Maxwellian.
* :doc:`../theory/collision_cross_sections` and
  :doc:`../theory/collision_sampling` —  every collision is
  sampled through the NTC acceptance rule with the VHS cross
  section, but because the distribution is already equilibrium,
  forward and backward rates match exactly (detailed balance).
* :doc:`../theory/macroscopic_properties` —  the per-cell
  estimators of :math:`n, T_t, T_{\mathrm{int}}, p,
  \mathbf{P}, \mathbf{q}` are exercised in the most forgiving
  regime; any systematic error in their definition shows up as
  a drift from the initial value rather than being hidden in a
  non-trivial flow gradient.

Validation goals
----------------

The case is used as a **smoke test** and as a **statistical
convergence reference**. Specifically:

#. **Baseline invariance.** Run with no perturbation; the
   cell-averaged :math:`n, T_t, T_{\mathrm{int}}, p` must
   remain within statistical bounds of their initial values.
#. **Statistical convergence.** With
   ``measureErrors`` enabled on the ``dsmcVolFields`` field
   model (see :doc:`../classes/measurements`), the per-cell
   standard deviation should follow the expected
   :math:`1/\sqrt{N_\mathrm{samples}}` scaling as the
   averaging window grows.
#. **Smoke test for the build.** The case must run to
   completion without NaNs, without unphysical temperature
   drift, and without any diagnostic warning from the cloud —
   a failure here is almost always a symptom of a build-time
   or linkage problem rather than a physics bug.
#. **Chemistry-on reference.** Turning chemistry on with
   otherwise identical settings and comparing to this case
   isolates the chemistry module's effect on the macroscopic
   state.

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`.
* Fully periodic cube.
* Run-time configured in ``system/controlDict``; the default
  end-time is sufficient to confirm that the sampled macroscopic
  fields have settled.

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/freeSpacePeriodic
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam
