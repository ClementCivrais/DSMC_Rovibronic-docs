Collision-rate test
===================

Directory: ``tutorials/collisionRateTest/``.

A periodic N\ :sub:`2` / O\ :sub:`2` case specifically designed
to stress the collision-partner-selection layer. The
``cellMeasurements`` class accumulates the per-cell collision
count and the mean collision separation, both of which are
written alongside the macroscopic fields.

Theoretical basis
-----------------

The case directly probes two theory pages of the guide:

* :doc:`../theory/collision_cross_sections` — the Variable Hard
  Sphere (VHS) cross section

  .. math::

     \sigma_T^{\mathrm{VHS}}(g)
       = \pi\,d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}\,
         \left(\frac{g_{\mathrm{ref}}}{g}\right)^{2\omega - 1}
         \frac{1}{\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)}

  fixes the collision cross section at any relative speed
  :math:`g`; the species-pair parameters :math:`d_\mathrm{ref},
  \omega` are read from :doc:`../classes/species_database`.
* :doc:`../theory/collision_sampling` — the No-Time-Counter
  (NTC) scheme draws

  .. math::

     N_{\mathrm{cand}}
       = \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{N(N-1)\,F_N\,
         (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\,\Delta t}{V_c}

  candidates per cell per time-step and accepts them with
  probability :math:`\sigma_T g / (\sigma_T g)_{\max}`, so that
  the expected cell-level collision rate is

  .. math::

     \nu_{\mathrm{coll}}
       = \frac{N(N-1)\,F_N\,\langle \sigma_T g\rangle}{2\,V_c}.

The case is the tightest available check that this construction
is bug-free at the cell level: agreement between the sampled
rate and the Maxwell-averaged theoretical rate is the acceptance
criterion.

Validation goals
----------------

Two quantitative targets:

#. **Collision rate.** The cell-averaged collision rate
   accumulated in ``cellMeasurements`` should match
   :math:`\nu_{\mathrm{coll}}` evaluated from the VSS species
   parameters to within a few percent. A systematic bias is
   usually traceable to either the :math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}`
   estimator, to an incorrect :math:`F_N` normalisation, or to
   a dropped factor of :math:`1/2` on a self-species candidate
   pair.
#. **Mean collision separation** — at equilibrium the
   characteristic collision separation should be comparable to
   :math:`\lambda / 2` with :math:`\lambda` the VSS mean free
   path predicted by the calculator at
   :doc:`../tools/mean_free_path`. A large departure in either
   direction is a signal that the DSMC sub-cell resolution is
   too coarse (collisions occur between parcels that are not
   close neighbours, inflating the separation) or that the
   particle-per-cell count is below the statistical floor.

Passing both checks is the sanity gate before running any case
that depends quantitatively on the collision rate (chemistry,
shock structure, boundary-layer drag).

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/collisionRateTest
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam
