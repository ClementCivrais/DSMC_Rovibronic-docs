Axisymmetric blunt cylinder
===========================

Directory: ``tutorials/axisymmetricBluntCylinder/``.

Hypersonic flow over a blunt-ended cylinder, solved in the
solver's axisymmetric mode (``axisymmetricSimulation true`` in
``constant/dsmcProperties``). The bow shock, the stagnation
region, and the cylindrical after-body are all captured in a
single 2-D slice.

.. note::

   The original tutorial used argon, which is not present in
   the rovibronic speciesCache. The case has been switched to
   N\ :sub:`2` while preserving the geometry.

Theoretical basis
-----------------

The case combines three distinct ingredients:

* :doc:`../theory/foundations` —  the Knudsen number varies
  sharply from inside the shock layer (rarefied, DSMC
  essential) to the post-stagnation region near the wall. The
  sub-cell resolution of :doc:`../classes/adaptation` is the
  natural tool for resolving the shock without refining the
  whole mesh.
* **Axisymmetric particle tracking.** The
  ``axisymmetricSimulation true`` switch tells the cloud to
  weight each parcel by its radial position :math:`r`, so that
  a 2-D wedge mesh reproduces the 3-D flux correctly. The
  statistical weight of a parcel at radius :math:`r` scales as
  :math:`r`, which is enforced in the moment accumulators of
  :doc:`../classes/measurements` and in the parcel
  insertion/deletion logic at inflows and deletion patches.
* :doc:`../theory/wall_models` —  a fully-diffuse wall at
  :math:`T_w` sets the stagnation-point heat flux; the
  Fay-Riddell correlation provides the continuum reference
  value against which the DSMC stagnation heat flux is
  compared.

Validation goals
----------------

Three quantitative targets:

#. **Bow-shock stand-off distance** — the computed stand-off
   :math:`\Delta` at the stagnation line should agree with the
   Ambrosio-Wortman correlation :math:`\Delta/R \approx
   0.386\,\exp(4.67/M_\infty^{2})` (for blunt bodies at
   moderate Mach numbers) within the finite-thickness
   uncertainty of the DSMC shock.
#. **Stagnation-point heat flux** — the wall heat flux at the
   stagnation point, constructed from the per-face
   accumulators of :doc:`../classes/measurements`, should
   follow the Fay-Riddell scaling with the free-stream
   conditions.
#. **Internal-temperature overshoot** — in the post-shock
   region the translational temperature spikes above the
   internal temperature; the two relax back to equilibrium
   within a few mean collision times, following the
   Landau-Teller decay documented in :doc:`../theory/relaxation`.
   The observed relaxation length should match the Park /
   Millikan-White estimate at the local density.

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2`.
* Axisymmetric geometry, 2-D wedge mesh.
* Free-stream hypersonic inflow; diffuse wall on the body.
* ``axisymmetricSimulation true`` switches the cloud into the
  axisymmetric particle-tracking mode, which applies the
  correct radial weighting.

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/axisymmetricBluntCylinder
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam
