Axisymmetric blunt cylinder
===========================

Directory: ``tutorials/axisymmetricBluntCylinder/``.

Hypersonic flow over a blunt-ended cylinder, solved in the
solver's axisymmetric mode (``axisymmetricSimulation true`` in
``constant/dsmcProperties``). The bow shock, the stagnation
region, and the cylindrical after-body are all captured in a
single 2-D slice.

.. note::

   The original tutorial used argon, which is not present in
   the rovibronic speciesCache. The case has been switched to
   N\ :sub:`2` while preserving the geometry.

Case parameters
---------------

* Species: N\ :sub:`2`.
* Axisymmetric geometry, 2-D wedge mesh.
* Free-stream hypersonic inflow; diffuse wall on the body.
* ``axisymmetricSimulation true`` switches the cloud into the
  axisymmetric particle-tracking mode, which applies the
  correct radial weighting.

Running
-------

.. code-block:: bash

   cd tutorials/axisymmetricBluntCylinder
   blockMesh
   dsmcInitialise
   dsmcFoam

Useful validations
------------------

* The stagnation-point heat flux should follow Fay-Riddell-like
  scaling with the free-stream conditions.
* The bow-shock stand-off distance should agree with Ambrosio
  and Wortman correlations at the relevant Reynolds and Mach
  numbers.
