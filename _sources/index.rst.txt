DSMC Rovibronic
===============

The modelling of aerothermodynamic flows is extremely challenging,
as it combines both aerodynamics and thermodynamics in a regime in
which the gas is out of equilibrium and the classical continuum
assumptions fail. The Direct Simulation Monte Carlo (DSMC) method
provides a particle-based description of the gas that remains
valid throughout the rarefied and transitional regimes, and is
therefore the method of choice for hypersonic re-entry, nozzle
expansion, and high-altitude plume simulations.

The present solver is a **custom rovibronic version of dsmcFoam+**,
itself a modification of the legacy OpenFOAM DSMC solver
``dsmcFoam``. The dsmcFoam+ solver, from which this work
descends, was developed by C. White and co-workers at the
University of Glasgow [White2018]_; the rovibronic extension
described here is developed by C. H. B. Civrais (University of
California, Irvine) under the supervision of C. White (University
of Glasgow, PI). The solver inherits the full DSMC core of
dsmcFoam+ and extends it with a state-resolved description of the
molecular internal energy, in which each simulator particle
carries three integers —  the electronic :math:`n`, vibrational
:math:`v`, and rotational :math:`J` quantum numbers —  that index
energy and degeneracy tables computed from spectroscopic
constants. We thereby replace the usual continuous, or
multi-temperature, representation of the internal energy by a
discrete, physically grounded rovibronic level structure, and
recover the non-Boltzmann population distributions that
characterise strong shocks, rapid expansions, and state-selective
chemistry.

The methodological foundations of the rovibronic extension have
been published in a series of articles [Civrais2024Electronic]_,
[Civrais2024ChemI]_, [Civrais2024ChemII]_, [Civrais2024Vib]_,
[Civrais2024Shock]_, and are documented in detail in the lead
developer's doctoral thesis [CivraisThesis]_.
