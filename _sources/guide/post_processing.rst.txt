Post-processing
===============

The solver writes the usual OpenFOAM time directories, augmented by
per-particle fields that capture the full rovibronic state. We
distinguish three families of diagnostics: macroscopic fields,
state-resolved populations, and dedicated validation quantities.

Macroscopic fields
------------------

The standard DSMC volume fields —  number density, translational
and internal temperatures, pressure, species mass fractions, and
mean velocity —  are written to ``<case>/<time>/`` and may be
visualised with ``paraFoam``. Under strong non-equilibrium
conditions, the internal temperatures should be interpreted as
cell-level effective temperatures extracted from the population
distributions; the physical solution resides in the populations
themselves (see :doc:`../theory/macroscopic_properties`).

State-resolved populations
--------------------------

The rovibronic quantum numbers :math:`(n, v, J)` are stored on
each Lagrangian parcel and can be extracted via dedicated
utilities under ``applications/utilities/``. Typical use cases
include:

* plotting the vibrational population :math:`P(v)` at fixed
  electronic state, and comparing it against the Boltzmann
  distribution at the local translational temperature;
* monitoring the population of the first few excited electronic
  levels as a function of time in a shock-heated flow;
* constructing rotational-vibrational distributions to assess the
  assumption of rotational-vibrational equilibrium.

Validation diagnostics
----------------------

The :doc:`../reference/validation` page collects the canonical
checks used to confirm the rovibronic machinery: heat-bath
relaxation against the multi-exponential Landau-Teller solution,
isothermal dissociation against the equilibrium constant, and
one-dimensional shock profiles against published reference data.
