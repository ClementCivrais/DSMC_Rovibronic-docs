Binary collision models
=======================

Source: ``src/lagrangian/DSMC/collisions/derived/``.

Role
----

Binary collision models prescribe two things: the
**cross section** used to weigh accepted collision candidates,
and the **post-collision scattering and internal-energy
redistribution** that is applied once a collision has been
accepted. They are selected through the ``BinaryCollisionModel``
key in ``constant/dsmcProperties`` and configured through the
associated ``<Model>Coeffs`` sub-dictionary.

Implemented classes
-------------------

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 40 60

   * - Class
     - Purpose
   * - ``LarsenBorgnakkeVariableHardSphere``
     - Variable Hard Sphere (VHS) cross section with
       Larsen-Borgnakke (LB) energy redistribution; the
       **default** and most frequently used model.
   * - ``LarsenBorgnakkeVariableSoftSphere``
     - Variable Soft Sphere (VSS) cross section with LB energy
       redistribution; adds the anisotropic scattering exponent
       :math:`\alpha`, which interpolates between isotropic and
       forward-peaked scattering.
   * - ``VariableHardSphere``
     - Plain VHS without LB: purely elastic collisions, no
       internal-energy exchange. Useful for debugging, or for
       cases in which internal degrees of freedom are not of
       interest.
   * - ``VariableSoftSphere``
     - Plain VSS variant of the above.
   * - ``NoBinaryCollision``
     - Disables binary collisions entirely. Used for free-
       molecular verification cases and for isolating
       boundary-driven effects.

VHS cross section
-----------------

The Variable Hard Sphere cross section depends on the relative
velocity :math:`g` through the viscosity exponent :math:`\omega`:

.. math::

   \sigma_T^{\mathrm{VHS}}(g)
     = \pi\, d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}\,
       \left(\frac{g_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}}{g^{2}}\right)^{\omega - 1/2}
       \frac{1}{\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)},

where :math:`d_{\mathrm{ref}}`, :math:`g_{\mathrm{ref}}`, and
:math:`\omega` are species-pair parameters tabulated in
:doc:`species_database`. The VHS law is isotropic in the centre-
of-mass frame.

VSS cross section and scattering
--------------------------------

The Variable Soft Sphere model augments VHS with an anisotropic
scattering law parameterised by :math:`\alpha`. The differential
cross section is

.. math::

   \frac{\mathrm{d}\sigma}{\mathrm{d}\Omega}
     \propto \cos^{2(\alpha - 1)}\!\left(\tfrac{\chi}{2}\right),

where :math:`\chi` is the centre-of-mass scattering angle. The
limit :math:`\alpha = 1` recovers the isotropic VHS scattering;
values :math:`\alpha > 1`, which are commonly fitted to viscosity
and diffusion data, yield forward-peaked scattering consistent
with the Lennard-Jones interaction of diatomics.

Larsen-Borgnakke energy redistribution
--------------------------------------

On each accepted collision, the LB variants sample a new internal
energy for the participating parcels, using the acceptance-
rejection criterion

.. math::

   f(E')
     = g(E')\,
       \left(\frac{E_c - E'}{E_c}\right)^{\alpha_{\mathrm{LB}}}, \qquad
   \alpha_{\mathrm{LB}}
     = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(\xi_{\mathrm{rel}}
                         + \xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}\bigr) - 1,

where :math:`E_c` is the pooled collision energy,
:math:`g(E')` is the degeneracy of the candidate state, and
:math:`\xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}` is the effective
number of internal degrees of freedom that enter the current
event. The rovibronic extension evaluates :math:`g(E')` and
:math:`E'` on the quantised state space rather than on a
continuous internal-energy variable; the three composite-mode
cascades (rotational, rovibrational, rovibronic) that build on
top of this acceptance rule are documented in
:doc:`../theory/relaxation`.

Coefficient sub-dictionary
--------------------------

For the LB variants, the ``<Model>Coeffs`` block holds the
reference temperature and the three relaxation collision numbers
:math:`Z_r,\,Z_{rv},\,Z_{rve}`:

.. code-block:: cpp

   LarsenBorgnakkeVariableHardSphereCoeffs
   {
       Tref                                    273; // [K]
       rotationalRelaxationCollisionNumber     2;   // [-]
       rovibrationalRelaxationCollisionNumber  10;  // [-]
       rovibronicRelaxationCollisionNumber     10;  // [-]
   }

The plain VHS/VSS variants do not expose the collision-number
entries, since they do not redistribute internal energy.
