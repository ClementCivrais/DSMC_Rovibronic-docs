Boundary classes
================

Source: ``src/lagrangian/DSMC/boundaries/`` (basic and derived).

The boundary subsystem implements the particle-patch interactions
that take place whenever a parcel's trajectory intersects a face
that lies on the domain boundary. Three families are
distinguished: **patch boundaries** (walls, inflows, outflows),
**cyclic boundaries** (periodic and reflective membranes), and
**general boundaries** (pressure inlets, mass-flow inlets, and a
handful of specialised inlet types).

Each patch-boundary class is selected by its ``boundaryModel``
key in ``system/boundariesDict``; the associated configuration
lives in an adjacent ``<BoundaryModel>Properties`` sub-dictionary.

Patch boundaries
----------------

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 40 15 45

   * - Class
     - Rovibronic-aware?
     - Purpose
   * - ``dsmcDiffuseWallPatch``
     - No
     - Fully diffuse reflection at a specified wall temperature.
       Velocity is redrawn from a Maxwellian at the wall
       temperature; internal energies are redrawn from the stock
       continuous model.
   * - ``dsmcDiffuseWallFieldPatch``
     - No
     - Field-varying diffuse-wall variant: the wall temperature
       and velocity are read from fields rather than scalar
       entries.
   * - ``dsmcSpecularWallPatch``
     - No
     - Perfectly specular reflection.
   * - ``dsmcMixedDiffuseSpecularWallPatch``
     - No
     - Weighted mixture of diffuse and specular, controlled by a
       scalar ``diffuseFraction``.
   * - ``dsmcMixedDiffuseSpecularWallFieldPatch``
     - No
     - Field-varying variant of the above.
   * - ``dsmcMixedDiffuseSpecularWallRotPatch``
     - **Yes**
     - Rovibronic-aware mixed wall: on a diffuse reflection,
       the rovibronic state :math:`(n, v, J)` is redrawn from the
       database partition function at the wall temperature.
   * - ``dsmcCLLWallPatch``
     - No
     - Cercignani-Lampis-Lord scattering model.
   * - ``dsmcCLLWallFieldPatch``
     - No
     - Field-varying CLL variant.
   * - ``dsmcDeletionPatch``
     - —
     - Particle sink: parcels that hit the patch are removed. A
       ``typeIds`` list or ``allSpecies`` switch controls which
       parcels are deleted.
   * - ``dsmcFreeStreamInflowPatch``
     - No
     - Maxwellian free-stream inflow at a prescribed density,
       temperature, and velocity.
   * - ``dsmcFreeStreamInflowFieldPatch``
     - No
     - Field-varying free-stream inflow.
   * - ``dsmcChapmanEnskogFreeStreamInflowPatch``
     - No
     - First-order (Chapman-Enskog) non-equilibrium inflow,
       useful when coupling DSMC to a continuum upstream.
   * - ``dsmcWangPressureInlet``, ``dsmcLiouFangPressureInlet``,
       ``dsmcMassFlowRateInlet``
     - No
     - Pressure- and mass-flow-driven inlets; consult the
       respective class headers for parameter lists.

Rovibronic wall
---------------

``dsmcMixedDiffuseSpecularWallRotPatch`` is the only patch class
that *samples on the quantum-state space* at the wall. On a
diffuse reflection it re-draws the reflected parcel's
:math:`(n, v, J)` from

.. math::

   P(n, v, J)
     = \frac{g(n, v, J)\,\exp\!\bigl[-E(n, v, J)/k_B T_w\bigr]}
            {\sum_{n',v',J'} g(n',v',J')\,
              \exp\!\bigl[-E(n',v',J')/k_B T_w\bigr]},

where :math:`T_w` is the wall temperature specified in the
sub-dictionary. For solver cases where the internal populations at
the wall matter —  surface catalysis, radiation coupling, high-
altitude re-entry —  this is the patch class to use.

Cyclic boundaries
-----------------

The ``dsmcCyclicBoundaries`` list in ``boundariesDict`` accepts
periodic-style boundaries. The reference tutorial ships a
commented example of ``dsmcReflectiveParticleMembranePatch``,
which reflects a fraction of incoming particles back into the
domain and lets the rest pass through.

General boundaries
------------------

The ``dsmcGeneralBoundaries`` list accepts non-patch, non-cyclic
boundary types that operate on a patch surface but also carry a
volume component —  for instance the various pressure-driven
inlets listed above. Their configuration follows the same
``generalBoundaryProperties { patchName … }`` /
``<model>Properties { … }`` two-block structure as the patch
boundaries, and they are driven through the ``dsmcGeneralBoundary``
interface at ``src/lagrangian/DSMC/boundaries/basic/dsmcGeneralBoundary/``.

Boundary measurements
---------------------

Independently of which patch class is attached, a
``boundaryMeasurements`` object accumulates per-patch, per-face
diagnostics on every particle impact:

* number density of neutral and internal particles
  (``rhoNBF_``, ``rhoNIntBF_``) and mass density (``rhoMBF_``);
* linear kinetic energy (``linearKEBF_``);
* momentum moments and mean velocity on the patch;
* **internal (rovibronic) energy** reaching the boundary
  (``internalEBF_``);
* heat flux and friction drag vectors.

These quantities are available at the end of a run through the
standard OpenFOAM boundary-field output, and are the inputs to
any downstream analysis of wall heat flux, drag, or surface
catalysis. The code lives in
``src/lagrangian/DSMC/boundaryMeasurements/``.
