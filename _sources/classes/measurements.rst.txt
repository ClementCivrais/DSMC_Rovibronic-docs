Measurements
============

Source: ``src/lagrangian/DSMC/macroscopicProperties/``,
``src/lagrangian/DSMC/cellMeasurements/``,
``src/lagrangian/DSMC/boundaryMeasurements/``.

Role
----

Measurements are the non-invasive observers of the simulation.
They do not alter the particle population; they only accumulate
and write diagnostics. Three layers coexist in the rovibronic
solver:

* **per-cell samplers** (``cellMeasurements``) that accumulate
  the primitive moments on which every volume field is built;
* **per-patch samplers** (``boundaryMeasurements``) that
  accumulate the equivalent moments on boundary faces;
* **configurable field models** (``macroscopicProperties/``)
  that the user selects in ``system/fieldPropertiesDict`` to
  produce the desired output: volume fields, bin histograms, zone
  averages, rovibronic population distributions, or surface
  fluxes.

Cell measurements
-----------------

``cellMeasurements`` (``src/lagrangian/DSMC/cellMeasurements/``) is
the primitive accumulator. It holds, per cell and per species:

* collision counts (``nColls_``) and the mean collision separation
  :math:`\langle s \rangle` (``collisionSeparation_``);
* mass-weighted moment tensors of the peculiar velocity
  (``muu_``, ``muv_``, ``muw_``, etc.) needed to build the
  translational temperature and the stress tensor;
* the total rovibronic energy accumulated over the cell and its
  square, for the variance of the internal-energy estimator.

These quantities are updated every time-step by the cloud's
``calculateFields()`` method and are consumed downstream by every
field model below.

Boundary measurements
---------------------

``boundaryMeasurements`` (``src/lagrangian/DSMC/boundaryMeasurements/``)
accumulates per-face impact statistics on every patch. Each
particle–face intersection contributes to:

* internal and mass densities on the patch
  (``rhoNIntBF_``, ``rhoNBF_``, ``rhoMBF_``);
* linear kinetic energy (``linearKEBF_``) and full momentum
  vector;
* **rovibronic internal energy** delivered to the wall
  (``internalEBF_``);
* heat-flux and friction-drag vectors.

These are the ingredients from which any wall diagnostic —  heat
flux, drag, catalytic chemistry —  is constructed, and they are
written as boundary fields in the standard OpenFOAM output.

Configurable field models
-------------------------

Field models are selected by the user through
``system/fieldPropertiesDict``. Each entry specifies a
``fieldModel`` (the class name) and a matching
``<fieldModel>Properties`` block that configures its behaviour.

``dsmcVolFields`` —  volume-averaged fields
```````````````````````````````````````````

*Source:* ``macroscopicProperties/derived/combined/dsmcVolFields/``.

The workhorse of DSMC post-processing. Produces the standard
volume fields that appear in ``<case>/<time>/``:

* number and mass density (``rhoN``, ``rhoM``);
* translational and internal temperatures;
* pressure scalar and tensor; shear-stress tensor;
* heat-flux vector;
* mean free path and Mach number;
* optional error estimators on each of the above.

Configuration keys include ``field``, ``typeIds``,
``averagingAcrossManyRuns``, ``measureMeanFreePath``,
``measureErrors``, ``densityOnly``, and
``measureHeatFluxShearStress``.

``dsmcBinsMethod`` —  one-, two-, or three-dimensional bins
```````````````````````````````````````````````````````````

*Source:* ``macroscopicProperties/derived/combined/dsmcBinsMethod/``.

Measures macroscopic properties in histogram-style bins that are
independent of the mesh. Useful for one-dimensional shock profiles
and for angular or radial binning. A pluggable ``binModel``
(e.g. ``uniformBins``) defines the bin geometry; the sub-dictionary
``dsmcBinsMethodProperties`` carries ``binModel``, ``field``,
``zone``, ``typeIds``, and ``area`` (for cross-sectional flux
normalisation).

``dsmcZone`` —  zone averages
`````````````````````````````

*Source:* ``macroscopicProperties/derived/combined/dsmcZone/``.

Returns volume-averaged macroscopic properties over a named
``cellZone``: number and mass density, translational and
effective internal temperatures, pressure, heat flux, shear
stress, and the internal-temperature bisection applied to the
rovibronic energy. Useful for zero-dimensional reference
quantities in heat-bath cases. The key
``measureCollisionRateOnly`` restricts the output to collision
statistics, which is the cheapest possible measurement mode.

``dsmcVelocityDistributionZone``, ``dsmcSpeedDistributionZone``
```````````````````````````````````````````````````````````````

*Source:* ``macroscopicProperties/derived/velocity/``.

Accumulates histograms of the velocity (component-wise) or speed
(magnitude) over the parcels inside a named zone. The histogram
bin width is set through the ``binWidth`` key. These measurements
are the primary way to confirm that the velocity distribution
remains Maxwellian (or to quantify how it does not).

``dsmcRovibronicPopulation`` —  state-resolved populations
``````````````````````````````````````````````````````````

*Source:*
``macroscopicProperties/derived/otherFields/dsmcRovibronicPopulation/``.

Accumulates the full four-index array
``population[cellI][n][v][J]`` for a selected species in a
selected zone. This is the diagnostic that exercises the central
promise of the rovibronic extension: it allows the user to plot
the vibrational-rotational-electronic distribution as it actually
is, rather than as it would be if it were Boltzmann. The
sub-dictionary takes ``fieldName``, ``zoneName``, and a single
``typeId``.

``dsmcMassFluxSurface`` —  flux through a face zone
```````````````````````````````````````````````````

*Source:* ``macroscopicProperties/derived/massFlux/dsmcMassFluxSurface/``.

Integrates mass, number, momentum, and energy fluxes through a
user-defined ``faceZone``. Correctly handles processor-patch
ownership (contributing factor :math:`0.5` on shared faces) so
that totals remain correct in parallel. The sub-dictionary takes
``field``, ``typeIds``, ``faceZone``, and ``fluxDirection``.

Common conventions
------------------

Every field model shares three lifecycle hooks: ``createFields()``
at construction, ``calculateField()`` at every time-step, and
``writeField()`` at the output interval. Measurements that need to
average across several time-steps maintain an internal counter and
reset it after each write. The container class
``dsmcFieldProperties``
(``macroscopicProperties/basic/dsmcFieldProperties/``) iterates
through the registered field models and orchestrates these calls.
