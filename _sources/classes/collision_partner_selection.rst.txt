Collision partner selection
===========================

Source: ``src/lagrangian/DSMC/collisionRate/derived/``.

Role
----

The collision partner selection layer decides, in each cell and at
each time-step, *how many* candidate pairs to draw and *which*
pairs to consider. It is orthogonal to the binary collision model
(:doc:`collision_models`), which determines what happens once a
pair has been accepted. The selection scheme is chosen through the
``collisionPartnerSelectionModel`` key in
``constant/dsmcProperties``.

The default is ``noTimeCounter``, which is the standard DSMC
scheme and is appropriate for the vast majority of cases. The
sub-cycled, majorant, and simplified-Bernoulli alternatives are
targeted at specific regimes —  notably electron physics and
cases in which the distribution of cross sections spans several
orders of magnitude —  and are documented below alongside the
default.

Implemented classes
-------------------

``noTimeCounter``
`````````````````

*Directory:* ``collisionRate/derived/noTimeCounter/``.

The No-Time-Counter (NTC) scheme of Bird [Bird1994p]_ is the
reference choice. The expected number of collision candidates in
a cell of volume :math:`V_c` over the time-step :math:`\Delta t`
is

.. math::

   N_{\mathrm{cand}}
     = \frac{1}{2}\,\frac{N (N - 1)\, F_N\,
                          (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\,\Delta t}{V_c},

where :math:`N` is the number of particles in the cell,
:math:`F_N` is the equivalent-molecules factor, and
:math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` is a running estimate of the maximum
cross-section-times-relative-speed product. Candidates are drawn
at random from the cell inventory and accepted with probability
:math:`\sigma_T g / (\sigma_T g)_{\max}`.

``noTimeCounterSubCycled``
``````````````````````````

*Directory:* ``collisionRate/derived/noTimeCounterSubCycled/``.

A sub-cycled variant of NTC in which a second, smaller internal
time-step is used for a specified subset of collision pairs
(typically those involving electrons, which have collision
frequencies orders of magnitude higher than those of heavy
species). The sub-dictionary exposes

.. code-block:: cpp

   noTimeCounterSubCycledProperties
   {
       nSubCycles   <label>;
   }

where ``nSubCycles`` is the number of internal sub-steps per
global DSMC step.

``majorantCollisionFrequency``
``````````````````````````````

*Directory:* ``collisionRate/derived/majorantCollisionFrequency/``.

The Majorant Collision Frequency (MCF) scheme of Ivanov et al. is
designed for cases in which a handful of species pairs dominate
the total collision rate, so that the NTC acceptance probability
becomes inefficiently small for the remaining pairs. The method
trades a tighter upper bound on :math:`\sigma_T g` for a higher
acceptance rate, at the cost of slightly more book-keeping. The
implemented variant follows the particle-based formulation of
Pfeiffer et al. (*Physics of Fluids*, 2019,
https://doi.org/10.1063/1.5119580).

``simplifiedBernoulliTrials``
`````````````````````````````

*Directory:* ``collisionRate/derived/simplifiedBernoulliTrials/``.

The Simplified Bernoulli Trials (SBT) scheme replaces the global
:math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` estimate by a per-pair Bernoulli
trial. It is closer to the original Nanbu algorithm in spirit and
is occasionally preferable in low-particle-per-cell regimes.

Choosing between schemes
------------------------

In practice:

* use ``noTimeCounter`` for general-purpose DSMC work; it has
  been exercised on every validation case in this project;
* use ``noTimeCounterSubCycled`` when ``chargedParticles`` is
  enabled and the electron-electron or electron-neutral collision
  rates are expected to dominate the DSMC time-step;
* consider ``majorantCollisionFrequency`` when the cross-section
  distribution across species pairs spans multiple decades;
* ``simplifiedBernoulliTrials`` is primarily of pedagogical
  interest in this implementation and is not recommended as a
  default.

.. [Bird1994p] G. A. Bird, *Molecular Gas Dynamics and the Direct
   Simulation of Gas Flows*, Oxford University Press (1994).
