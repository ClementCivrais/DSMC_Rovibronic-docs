Energy Relaxation
=================

Internal relaxation drives the rovibronic population of each
molecular species toward equilibrium with the local translational
temperature. In the present solver this is achieved through a
**parallel-marginal cascade** in which rotational, rovibrational,
and rovibronic events are mutually exclusive at the single-collision
level, yet reproduce the correct multi-exponential Landau-Teller
relaxation of the composite internal energy in the macroscopic
limit [CivraisThesis_r]_.

Landau-Teller rate per mode
---------------------------

Following the Landau-Teller framework, the mean internal energy in
mode :math:`k` approaches its equilibrium value with time constant
:math:`\tau_k`:

.. math::

   \frac{\mathrm{d}\,\langle E_k\rangle}{\mathrm{d}t}
     = \frac{\langle E_k\rangle(T_T) - \langle E_k\rangle(t)}
            {Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}\,\tau_c},

where :math:`T_T` is the translational temperature,
:math:`Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}` is the continuum collision number for
mode :math:`k`, and :math:`\tau_c = 1/\nu_{\mathrm{coll}}` is the
mean collision time. Under isothermal conditions the solution is a
simple exponential decay; in the multi-mode case, with three
composite modes relaxing on well-separated timescales, the
approach is the sum of three exponentials

.. math::

   \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t) =
     \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star}
     - \Delta E_R\, e^{-t/\tau_R}
     - \Delta E_V\, e^{-t/\tau_{RV}}
     - \Delta E_E\, e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}}.

Equivalently,

.. math::

   \frac{\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star}
         - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t)}
        {\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star}
         - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(0)}
   = A_R e^{-t/\tau_R} + A_V e^{-t/\tau_{RV}} + A_E e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}},

with mode amplitudes :math:`A_k = \Delta E_k / \Delta E_{\mathrm{total}}`
satisfying :math:`A_R + A_V + A_E = 1`.

Composite modes and effective DOF
---------------------------------

We identify three composite modes, each characterised by which
quantum numbers are sampled and by the energy floor below which the
mode's contribution vanishes:

* mode :math:`R` (rotational only) — samples :math:`J` at fixed
  :math:`(n, v)`; floor :math:`E_R^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, v, 0)`;
* mode :math:`RV` (rovibrational) — samples :math:`(v, J)` at fixed
  :math:`n`; floor :math:`E_{RV}^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, 0, 0)`;
* mode :math:`RVE` (rovibronic) — samples :math:`(n, v, J)` over
  the full table; floor :math:`E_{RVE}^{\mathrm{floor}} = 0`.

The effective degrees of freedom of each composite mode are computed
on the fly from the Boltzmann average

.. math::

   \xi_k(T) = \frac{2 \langle E_k\rangle(T)}{k_B T},

evaluated from the tabulated partition functions.

Parallel-marginal cascade
-------------------------

On each accepted collision we draw a single uniform
:math:`R \sim \mathcal{U}(0, 1)` and select one of four mutually
exclusive events :math:`\{E_0, E_1, E_2, E_3\}` with probabilities

.. math::

   P_1 = \frac{1}{Z_{r}\,C_{r}}, \quad
   P_2 = \frac{1}{Z_{rv}\,C_{rv}}, \quad
   P_3 = \frac{1}{Z_{rve}\,C_{rve}},

and :math:`P_0 = 1 - P_1 - P_2 - P_3`. Events :math:`E_1,\,E_2,\,E_3`
correspond to rotational, rovibrational, and rovibronic sampling on
their respective subspaces, with Larsen-Borgnakke acceptance and
the exponent

.. math::

   \alpha_k = \tfrac{1}{2}(\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \xi_k^{\mathrm{pooled}})
              - 1

where :math:`\xi_k^{\mathrm{pooled}}` is the effective DOF of the
mode plus any lower modes that re-equilibrate at the same event.
Event :math:`E_0` leaves the internal state unchanged (elastic-like
collision from the internal-energy standpoint).

The factors :math:`C_k` are the **Nizenkov correction factors**

.. math::

   C_k = \frac{\xi_T\,(T_T - T'_k)}
              {\xi_k(T_T)\,T_T - \xi_k(T_k)\,T_k},

where :math:`T'_k` is the pool temperature satisfying local energy
conservation. Their role is to recover the target Landau-Teller
rate per mode from the cell-level acceptance statistics; without
them, the macroscopic relaxation drifts systematically from the
analytical LT solution.

When the sum :math:`P_1 + P_2 + P_3 > 1`, the probabilities are
rescaled to saturate the collision; this effectively raises the
operational :math:`Z_k` seen by the solver, and is flagged in the
diagnostic output.

Validation protocol (phase-by-phase)
------------------------------------

The cascade is validated in three phases, each isolating one
composite mode by sending the collision numbers of the others to
infinity (i.e. setting them to very large values in the input
dictionary):

* **Phase 1** — rotational only:
  :math:`Z_{rv} = Z_{rve} = \infty`; initial condition
  :math:`T_T = 10^{4}~\mathrm{K}`, :math:`T_R(0) = 10^{3}~\mathrm{K}`.
  Expected: single-exponential decay with time constant
  :math:`\tau_R = Z_r^{\mathrm{cont}}\,\tau_c`.
* **Phase 2** — rovibrational:
  :math:`Z_r = \infty`, :math:`Z_{rve} = \infty`.
  Expected: double-exponential decay with a fast rotational
  equilibration followed by vibrational relaxation.
* **Phase 3** — all modes active: finite
  :math:`Z_r,\,Z_{rv},\,Z_{rve}`.
  Expected: triple-exponential with the hierarchy
  :math:`\tau_R \ll \tau_{RV} \ll \tau_{RVE}`.

Success is declared when the fitted collision number satisfies
:math:`|Z_{k,\mathrm{fit}}/Z_k - 1| < 5\%`. At the time of writing,
Phase 1 passes comfortably (:math:`Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z \approx 1.07`),
Phase 2 overcorrects (:math:`\approx 0.83`), and Phase 3 is still
being diagnosed.

On the absence of the classic ZCorr
-----------------------------------

A separate study of the rovibronic Larsen-Borgnakke path showed that
the ratio :math:`Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z_{\mathrm{input}}` sits at
approximately 0.99 without any additional Z-correction factor
[Civrais2024Vib_r]_. The classic ZCorr used in continuous-
internal-energy models is therefore **not required** on the
rovibronic LB path, and its prior inclusion was traced to a
compensating bug that has since been fixed; the interested reader
will find the full analysis in
``docs/Rovibronic Article/DSMC_Bugs_Report.pdf``.
