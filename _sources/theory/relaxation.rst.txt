Internal-energy relaxation
==========================

We now document the relaxation of the rotational, vibrational,
and electronic internal modes of the molecular species toward
equilibrium with the translational mode. The present section
derives the Landau-Teller rate equation from first principles,
describes the Millikan-White and Parker empirical correlations
from which the relaxation collision numbers are read, introduces
the Nizenkov correction that guarantees rate-matching in a
multi-mode cascade, and documents the parallel-marginal cascade
by which the solver turns the analytic rate equations into an
event-by-event sampler on the discrete rovibronic state space.

Landau-Teller rate equation
---------------------------

Under the assumption of an exponential decay of correlations, the
mean energy :math:`\langle E_k\rangle` of internal mode :math:`k`
approaches its equilibrium value :math:`\langle E_k\rangle(T_T)`
at the translational temperature :math:`T_T` according to

.. math::

   \frac{\mathrm{d}\,\langle E_k\rangle}{\mathrm{d}t}
     = \frac{\langle E_k\rangle(T_T) - \langle E_k\rangle(t)}
            {\tau_k},

where :math:`\tau_k` is the relaxation time of mode :math:`k`.
The Landau-Teller form

.. math::

   \tau_k = Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}\,\tau_c,

with :math:`\tau_c = 1/\nu_{\mathrm{coll}}` the mean collision
time, relates the continuum-level relaxation time to a
**collision number** :math:`Z_k^{\mathrm{cont}}`, i.e. the mean
number of collisions required to equilibrate the mode. Under
isothermal heat-bath conditions the equation admits the explicit
solution

.. math::

   \langle E_k\rangle(t)
     = \langle E_k\rangle(T_T)
       - \bigl[\langle E_k\rangle(T_T) - \langle E_k\rangle(0)\bigr]\,
         \exp\!\left(-\frac{t}{\tau_k}\right),

which is the reference curve against which the solver is
validated (:doc:`../examples/relaxation_landau_teller`).

Multi-mode decomposition
------------------------

When several internal modes relax on well-separated timescales
:math:`\tau_R \ll \tau_{RV} \ll \tau_{RVE}`, the total internal
energy approaches equilibrium as a sum of exponentials:

.. math::

   \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t)
     = \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star}
     - \Delta E_R\, e^{-t/\tau_R}
     - \Delta E_V\, e^{-t/\tau_{RV}}
     - \Delta E_E\, e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}},

where :math:`\Delta E_k` is the initial energy deficit of mode
:math:`k`. In normalised form,

.. math::

   \frac{\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star}
         - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(t)}
        {\langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle^{\star}
         - \langle E_{\mathrm{int}}\rangle(0)}
     = A_R\, e^{-t/\tau_R}
     + A_V\, e^{-t/\tau_{RV}}
     + A_E\, e^{-t/\tau_{RVE}},

with :math:`A_k = \Delta E_k / \Delta E_{\mathrm{tot}}` and
:math:`A_R + A_V + A_E = 1`.

Millikan-White and Parker correlations
--------------------------------------

The rotational collision number :math:`Z_r(T)` is fitted, in the
manner of Parker [Parker1959rel]_, to measured rotational-
translational thermalisation data:

.. math::

   Z_r(T)
     = \frac{Z_r^{\infty}}{1 + (\pi^{3/2}/2)\,\sqrt{\theta_r^{\star}/T}
                           + (\pi + \pi^{2}/4)\,(\theta_r^{\star}/T)},

with :math:`\theta_r^{\star}` a reference rotational temperature
and :math:`Z_r^{\infty}` an asymptotic value that is typically
fitted to 5–10 for air diatomics. In the DSMC implementation the
low-temperature asymptote is tabulated directly as
``rotationalRelaxationCollisionNumber`` in the
``<Model>Coeffs`` sub-dictionary.

The vibrational relaxation time follows the Millikan-White
correlation [MillikanWhite1963rel]_:

.. math::

   p\,\tau_v^{\mathrm{MW}}(T)
     = \exp\!\left[A\,\bigl(T^{-1/3} - B\bigr) - 18.42\right],
   \qquad \text{[atm}\cdot\text{s]},

with :math:`A = 1.16 \times 10^{-3}\,\mu^{1/2}\,\theta_v^{4/3}`
and :math:`B = 0.015\,\mu^{1/4}`, the reduced mass :math:`\mu` in
atomic units, and the characteristic vibrational temperature
:math:`\theta_v` in kelvin. Park's high-temperature correction,
:math:`\tau_v^{\mathrm{Park}} = \tau_v^{\mathrm{MW}} +
1/(\sigma_v\,c_s\,n_s)`, brings the relaxation rate under control
above :math:`10^{4}~\mathrm{K}`, where the Millikan-White
exponential extrapolation would otherwise produce unphysically
fast relaxation. In the DSMC implementation, the effective
vibrational collision number
``rovibrationalRelaxationCollisionNumber`` absorbs the combined
Millikan-White/Park relation, and the database-derived
:math:`\tau_v(T)` is checked against the input at solver
initialisation.

Electronic relaxation times are less well characterised
experimentally than rotational or vibrational ones, and the
effective ``rovibronicRelaxationCollisionNumber`` in
``<Model>Coeffs`` is conventionally set to a large value
(:math:`10^{3}\text{–}10^{4}`) that reflects the slow equilibration
typical of atomic emission observations. Setting it to
:math:`\infty` amounts to freezing the electronic populations —
useful diagnostically, but physically justified only in short
transient cases.

Composite modes in the parallel-marginal cascade
-------------------------------------------------

Rather than redistributing energy independently between each
internal mode and the translational pool, the solver groups the
relaxation events into three composite modes organised by the
sub-space of the rovibronic state that they sample:

* mode :math:`R` (rotational-only) samples :math:`J` at fixed
  :math:`(n, v)`; the mode energy is measured relative to the
  floor :math:`E_R^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, v, 0)`;
* mode :math:`RV` (rovibrational) samples :math:`(v, J)` at fixed
  :math:`n`; floor :math:`E_{RV}^{\mathrm{floor}} = E(n, 0, 0)`;
* mode :math:`RVE` (rovibronic) samples :math:`(n, v, J)` on the
  full table; floor :math:`E_{RVE}^{\mathrm{floor}} = 0`.

Each composite mode carries its own effective degrees of
freedom, computed from the sub-space partition function,

.. math::

   \xi_k(T)
     = \frac{2\,\langle E_k\rangle(T)}{k_B\,T},

and these :math:`\xi_k(T)` feed the Larsen-Borgnakke exponent
(:doc:`larsen_borgnakke`).

Event selection
---------------

On each accepted collision the solver draws a single uniform
:math:`R \sim \mathcal{U}(0, 1)` and selects one of four
**mutually exclusive** events:

.. math::

   \begin{aligned}
   P_1 &= \frac{1}{Z_r\,C_r}, \\
   P_2 &= \frac{1}{Z_{rv}\,C_{rv}}, \\
   P_3 &= \frac{1}{Z_{rve}\,C_{rve}}, \\
   P_0 &= 1 - P_1 - P_2 - P_3,
   \end{aligned}

where event :math:`E_k` (for :math:`k = 1, 2, 3`) triggers
Larsen-Borgnakke sampling in the corresponding composite mode,
and :math:`E_0` leaves the internal state unchanged (elastic-like
event). When :math:`P_1 + P_2 + P_3 > 1` the probabilities are
rescaled to saturate, and a warning is emitted in the solver log:
the effective collision numbers seen by the simulation then
differ from the input values.

Nizenkov correction
-------------------

The collision numbers :math:`Z_k` are defined at the continuum
level, while the cascade acts at the event level. The two are
related by a temperature-dependent correction factor derived by
Nizenkov [Pfeiffer2016rel]_:

.. math::

   C_k
     = \frac{\xi_T\,(T_T - T'_k)}
            {\xi_k(T_T)\,T_T - \xi_k(T_k)\,T_k},

where :math:`\xi_T` is the translational DOF entering the
exchange, and :math:`T'_k` is the pool temperature that
satisfies local energy conservation in the cascade event. Without
the :math:`C_k` factors, the macroscopic relaxation observed by
the solver drifts systematically from the analytical
Landau-Teller solution; with them in place, the ratio
:math:`Z_{k,\mathrm{fit}}/Z_k` recovers the input to within a few
percent on the validation cases. A derivation of
:math:`C_k` is given in ``doc/LT_Correction_MultiMode.pdf``.

Phase-by-phase validation protocol
----------------------------------

The cascade is validated phase by phase by sending the collision
numbers of all but one mode to a very large value,
:math:`Z_j \to 10^{9}` for :math:`j \ne k`, and comparing the
observed relaxation curve against the analytical single-mode LT
solution.

* **Phase 1** —  rotational only: freeze
  :math:`Z_{rv}, Z_{rve}`. Initial state :math:`T_T =
  10^{4}~\mathrm{K}`, :math:`T_R(0) = 10^{3}~\mathrm{K}`. The
  fitted :math:`Z_r` should match the input within 5%. At the
  time of writing the ratio is :math:`Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z \approx
  1.07`, acceptable.
* **Phase 2** —  rovibrational: freeze :math:`Z_r, Z_{rve}`.
  Expected: rotational sub-ladder equilibrates near
  instantaneously (because rotational events are frozen, the
  cascade has only one active mode), followed by vibrational
  relaxation. Current ratio :math:`\approx 0.83`; the phase
  overcorrects and is under investigation.
* **Phase 3** —  all modes active. Expected triple-exponential
  decay; ratio pending diagnosis.

On the absence of the classic ZCorr
-----------------------------------

The continuous-variable implementations of Larsen-Borgnakke
require the so-called ZCorr correction to match the macroscopic
Landau-Teller rate (since, in the continuous formulation, the
effective DOF enter the exponent while the sampled population is
unphysically concentrated near the mean). In the rovibronic
extension the sampled population is the *actual* quantum
distribution, and a direct measurement
[Civrais2024Vib_rel]_ of :math:`Z_{\mathrm{fit}}/Z_{\mathrm{input}}`
returns :math:`\approx 0.99` without any correction factor,
confirming that **ZCorr is not required** on the rovibronic
Larsen-Borgnakke path. The relevant diagnostic history is
tracked in the in-repo bug-audit report.
