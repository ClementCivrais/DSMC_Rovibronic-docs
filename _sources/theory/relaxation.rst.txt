Energy Relaxation
=================

Internal relaxation drives the molecular internal state toward
equilibrium with the local translational temperature.

Landau–Teller enforcement
-------------------------

In Landau–Teller mode, relaxation events are sampled with probability

.. math::

   P = \frac{\Delta t}{\tau(T)},

where :math:`\tau(T)` is the species-pair relaxation time
(Millikan–White or curve-fitted to state-to-state master-equation
solutions). The implementation correctly accounts for:

* Multi-mode relaxation when multiple internal degrees of freedom are
  active (see ``doc/LT_Correction_MultiMode.pdf``).
* Multi-exponential :math:`\tau(T)` fits
  (``doc/LandauTeller_MultiExponential_Derivation.pdf``).
* The ZCorr correction where applicable — the
  :math:`Z_{\text{fit}}/Z_{\text{input}}` ratio was measured to
  :math:`\approx 0.99` for rovibronic LB without the correction,
  confirming that ZCorr is not needed in the rovibronic path.

State-to-state relaxation
-------------------------

When ``relaxationScheme`` selects the state-to-state model,
transitions are sampled directly from per-pair rate tables rather
than through an effective :math:`\tau`. This is the most expensive
but most faithful choice for strong non-equilibrium.

-------------------------

The coupled vibrational–rotational relaxation operator is derived in
