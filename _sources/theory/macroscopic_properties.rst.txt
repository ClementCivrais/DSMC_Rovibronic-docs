Macroscopic properties
======================

Volume- and time-averaged moments of the simulator-particle
distribution constitute the macroscopic output of the solver.
The present section defines, for each field, the estimator used
by the solver, the inversion from sampled moments to effective
temperatures, and the corresponding transport coefficients that
close the rarefied continuum description.

Number density and species fractions
------------------------------------

For a cell of volume :math:`V_{\mathrm{cell}}` containing
:math:`N_s` simulator parcels of species :math:`s`, each
representing :math:`F_N` real molecules,

.. math::

   n_s = \frac{N_s\, F_N}{V_{\mathrm{cell}}}, \qquad
   \rho_s = m_s\, n_s, \qquad
   x_s = \frac{n_s}{\displaystyle \sum_{s'} n_{s'}}.

Bulk velocity and peculiar velocity
-----------------------------------

The bulk velocity and the peculiar velocity of a parcel follow
from the mass-weighted moments:

.. math::

   \bar{\mathbf{v}}
     = \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{i}
             m_i\,\mathbf{v}_i}
            {\displaystyle\sum_{i} m_i},
   \qquad
   \mathbf{c}_i = \mathbf{v}_i - \bar{\mathbf{v}}.

Translational temperature
-------------------------

The translational temperature is extracted, as in standard DSMC,
from the second central moment of the velocity,

.. math::

   \tfrac{3}{2}\, k_B\, T_T
     = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}
       \tfrac{1}{2}\,m_i\,c_i^{2}.

In strongly anisotropic flows (boundary layers, shocks), the
directional temperatures

.. math::

   k_B\,T_{x}
     = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i m_i\,c_{i,x}^{2},
   \qquad\text{etc.,}

are available from the same cell accumulators and are useful
diagnostics of the onset of translational non-equilibrium.

Stress tensor and heat flux
---------------------------

The translational stress tensor and heat flux are

.. math::

   \mathbf{P} = \sum_{s} \left\langle
     \rho_s\,\mathbf{c}\,\mathbf{c}
   \right\rangle,
   \qquad
   \mathbf{q} = \sum_{s} \left\langle
     \tfrac{1}{2}\,\rho_s\,|\mathbf{c}|^{2}\,\mathbf{c}
     + n_s\,E_s^{\mathrm{int}}\,\mathbf{c}
   \right\rangle,

with :math:`\langle\cdot\rangle` the cell average and
:math:`E_s^{\mathrm{int}}` the internal energy carried by each
parcel of species :math:`s`. The pressure scalar is
:math:`p = \tfrac{1}{3}\,\mathrm{tr}\,\mathbf{P}`, and the
deviatoric stress is :math:`\boldsymbol{\tau} = \mathbf{P} -
p\,\mathbf{I}`. Both quantities are written to the time
directories when ``measureHeatFluxShearStress`` is enabled on the
``dsmcVolFields`` field model (see :doc:`../classes/measurements`).

Internal-mode effective temperatures
------------------------------------

For each composite internal mode
:math:`k \in \{R, V, E\}`, the solver first averages the
per-parcel mode energy over the cell,

.. math::

   \langle E_k\rangle
     = \frac{1}{N_s}
       \sum_i E_k^{(i)},

and then inverts the pre-tabulated equilibrium relation
:math:`\langle E_k\rangle(T_k)` by bisection to obtain the
effective mode temperature :math:`T_k`. The bisection uses the
internal partition function of :doc:`rovibronic_levels`, which
covers the simulation temperature range at solver start-up; if
the sampled mode energy lies outside the tabulated range, the
bisection extrapolates and a warning is printed.

The effective DOF of each mode follow immediately,

.. math::

   \xi_k(T_k) = \frac{2\,\langle E_k\rangle(T_k)}{k_B\,T_k},

and are the same quantities that enter the Larsen-Borgnakke
exponent (:doc:`larsen_borgnakke`).

Under strong non-equilibrium these :math:`T_k` are **cell-level
effective temperatures** rather than thermodynamic temperatures.
They reproduce the first moment of the population distribution
but do not characterise the distribution as a whole. Any
downstream coupling that assumes a Boltzmann shape (radiation
transport, inter-code coupling) should revisit that assumption
against the population diagnostics below.

Mean free path and Mach number
------------------------------

Derived fields exposed by the ``dsmcVolFields`` field model
include:

.. math::

   \lambda
     = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}\,\pi\,n\,d^{2}}\,
       \left(\frac{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}{T}\right)^{\omega - 1/2},
   \qquad
   \mathrm{Ma}
     = \frac{|\bar{\mathbf{v}}|}
            {\sqrt{\gamma\,k_B\,T_T/m}},

which are needed to verify the DSMC convergence conditions
*a posteriori* and to position the flow within the Knudsen
classification of :doc:`foundations`.

Transport coefficients from VSS parameters
------------------------------------------

In the free-molecular-to-transitional regime, the VSS parameters
:math:`(d_{\mathrm{ref}}, \omega, \alpha)` in
:doc:`../classes/species_database` determine the transport
coefficients that close the continuum description. The viscosity,

.. math::

   \eta(T)
     = \frac{5}{16}\,\frac{(\pi\, m\, k_B T)^{1/2}}
                          {\pi\,d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}}\,
       \frac{(1 - \alpha/2)\,\alpha}
            {\Gamma(9/2 - \omega)}\,
       \left(\frac{T}{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)^{\omega},

and the thermal conductivity,

.. math::

   \kappa(T)
     = \frac{15\,k_B}{4\,m}\,\eta(T),

follow directly and recover the empirical
:math:`T^{\omega}` scaling by construction. The resulting
Prandtl number :math:`\mathrm{Pr} = \mu c_p/\kappa = 2/3` is the
standard value for a monatomic VSS gas; deviations in polyatomic
species are captured through the rovibronic internal DOF of
:doc:`rovibronic_levels`.

Population diagnostics
----------------------

Beyond the scalar fields above, the
``dsmcRovibronicPopulation`` field model
(:doc:`../classes/measurements`) accumulates the full four-index
array :math:`P(\mathrm{cell}; n, v, J)` for a selected species
inside a selected zone. The output of this diagnostic is the
only observable that directly reflects the central promise of
the rovibronic extension: it shows the distribution as the
simulation actually carries it, rather than the Boltzmann
projection that the effective temperatures report. Typical
uses include:

* plotting the vibrational population :math:`P(v)` at fixed
  electronic state and comparing it against the Boltzmann
  distribution at :math:`T_T`;
* monitoring the population of the first few excited electronic
  levels as a function of time in a shock-heated flow;
* constructing rotational-vibrational distributions to assess
  the assumption of rotational-vibrational equilibrium under
  non-equilibrium chemistry.

Bin, zone, and face-flux outputs
--------------------------------

The :doc:`../classes/measurements` page catalogues the
configurable field models that complement the per-cell
``dsmcVolFields`` output: one-, two-, and three-dimensional bin
histograms (``dsmcBinsMethod``) for mesh-independent profiles,
zone averages (``dsmcZone``) for zero-dimensional reference
quantities, velocity and speed distributions
(``dsmcVelocityDistributionZone``,
``dsmcSpeedDistributionZone``), and flux integrals through named
face zones (``dsmcMassFluxSurface``). The present page
concentrates on the underlying per-cell estimators; the
configurable layer is documented in full on the class page.
