Macroscopic Properties
======================

Volume-averaged fields are accumulated from the simulator particles
in each computational cell. Below we summarise how the common
diagnostics are defined in the rovibronic setting, and we emphasise
the status of the per-mode "temperatures" as effective scalars
extracted from population distributions rather than as primary
thermodynamic variables.

Number density and species fractions
------------------------------------

For a cell of volume :math:`V_{\mathrm{cell}}` containing
:math:`N_s` simulator particles of species :math:`s`, each
representing :math:`w = \mathtt{nEquivalentParticles}` real
molecules,

.. math::

   n_s = \frac{N_s\, w}{V_{\mathrm{cell}}}, \qquad
   x_s = \frac{n_s}{\displaystyle\sum_{s'} n_{s'}}.

Translational temperature
-------------------------

The translational temperature is extracted, as in standard DSMC,
from the second moment of the peculiar velocity,

.. math::

   \frac{3}{2}\, k_B T_T
     = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N}
       \tfrac{1}{2}\, m_i\,(\mathbf{v}_i - \bar{\mathbf{v}})^{2}.

Rotational, vibrational, and electronic temperatures
----------------------------------------------------

For each composite internal mode :math:`k \in \{R,\,V,\,E\}`, we
first average the mode-specific energy over the cell,
:math:`\langle E_k\rangle = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i E_k^{(i)}`, and then
invert the (pre-tabulated) equilibrium relation
:math:`\langle E_k\rangle(T_k)` by bisection to obtain the effective
temperature :math:`T_k`. The associated effective DOF is

.. math::

   \xi_k(T_k) = \frac{2\,\langle E_k\rangle(T_k)}{k_B\,T_k},

which matches the definition used in the Landau-Teller cascade (see
:doc:`relaxation`).

Status of effective temperatures under non-equilibrium
``````````````````````````````````````````````````````

It is important to emphasise that, under strong non-equilibrium —
for instance in the early stages of a shock-heated flow, or in a
rapidly expanding nozzle —  the quantities :math:`T_R`,
:math:`T_V`, :math:`T_E` are **cell-level effective temperatures**
rather than thermodynamic temperatures. They correctly describe the
first moment of the population distribution but do not characterise
the distribution as a whole, which in general is strongly
non-Boltzmann. The physical solution resides in the state-resolved
population vector :math:`P(n, v, J)` itself, and any downstream
coupling that assumes a Boltzmann shape (e.g. coupling with a
radiation transport solver) must be revisited accordingly
[Civrais2024Electronic_m]_.

Population diagnostics
----------------------

Per-cell histograms over :math:`(n, v, J)` are available from the
post-processing utilities under ``applications/utilities/``. Typical
use cases include:

* the vibrational population :math:`P(v)` at fixed electronic state
  :math:`n`, plotted against the Boltzmann distribution at
  :math:`T_T` (a direct diagnostic of vibrational non-equilibrium);
* the rotational population :math:`P(J)` at fixed :math:`(n, v)`
  to assess the assumption of rotational equilibrium;
* the electronic population :math:`P(n)` as a function of time in
  shock-heated flows, to monitor the rate at which the excited
  electronic manifolds become populated.

.. [Civrais2024Electronic_m] C. H. B. Civrais, M. Pfeiffer, C. White,
   and R. Steijl, "Modelling of the electronic excited states in
   high-temperature flows," *Physics of Fluids* (2024).
