Adaptation and controllers
==========================

The present section documents the two families of active agents
in the solver that modify the simulation state at run-time: the
adaptation layer, which reshapes the sub-cell and parcel-weight
resolution without touching the mesh, and the controllers, which
drive a target macroscopic quantity toward a prescribed value.
Both are orthogonal to the collision, chemistry, and measurement
layers, and can be combined at will.

.. note::

   The solver implements three adaptation mechanisms (sub-cell
   subdivision, cell weighting, and time-step adaptation)
   described below. **Geometric adaptive mesh refinement**, in
   which the mesh itself is split or merged at run-time, is
   **not implemented yet** —  the sub-cell mechanism is the
   intended substitute (see the *Relationship with mesh-level
   AMR* note at the end of this page).

Sub-cell adaptation
-------------------

The DSMC convergence condition :math:`\Delta x < \lambda/3`
cannot in general be met everywhere by a fixed mesh, since the
mean free path varies by orders of magnitude across a hypersonic
flow. The sub-cell machinery keeps the mesh fixed and instead
subdivides each cell, per coordinate direction, into
:math:`L_i` sub-cells of size :math:`\Delta x_i /
L_i`, where :math:`\Delta x_i` is the parent cell size. Collision
partner selection (:doc:`collision_sampling`) is then restricted
to particles within the same sub-cell, tightening the effective
discretisation where it matters.

The per-cell vector :math:`\mathbf{L}(\mathbf{x})` is recomputed
at the adaptation interval so that

.. math::

   \frac{\Delta x_i(\mathbf{x})}{L_i(\mathbf{x})}
     \lesssim r_{\lambda}\,\lambda(\mathbf{x}),

with :math:`r_{\lambda}` the prescribed
``maxSubCellSizeMFPRatio`` (default :math:`1/3`) and
:math:`\lambda(\mathbf{x})` the local mean free path. The bounds
``minSubCellLevels`` and ``maxSubCellLevels`` in the
``dynamicAdaptationProperties`` sub-dictionary cap the
adaptation so that runaway refinement in unresolved regions does
not collapse the per-sub-cell particle count below an
operational minimum.

Time-step adaptation
--------------------

The second convergence condition, :math:`\Delta t < \tau_c/3`,
is enforced through the ``timeStepAdaptation`` switch. At each
adaptation event the solver computes the Courant number

.. math::

   \mathrm{Co}(\mathbf{x})
     = \frac{|\bar{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{x})|\,\Delta t}
            {\min_i \Delta x_i(\mathbf{x}) / L_i(\mathbf{x})}

and the ratio of the global time-step to the local mean
collision time,

.. math::

   r_{\mathrm{MCT}}(\mathbf{x})
     = \frac{\Delta t}{\tau_c(\mathbf{x})},

and rescales :math:`\Delta t` so that both quantities fall
below the user-supplied bounds ``maxCourantNumber`` and
``maxTimeStepMCTRatio``. The rescaling is monotonic across
adaptation events to avoid oscillations.

Cell-weight adaptation
----------------------

The statistical resolution condition
:math:`N_{\mathrm{cell}} \gtrsim 10\text{–}20` is enforced
through the ``cellWeightedSimulation`` switch. Each cell carries
an effective parcel weight

.. math::

   F_N^{\mathrm{cell}}(\mathbf{x})
     = F_N\,w(\mathbf{x}),

with :math:`w(\mathbf{x})` set so that the number of simulator
parcels per sub-cell meets the target ``particlesPerSubCell``,

.. math::

   w(\mathbf{x})
     = \frac{n(\mathbf{x})\,V_{\mathrm{subcell}}(\mathbf{x})}
            {F_N\,\mathtt{particlesPerSubCell}}.

Parcels that cross a cell boundary have their weight updated to
match the destination cell; the resulting mass, momentum, and
energy are preserved through a standard split-merge operation
that is invisible to the user. The ``minParticlesPerSubCell``
and ``smoothCellWeights`` keys respectively guard against
runaway weight collapses and smooth the weight field across
neighbouring cells to avoid ringing.

Controllers — active flow-state drivers
---------------------------------------

Controllers operate on a named ``cellZone`` and act at an
interval set in their sub-dictionary.

Temperature controller
``````````````````````

The ``temperatureController`` maintains a target translational
temperature :math:`T^{\star}` by rescaling the peculiar
velocities of the parcels in the zone:

.. math::

   \mathbf{c}_i \leftarrow
     \sqrt{\frac{T^{\star}}{T_T^{\mathrm{obs}}}}\,\mathbf{c}_i,

where :math:`T_T^{\mathrm{obs}}` is the observed translational
temperature averaged over the controller's sampling window. The
rescaling preserves the bulk velocity and the internal state
exactly; the translational temperature converges to
:math:`T^{\star}` on the controller interval.

Maxwellian resampling controller
````````````````````````````````

The ``maxwellianResamplingController`` performs a harder reset:
at each actuation it redraws the full velocity distribution of
the parcels in the zone from a Maxwellian at :math:`T^{\star}`.
The bulk velocity is preserved, but the peculiar-velocity
distribution is **completely replaced**, which is occasionally
useful as a hard boundary condition in equilibrium-approach
studies.

Density controller
``````````````````

The ``densityController`` maintains a target per-species number
density :math:`n_s^{\star}` by inserting new parcels (sampled
from a Maxwellian at the current translational temperature)
when the population is too low, or deleting parcels at random
when it is too high. The insertion/deletion is carried out at
the controller interval and is kept small so that the cell-
level statistics remain smooth.

Gravitational-acceleration controller
`````````````````````````````````````

The ``gravitationalAccelerationController`` applies a uniform
acceleration :math:`\mathbf{g}` to every parcel in the zone on
every DSMC step,

.. math::

   \mathbf{v}_i \leftarrow \mathbf{v}_i + \mathbf{g}\,\Delta t,

with :math:`\mathbf{g}` supplied through the ``acceleration``
vector entry in the controller's sub-dictionary. It is the
simplest possible body-force implementation and is adequate for
gravity and for constant-field drift in plasma studies.

Orchestration and regression implications
-----------------------------------------

All controllers and the adapter are orchestrated by the cloud
(:doc:`../classes/cloud`). They share the single cloud RNG
``rndGen_``, which is the key observation for the planned
regression-test infrastructure (see
:doc:`../guide/regression_tests`): controllers that sample
Maxwellians (temperature, Maxwellian resampling, density) or
decide on parcel insertion/deletion draw from the same stream
as the collision layer, so reproducibility at the collision
level suffices to reproduce the controller actions deterministically
under a fixed seed. Bit-identical reproduction in parallel is
frustrated only by the cell-to-rank ownership history, which is
itself a :doc:`../classes/load_balancing` concern.

Relationship with mesh-level AMR *(Not implemented yet)*
--------------------------------------------------------

Geometric adaptive mesh refinement, in which cells are actually
split or merged at run-time, is **not implemented** in the
rovibronic solver. The sub-cell mechanism described at the top
of this page is the intended substitute: it keeps the mesh
fixed but subdivides each cell at run-time along whichever
directions need tighter resolution, which is enough to satisfy
the :math:`\Delta x < \lambda/3` criterion without rebuilding
the parallel topology. A genuine AMR layer would require
significant additional infrastructure (parallel cell
connectivity updates, parcel rebalancing, and a refinement
criterion compatible with the DSMC collision loop) and is out
of scope for the current development cycle.
