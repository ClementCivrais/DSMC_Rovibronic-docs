Collision sampling
==================

Given a chosen cross-section model, the second half of the DSMC
collision step is to decide, in each cell, **how many** candidate
pairs to draw and **which** of them to accept. Four sampling
schemes are implemented in the solver. They are mathematically
equivalent in the limit of infinite particle count but differ in
statistical efficiency and in the way they handle collision-rate
disparities across species pairs. The scheme is selected via the
``collisionPartnerSelectionModel`` key in
``constant/dsmcProperties``.

No-Time-Counter (NTC)
---------------------

The No-Time-Counter scheme, introduced by Bird, is the default.
The per-cell candidate count over :math:`\Delta t` is

.. math::

   N_{\mathrm{cand}}
     = \frac{1}{2}\,
       \frac{N(N - 1)\, F_N\, (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\,\Delta t}
            {V_c},

where :math:`N` is the number of particles in the cell,
:math:`F_N` is the equivalent-molecules factor, :math:`V_c` is
the cell volume, and :math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` is a running
estimate of the maximum of
:math:`\sigma_T(g_{\mathrm{ref}})\, g`. Each candidate pair
:math:`(P, Q)` is accepted with probability

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{acc}} = \frac{\sigma_T(g)\, g}{(\sigma_T g)_{\max}},

which, combined with the candidate-generation formula above,
recovers the target collision rate

.. math::

   \nu_{\mathrm{coll}}
     = \frac{N(N - 1)\, F_N\,\langle\sigma_T g\rangle}
            {2\,V_c}

on expectation, with :math:`\langle \cdot \rangle` the ensemble
average over the cell distribution. The running maximum
:math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` is monotonically updated whenever a
candidate exceeds the current estimate, and is therefore
self-correcting.

*Validity.* NTC is unbiased in the limit of infinite particle
count per cell and in the limit of infinite time-steps; the
leading correction is :math:`O(1/N)` and is addressed by keeping
:math:`N \gtrsim 10\text{–}20` per cell. Its main limitation is
that the acceptance probability :math:`\sigma_T g /
(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` becomes small whenever the cross-section
distribution across species pairs spans several decades, which
the alternatives below are designed to address.

No-Time-Counter with sub-cycling
--------------------------------

When charged species are present, electron-electron and electron-
neutral collision rates routinely exceed heavy-species collision
rates by several orders of magnitude. A single DSMC time-step is
then either too coarse to resolve electron kinetics or too fine
to be practical for the rest of the flow. The
``noTimeCounterSubCycled`` variant performs :math:`n_{\mathrm{sc}}`
internal NTC sub-cycles on the electron population within each
global DSMC step:

.. math::

   \Delta t_{\mathrm{sc}}
     = \frac{\Delta t}{n_{\mathrm{sc}}},
   \qquad
   N_{\mathrm{cand}}^{\mathrm{sc}}
     = \frac{1}{2}\,
       \frac{N_e(N_e - 1)\, F_N\, (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\,
             \Delta t_{\mathrm{sc}}}{V_c}
       \times n_{\mathrm{sc}},

so that the total number of sampled electron collisions remains
unchanged but they are now distributed over :math:`n_{\mathrm{sc}}`
smaller steps. The key ``nSubCycles`` in the
``noTimeCounterSubCycledProperties`` sub-dictionary sets
:math:`n_{\mathrm{sc}}`.

Majorant Collision Frequency (MCF)
----------------------------------

The majorant collision frequency method addresses the acceptance-
rate inefficiency of NTC by replacing the global
:math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` bound by per-pair majorants. For each
candidate pair, an analytical upper bound
:math:`\hat{\sigma}_T(g)` is available that is tighter than the
global maximum; the acceptance probability then becomes

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{acc}}^{\mathrm{MCF}}
     = \frac{\sigma_T(g)\, g}{\hat{\sigma}_T(g_{\max})\, g_{\max}}.

The method is particularly effective in multi-species flows in
which a handful of pairs dominate the collision rate. The
implementation follows the particle-based formulation of Pfeiffer
et al. [Pfeiffer2019cs]_.

Simplified Bernoulli Trials (SBT)
---------------------------------

The SBT scheme of Stefanov replaces the NTC acceptance step by a
sequence of Bernoulli trials with per-pair probability

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{coll}}^{\mathrm{SBT}}
     = \frac{\sigma_T(g)\, g\, F_N\,\Delta t}{V_c / (N - 1)}.

Each particle is tested exactly once against a randomly chosen
partner, and the global estimate :math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` is
not needed. SBT is closer in spirit to the original Nanbu
algorithm and is occasionally preferable in low-particle-per-cell
regimes, at the cost of a slightly different statistical bias
profile at small :math:`N`.

Sub-cell resolution of collision partners
-----------------------------------------

Whichever sampling scheme is chosen, accepted pairs may be
restricted to particles within the **same sub-cell** of the
parent cell when the adaptation machinery
(:doc:`adaptation_controllers`) is enabled. This is the solver's
principal mechanism for tightening the :math:`\Delta x <
\lambda/3` condition in regions of locally steep gradients, and
it is applied transparently to the scheme above: the partner
pool in the formulae is reduced to the sub-cell inventory, while
the sampling logic is otherwise unchanged.

Choosing between schemes
------------------------

In practice:

* use ``noTimeCounter`` for general-purpose DSMC work; it has
  been exercised on every validation case in the project;
* use ``noTimeCounterSubCycled`` when ``chargedParticles`` is
  enabled and electron-electron or electron-neutral collision
  rates are expected to dominate the global DSMC time-step;
* consider ``majorantCollisionFrequency`` when the cross-section
  distribution across species pairs spans several decades;
* ``simplifiedBernoulliTrials`` is primarily of pedagogical
  interest in this implementation and is not recommended as a
  default.
