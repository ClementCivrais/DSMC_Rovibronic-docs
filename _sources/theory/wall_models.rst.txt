Wall and inflow models
======================

The present section documents the physical models attached to
domain boundaries. Four families are distinguished —  specular,
diffuse, Cercignani-Lampis-Lord, and rovibronic-aware —
together with the accommodation coefficients and the treatment of
the internal degrees of freedom at reflection.

Specular reflection
-------------------

A specular wall reverses the velocity component normal to the
patch face and leaves the tangential components unchanged,

.. math::

   \mathbf{v}' = \mathbf{v} - 2\,(\mathbf{v}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}})\,
                 \hat{\mathbf{n}},

with :math:`\hat{\mathbf{n}}` the outward face normal. The
internal state of the parcel is unaffected, and no momentum,
energy, or mass is exchanged with the wall. Specular walls are
primarily of use in symmetry planes, periodic surrogates, and
free-molecular verification.

Fully diffuse reflection
------------------------

The Maxwellian diffuse wall thermalises the reflected parcel
with the wall. After reflection, the velocity is drawn from the
half-Maxwellian at the wall temperature :math:`T_w`,

.. math::

   f_w(\mathbf{v}) \propto
     (\mathbf{v}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}_w)\;
     \exp\!\left[-\frac{m\,|\mathbf{v} - \mathbf{v}_w|^{2}}
                      {2\,k_B\,T_w}\right],
   \qquad \mathbf{v}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}_w > 0,

with :math:`\mathbf{v}_w` the (usually zero) wall velocity and
:math:`\hat{\mathbf{n}}_w` the inward-pointing face normal. In
the continuous-internal-energy implementation the internal
energy is redrawn from a Boltzmann distribution at :math:`T_w`;
the rovibronic-aware variant discussed below redraws the
quantum state :math:`(n, v, J)` instead.

Mixed diffuse / specular
------------------------

The Maxwell mixed model introduces an accommodation coefficient
:math:`\alpha \in [0, 1]`: a fraction :math:`\alpha` of incoming
parcels reflect diffusely, the remainder specularly. In the
implemented variant ``dsmcMixedDiffuseSpecularWallPatch``,
:math:`\alpha` is a single scalar; the field variant
``dsmcMixedDiffuseSpecularWallFieldPatch`` allows :math:`\alpha`
and :math:`T_w` to vary patch face by patch face.

Cercignani-Lampis-Lord (CLL) scattering
---------------------------------------

The CLL model [Lord1991wa]_ offers an anisotropic alternative to
the fully-diffuse Maxwellian kernel, with two accommodation
coefficients :math:`\alpha_n` and :math:`\alpha_t` for the
normal and tangential components of the momentum. Writing
:math:`V_n, V_t` for the normal and tangential velocity
magnitudes scaled by :math:`\sqrt{2 k_B T_w / m}`, the CLL
kernel samples post-collision :math:`V_n', V_t'` from

.. math::

   V_n' = \sqrt{(1 - \alpha_n)\,V_n^{2}
                + 2\,\sqrt{(1 - \alpha_n)\,\alpha_n}\,V_n\,V_r\cos\theta
                + \alpha_n\,V_r^{2}},

.. math::

   V_t' = (1 - \alpha_t)\,V_t + \sqrt{\alpha_t\,(2 - \alpha_t)}\,V_r',

with :math:`V_r, V_r'` Maxwell-Boltzmann-distributed thermal
speeds and :math:`\theta` uniform on :math:`[0, 2\pi)`. The two
limits :math:`\alpha_n = \alpha_t = 0` (specular) and
:math:`\alpha_n = \alpha_t = 1` (fully diffuse) are recovered
from the same kernel.

Rovibronic-aware wall: ``dsmcMixedDiffuseSpecularWallRotPatch``
----------------------------------------------------------------

The rovibronic-aware wall is the only boundary class that samples
on the quantum-state space at reflection. It behaves like the
Maxwell mixed model for the velocity, but replaces the internal-
energy redistribution by a Boltzmann draw over the species'
rovibronic levels:

.. math::

   P(n, v, J)
     = \frac{g(n, v, J)\,\exp\!\bigl[-E(n, v, J)/k_B\,T_w\bigr]}
            {\displaystyle\sum_{n',v',J'}
              g(n', v', J')\,\exp\!\bigl[-E(n', v', J')/k_B\,T_w\bigr]},

with :math:`T_w` the wall temperature. The partition function in
the denominator is the full internal partition function of
:doc:`rovibronic_levels`. This is the right choice for flows in
which the internal populations impinging on the wall carry
information (radiation, catalysis, non-equilibrium boundary
layers) and must be equilibrated with the wall on reflection.

Free-stream inflow
------------------

The free-stream inflow patch injects parcels drawn from a
Maxwellian distribution at prescribed density :math:`n_\infty`,
temperature :math:`T_\infty`, and drift velocity
:math:`\mathbf{v}_\infty`. The number of particles entering the
domain over a patch face of area :math:`A_f` during
:math:`\Delta t` follows from the half-range flux integral:

.. math::

   \dot{N}_{\mathrm{in}}
     = \frac{n_\infty\,A_f\,\Delta t}{\sqrt{2\pi\,m/k_B T_\infty}}\,
       \bigl[\exp(-s^{2}) + \sqrt{\pi}\,s\,(1 + \mathrm{erf}\,s)\bigr],

with :math:`s = (\mathbf{v}_\infty\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}_w) /
\sqrt{2 k_B T_\infty/m}` the speed ratio. Sampled velocities
follow the acceptance-rejection construction of Bird.

Chapman-Enskog inflow
---------------------

The ``dsmcChapmanEnskogFreeStreamInflowPatch`` extends the
Maxwellian inflow with a first-order Chapman-Enskog correction
accounting for a non-zero stress and heat flux at the patch
face,

.. math::

   f(\mathbf{v}) = f_{\mathrm{MB}}(\mathbf{v})\,
                   \Bigl[1 + \tfrac{2}{5}\,\tilde{\mathbf{q}}\cdot\mathbf{c}\,
                              (\mathbf{c}^{2} - 5/2)
                       + \tilde{\boldsymbol{\tau}} : \mathbf{c}\,\mathbf{c}
                   \Bigr],

with :math:`\mathbf{c}` the peculiar velocity in units of the
most-probable speed, and :math:`\tilde{\mathbf{q}},
\tilde{\boldsymbol{\tau}}` the scaled heat-flux and stress
tensors. This is the natural inflow when coupling DSMC to a
continuum upstream, where non-zero Navier-Stokes-like
first-order moments characterise the state of the gas.

Pressure and mass-flow inlets
-----------------------------

Pressure- and mass-flow-driven inlets
(``dsmcWangPressureInlet``, ``dsmcLiouFangPressureInlet``,
``dsmcMassFlowRateInlet``) apply Maxwellian sampling at an
inferred density chosen so that the desired pressure or the
desired mass flow rate is respected in the long-time average.
They are the natural choice when the upstream reservoir is
better characterised through :math:`p_0` or :math:`\dot{m}`
than through :math:`n_\infty`.

Particle deletion
-----------------

Parcels that reach a ``dsmcDeletionPatch`` are removed from the
simulation. The patch supports either the ``allSpecies`` switch
or a ``typeIds`` list, so that outflow patches may be made
species-selective when required.

Cyclic boundaries
-----------------

The ``dsmcCyclicBoundaries`` block in ``boundariesDict`` supports
periodic pairing. The
``dsmcReflectiveParticleMembranePatch`` variant reflects a
user-specified fraction of incoming parcels back into the domain
while letting the rest pass through; this is the mechanism used,
for instance, to model permeable membranes in micro-nano-flow
applications.

   Cercignani-Lampis gas-surface scattering kernel," *Physics
   of Fluids A*, **3** (4), 706–710 (1991).
