Theory guide
============

The present guide gives a comprehensive account of the physics and
numerics implemented in the solver, with the goal of mapping every
capability of the code to its mathematical foundation. Each page
may be read as a stand-alone technical note and includes
derivations at a level of detail sufficient to verify, modify, or
extend the corresponding implementation.

.. note::

   **Convention.** Sections that describe a method documented
   here for completeness but **not available in the current
   build** are flagged with the marker **(Not implemented yet)**
   in their heading. Classes flagged elsewhere as *work in
   progress* (e.g. the charged-species reactions of
   :doc:`chemistry_charged`) are implemented but not yet
   verified and validated; they are distinguished from
   not-implemented methods by their own dedicated warnings.
