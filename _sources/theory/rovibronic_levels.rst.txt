Rovibronic level structure
==========================

The rovibronic extension replaces the continuous internal energy
usually carried on a DSMC particle by an indexed triple
:math:`(n, v, J)` that selects one level out of a three-dimensional
energy table. The present section documents how that table is
built, from the Born-Oppenheimer separation of electronic and
nuclear motion through to the centrifugal-barrier cut-off, and
how the associated degeneracies and partition functions are
evaluated.

Born-Oppenheimer separation
---------------------------

The total Hamiltonian of a diatomic molecule in the centre-of-mass
frame reads

.. math::

   \hat{H}
     = \hat{T}_{N} + \hat{T}_{e} + \hat{V}_{eN}
       + \hat{V}_{ee} + \hat{V}_{NN},

where :math:`\hat{T}_{N}` and :math:`\hat{T}_{e}` are the kinetic
energies of the nuclei and electrons respectively, and the
remaining terms collect the Coulombic interactions. Because the
electrons are much lighter than the nuclei
(:math:`m_e/m_N \sim 10^{-4}`), the Born-Oppenheimer approximation
separates the problem into a fast electronic sub-problem, solved
at fixed nuclear positions and yielding a potential-energy
surface (PES) :math:`V_n(R)` for each electronic state :math:`n`,
and a slow nuclear sub-problem that evolves on that surface:

.. math::

   \Bigl[-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2\mu}\frac{1}{R^{2}}
         \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}R}
         \left(R^{2}\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}R}\right)
         + \frac{\hbar^{2}\, J(J+1)}{2\mu R^{2}}
         + V_n(R)\Bigr]\,\chi_{nvJ}(R)
     = E_{nvJ}\,\chi_{nvJ}(R),

where :math:`\mu` is the reduced mass,
:math:`\hbar^{2}J(J+1)/(2\mu R^{2})` is the centrifugal
contribution, and :math:`\chi_{nvJ}(R)` is the radial vibrational
wave-function. The total internal energy is approximated as a sum,

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J)
     = T_e^{(n)} + E_v^{(n)}(v) + E_r^{(n, v)}(J),

where :math:`T_e^{(n)}` is the electronic term energy of state
:math:`n` (the minimum of :math:`V_n`), :math:`E_v^{(n)}` is the
vibrational term in electronic level :math:`n`, and
:math:`E_r^{(n,v)}` is the rotational term in the :math:`(n, v)`
manifold.

Vibrational ladder — Morse oscillator
-------------------------------------

A widely used and tractable model of the molecular PES is the
Morse potential

.. math::

   V_{\mathrm{M}}(R)
     = D_e\,\bigl[1 - e^{-a(R - R_e)}\bigr]^{2},

with dissociation depth :math:`D_e`, equilibrium bond length
:math:`R_e`, and range parameter :math:`a`. The Morse potential
has the distinctive feature of admitting an **analytical
solution** of the non-rotating radial Schrödinger equation, whose
eigenvalues are

.. math::

   E_v^{(n)}(v)
     = hc\left[\omega_e^{(n)}\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)
               - \omega_e x_e^{(n)}
                \left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{2}\right],

with the spectroscopic constants :math:`\omega_e^{(n)}` (harmonic
frequency) and :math:`\omega_e x_e^{(n)}` (anharmonicity)
related to the Morse parameters through
:math:`\omega_e = a\sqrt{2D_e/\mu}/(2\pi c)` and
:math:`\omega_e x_e = hc\,\omega_e^{2}/(4 D_e)`. The quadratic
correction in :math:`(v + 1/2)` captures the softening of the
vibrational spacing toward dissociation.

The ladder is truncated at the dissociation limit: the maximum
bound vibrational quantum number :math:`v_{\max}^{(n)}` is
determined as the largest :math:`v` for which the fitted series
still lies below the dissociation energy :math:`E_d^{(n)}` of
electronic state :math:`n`. In practice a Dunham series is used
in place of the pure Morse expression, which preserves the
analytical form of the spacing but allows higher-order terms to
be fitted to ab-initio data:

.. math::

   E_v^{(n)}(v) = \sum_{k \ge 1} Y_{k,0}^{(n)}
                  \left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{k}.

Rotational sub-ladder — rigid rotor with distortion
---------------------------------------------------

For each :math:`(n, v)`, the rotational term is built on the
rigid-rotor energy :math:`B\,J(J+1)`, augmented by a centrifugal-
distortion correction :math:`-D\,[J(J+1)]^{2}`:

.. math::

   E_r^{(n, v)}(J)
     = B_v\, J(J+1) - D_v\,[J(J+1)]^{2}.

The rotational and distortion constants :math:`B_v` and
:math:`D_v` depend on the vibrational state through the
rotation-vibration coupling:

.. math::

   B_v = B_e - \alpha_e\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right), \qquad
   D_v = D_e - \beta_e\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right).

The maximum rotational quantum number :math:`J_{\max}^{(n,v)}` is
obtained from a **centrifugal-barrier analysis**: as :math:`J`
grows, the effective potential

.. math::

   V_{\mathrm{eff}}(R; J)
     = V_n(R) + \frac{\hbar^{2}\, J(J+1)}{2\mu R^{2}}

develops a maximum that eventually falls below the dissociation
asymptote; beyond that point the :math:`(n, v, J)` state is no
longer bound. :math:`J_{\max}^{(n,v)}` is set to the largest
:math:`J` for which a bound state persists; the numerical
determination loops over :math:`J` and tests whether the energy
derived from the Dunham expansion is still smaller than the local
centrifugal barrier.

Dunham expansion and ab-initio fits
-----------------------------------

The general expansion carried by the database is

.. math::

   E(n, v, J)
     = T_e^{(n)}
     + \sum_{k, \ell \ge 0} Y_{k,\ell}^{(n)}
       \left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{k} [J(J+1)]^{\ell},

which subsumes the Morse-plus-distortion form as the leading
terms. The coefficients :math:`Y_{k,\ell}^{(n)}` are fitted,
electronic state by electronic state, against ab-initio PECs or
against the term values of Huber-Herzberg-style compilations. Fit
quality and provenance are documented, species by species, in
``docs/Database/dunham_coefficients_from_ab_initio_PES.md`` in
the repository.

Degeneracies
------------

The statistical weight of a rovibronic level is the product of
rotational and electronic multiplicities:

.. math::

   g(n, v, J) = (2J+1)\, g_{\mathrm{elec}}^{(n)},

where the :math:`(2J+1)` factor counts the :math:`m_J = -J,
\ldots, +J` projections of the rotational angular momentum, and
:math:`g_{\mathrm{elec}}^{(n)}` is the electronic spatial-spin
multiplicity —  for instance :math:`g = 3` for a
:math:`{}^{3}\Sigma` state, :math:`g = 2` for :math:`{}^{2}\Sigma`,
and :math:`g = 4` for :math:`{}^{2}\Pi` (spin-orbit doubling
included). Nuclear spin contributions to the multiplicity cancel
out across reactant and product in the types of balance we are
concerned with, and are omitted from the database entries.

Nuclear-spin statistics and ortho/para sub-ladders
--------------------------------------------------

For homonuclear diatomics such as N\ :sub:`2`, O\ :sub:`2`, and
H\ :sub:`2`, the symmetry of the nuclear wave-function under
exchange of the two identical nuclei constrains the allowed
:math:`(v, J)` combinations:

* symmetric electronic states
  (:math:`\Sigma^{+}_{g}`, :math:`\Pi_{g}`, …) with nuclei of
  spin :math:`I`: even :math:`J` pair with one total-spin state
  count, odd :math:`J` with the complementary one;
* antisymmetric electronic states
  (:math:`\Sigma^{-}_{u}`, …): the parity is reversed.

The practical consequence is that, for a homonuclear species,
only alternate :math:`J` values are physically populated within
each electronic state. The sampling routines in the solver
respect this parity constraint by enforcing the allowed parity
at the Larsen-Borgnakke acceptance step, which amounts to halving
the effective rotational partition function.

Ground-state normalisation and provenance
-----------------------------------------

Every energy in the database is stored **relative to the
molecular ground state** :math:`(n, v, J) = (0, 0, 0)`:

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J) \leftarrow
     E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J) - E_{\mathrm{int}}(0, 0, 0),

so that the ground state carries zero internal energy by
convention. This choice has no physical content, but ensures that
the dissociation energy :math:`D_0` that the chemistry module
reads from the database is the physical bond energy, rather than
the potential minimum :math:`D_e = D_0 + E_v(0, 0, 0)`. It also
means that the partition functions defined below are explicitly
computed relative to the ground state, which removes the
additive constant :math:`T_e^{(0)}` from every rate integral.

Partition functions
-------------------

The internal partition function of species :math:`s` at
temperature :math:`T` is the sum, over all bound rovibronic
levels, of the Boltzmann factor weighted by the level degeneracy:

.. math::

   Q_s^{\mathrm{int}}(T)
     = \sum_{n=0}^{N_{\mathrm{el}}^{(s)}-1}
       \sum_{v=0}^{v_{\max}^{(n)}}
       \sum_{J=0}^{J_{\max}^{(n,v)}}
       g(n, v, J)\,
       \exp\!\left[-\frac{E(n, v, J)}{k_B T}\right].

These internal partition functions, together with the
translational contribution :math:`Q_s^{\mathrm{tr}}(T) =
V(2\pi m_s k_B T / h^{2})^{3/2}`, enter the equilibrium
constants used by the chemistry module (see
:doc:`chemistry`). They are pre-tabulated over the simulation
temperature range at solver start-up, which turns the run-time
rate-constant lookups into constant-cost bisection calls.

Typical state-space sizes
-------------------------

For a diatomic species in air chemistry, the level space is
dense. O\ :sub:`2`, for example, carries of order six electronic
states populated at re-entry temperatures, of the order of 60
vibrational levels per electronic state, and on average some 15
rotational levels per vibrational level, for a total of
:math:`\sim 3 \times 10^{5}` rovibronic levels.
N\ :sub:`2` has fewer bound electronic states within the energy
range of interest but a denser rotational ladder, and lands at a
comparable order of magnitude. The tables are built once, at
solver start-up, and cached on disk so that subsequent runs with
the same species list incur no rebuild cost.
