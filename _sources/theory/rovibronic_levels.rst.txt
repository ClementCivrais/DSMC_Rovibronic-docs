Rovibronic Level Structure
==========================

The rovibronic extension replaces the continuous internal energy
usually carried on a DSMC particle by an indexed triple
:math:`(n, v, J)` that selects one level out of a three-dimensional
table. The present section describes how that table is built, which
symbols are involved, and which assumptions underpin each step.

Energy expansion
----------------

We expand the total internal energy of a diatomic molecule as

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J)
     = T_e^{(n)} + E_v^{(n)}(v) + E_r^{(n, v)}(J),

where :math:`T_e^{(n)}` is the electronic term energy of level
:math:`n`, :math:`E_v^{(n)}` is the vibrational contribution in
electronic level :math:`n`, and :math:`E_r^{(n,v)}` is the
rotational contribution in the :math:`(n, v)` manifold. The three
contributions are modelled as follows.

Vibrational ladder (Morse oscillator)
`````````````````````````````````````

Following the Morse oscillator description, the vibrational term
energy in electronic level :math:`n` reads

.. math::

   E_v^{(n)}(v) = hc\left[\omega_e^{(n)}\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)
                  - \omega_e x_e^{(n)}\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{2}\right],

with the harmonic frequency :math:`\omega_e^{(n)}` and the
anharmonicity :math:`\omega_e x_e^{(n)}` given in
:math:`\mathrm{cm}^{-1}`. The ladder is truncated at the dissociation
limit: the maximum bound vibrational quantum number
:math:`v_{\max}^{(n)}` is determined as the largest :math:`v` for
which the fitted series still lies below the dissociation energy
:math:`E_d^{(n)}` of electronic state :math:`n`.

Rotational sub-ladder (rigid rotor + distortion)
````````````````````````````````````````````````

For each :math:`(n, v)`, the rotational ladder is built from

.. math::

   E_r^{(n, v)}(J) = B_v\, J(J+1) - D_v\,[J(J+1)]^{2},

with vibration-dependent rotational and centrifugal-distortion
constants

.. math::

   B_v = B_e - \alpha_e\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right), \qquad
   D_v = D_e - \beta_e\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right).

The maximum rotational quantum number :math:`J_{\max}^{(n,v)}` is
obtained from a centrifugal-barrier analysis, i.e. as the largest
:math:`J` for which the effective potential (including the
centrifugal contribution) still supports a bound state.

Degeneracy
``````````

The statistical weight of a level is

.. math::

   g(n, v, J) = (2J+1)\, g_{\mathrm{elec}}^{(n)},

where :math:`g_{\mathrm{elec}}^{(n)}` accounts for the electronic
degeneracy (e.g. 3 for a :math:`{}^{3}\Sigma` state, 4 for
:math:`{}^{2}\Pi`). For homonuclear molecules the parity constraint
of the nuclear spin further suppresses either even- or odd-:math:`J`
sub-ladders, which is enforced in the sampling routines.

Ground-state normalisation and provenance
-----------------------------------------

It is important to emphasise that every energy in the database is
stored relative to the molecular ground state :math:`(n, v, J) =
(0, 0, 0)`, i.e.

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J) \leftarrow
     E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J) - E_{\mathrm{int}}(0, 0, 0),

so that the ground state carries zero internal energy by convention.
This choice has no physical content but ensures that the
dissociation energy :math:`D_0` that the chemistry module extracts
from the database is the physical bond energy, rather than the
fitted potential minimum.

The Dunham coefficients are stored in
``constant/speciesCache/<species>.dat`` together with the
:math:`(n, v, J)` level tables and the associated degeneracies. Fit
quality and ab-initio provenance are documented in
``docs/Database/dunham_coefficients_from_ab_initio_PES.md``.

Typical state-space sizes
-------------------------

For a diatomic species in air chemistry, the level space is dense.
As a representative example, O\ :sub:`2` carries six electronic
states, of the order of 60 vibrational levels per electronic state,
and on average some 15 rotational levels per vibrational level —
altogether :math:`\sim 3 \times 10^{5}` rovibronic levels. The
tables are built once, at solver start-up, and cached on disk so
that subsequent runs with the same species list incur no rebuild
cost.
