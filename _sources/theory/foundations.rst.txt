Kinetic foundations and the DSMC algorithm
==========================================

The present section establishes the kinetic-theoretic foundations
on which the solver rests, states the regime of validity in terms
of dimensionless numbers, and describes the DSMC algorithm at the
level of detail required to interpret the input parameters and the
numerical diagnostics produced by the code.

The Boltzmann equation
----------------------

For a dilute monatomic gas, the single-particle phase-space
distribution :math:`f(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{v}, t)` satisfies

.. math::

   \frac{\partial f}{\partial t}
     + \mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} f
     + \mathbf{F}\cdot\nabla_{\mathbf{v}} f
     = \mathcal{C}[f, f],

with :math:`\mathbf{F}` an external body force (per unit mass) and
the binary-collision operator

.. math::

   \mathcal{C}[f, f](\mathbf{v})
     = \int\!\mathrm{d}\mathbf{v}_{\star}\int\!\mathrm{d}\Omega\;
       |\mathbf{v} - \mathbf{v}_{\star}|\,
       \sigma(g, \chi)\,
       \bigl[f'\,f'_{\star} - f\,f_{\star}\bigr],

where :math:`g = |\mathbf{v} - \mathbf{v}_{\star}|`,
:math:`\sigma(g, \chi)` is the differential cross section at
relative speed :math:`g` and centre-of-mass scattering angle
:math:`\chi`, and primes denote post-collision velocities. For a
polyatomic mixture with internal states indexed by
:math:`\mathrm{s}`, one carries one distribution
:math:`f_{\mathrm{s}}` per state and the collision operator
becomes a sum over reactant states of the corresponding
state-to-state cross sections.

Three assumptions underpin this form and are carried into DSMC:

* **Molecular chaos** —  the joint distribution of two colliding
  molecules factorises,
  :math:`f^{(2)}(\mathbf{v}, \mathbf{v}_{\star})
  = f(\mathbf{v})\,f(\mathbf{v}_{\star})`.
* **Binary collisions only** —  three- and higher-body
  collisions are negligible, which holds for :math:`n d^{3}
  \ll 1` with :math:`n` the number density and :math:`d` the
  collision diameter.
* **Point collisions** —  the collision event is localised at a
  single point despite the finite collision diameter.

Detailed balance and the H-theorem
----------------------------------

Detailed balance is the microscopic statement that, at
thermodynamic equilibrium, the forward and reverse rates of every
elementary event are equal. Combined with time-reversal symmetry
of the collision kernel, it implies Boltzmann's H-theorem,

.. math::

   \frac{\mathrm{d}H}{\mathrm{d}t} \le 0, \qquad
   H = \int f\,\ln f\;\mathrm{d}\mathbf{v},

with equality if and only if :math:`f` is Maxwell-Boltzmann. The
theorem is the formal justification for interpreting the
equilibrium distribution of a DSMC simulation —  once transients
have decayed —  as the correct physical one.

Knudsen number and flow regimes
-------------------------------

The regime of validity is characterised by the Knudsen number

.. math::

   \mathrm{Kn} = \frac{\lambda}{L},

with :math:`\lambda` the mean free path and :math:`L` a
characteristic flow length. Standard classification:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 35 65

   * - Regime
     - Description
   * - :math:`\mathrm{Kn} < 10^{-2}`
     - Continuum: Navier-Stokes with no-slip boundary conditions
       is adequate.
   * - :math:`10^{-2} < \mathrm{Kn} < 10^{-1}`
     - Slip: Navier-Stokes with velocity-slip and
       temperature-jump conditions on walls.
   * - :math:`10^{-1} < \mathrm{Kn} < 10`
     - Transitional: neither continuum nor free-molecular; DSMC
       is the method of choice.
   * - :math:`\mathrm{Kn} > 10`
     - Free-molecular: particles collide almost exclusively with
       walls; collisionless kinetic-theory expressions apply.

The mean free path in a mixture of species with VHS cross
sections is

.. math::

   \lambda = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}\,\pi\,n\,d^{2}}\,
             \left(\frac{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}{T}\right)^{\omega - 1/2},

where :math:`n` is the total number density, :math:`d` is the
reference VHS diameter, and :math:`\omega` is the viscosity
exponent. The mean collision time :math:`\tau_c` is
:math:`\lambda / \bar{c}` with :math:`\bar{c}` the mean thermal
speed.

The DSMC time-step
------------------

DSMC does not solve the Boltzmann equation directly. Instead it
follows a statistically equivalent particle system, decoupling
motion and collisions on a sub-mean-collision-time step. One
global time-step :math:`\Delta t` of the cloud executes, in order:

#. **Advection.** Each simulator particle is advected
   ballistically over :math:`\Delta t` under the current body
   force. Parcel-boundary interactions are processed as they
   arise (walls, cyclic patches, inflow/outflow, processor
   patches); rovibronic-aware walls resample the internal state
   from the wall temperature, other patches leave it unchanged.
#. **Collisions.** In each computational cell of volume
   :math:`V_c`, collision candidates are generated through the
   selected partner-selection scheme
   (:doc:`collision_sampling`) and accepted with probability
   :math:`\sigma_T\,g / (\sigma_T g)_{\max}`. On acceptance,
   the binary collision model
   (:doc:`collision_cross_sections`) rotates the relative
   velocity and redistributes internal energy through
   Larsen-Borgnakke sampling (:doc:`larsen_borgnakke`); reactive
   channels (:doc:`chemistry`) are tested in the same pass.
#. **Controllers.** Configured flow-state controllers (see
   :doc:`adaptation_controllers`) sample their target quantities
   and, if due, act on the parcel population.
#. **Measurement.** Per-cell accumulators update the primitive
   moments from which macroscopic fields are later built
   (:doc:`macroscopic_properties`); per-patch accumulators do
   the same on boundary faces.
#. **Adaptation.** If the adaptive machinery
   (:doc:`adaptation_controllers`) is enabled and the
   configured adaptation interval has elapsed, sub-cell levels
   and/or parcel weights are recomputed and :math:`\Delta t` may
   be revised.

Equivalent-particles factor
---------------------------

Each simulator particle represents :math:`F_N` real molecules
(``nEquivalentParticles`` in the input dictionary). The physical
collision rate scales as :math:`n^{2}`, so the simulated collision
candidates must scale as :math:`F_N \cdot N^{2}`; the No-Time-
Counter scheme absorbs this scaling into its expected-candidates
formula (see :doc:`collision_sampling`). Equivalently,
:math:`F_N` converts between simulator and physical cross sections
at every step and is the single knob that trades statistical
resolution against cost: halving :math:`F_N` doubles the particle
count per cell and halves the statistical variance.

Numerical convergence conditions
--------------------------------

Three conditions must be satisfied jointly for DSMC to reproduce
the Boltzmann solution to leading order:

.. math::

   \Delta x < \frac{\lambda}{3}, \qquad
   \Delta t < \frac{\tau_c}{3}, \qquad
   N_{\mathrm{cell}} \gtrsim 10\text{–}20.

The first two are conditions on the discretisation and are the
reason sub-cell refinement is valuable in regions of steep
gradients (:doc:`adaptation_controllers`); the third is a
statistical condition whose violation merely amplifies noise. Bird
[Bird1994f]_ gives a thorough discussion of the bias introduced
by violating each condition, which we do not repeat here.

Statistical convergence and errors
----------------------------------

A cell-level macroscopic field :math:`\langle Q\rangle` is
estimated from :math:`N` independent samples as

.. math::

   \overline{Q}
     = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} Q_i,
   \qquad
   \mathrm{Var}(\overline{Q})
     = \frac{\mathrm{Var}(Q)}{N},

so that the relative statistical error on a macroscopic quantity
decays as :math:`1/\sqrt{N}`. Resolving a 1% macroscopic quantity
requires of the order of :math:`10^{4}` independent samples per
cell; in steady flows this cost is absorbed through temporal
averaging after the transient phase has decayed; in unsteady flows
it is absorbed through ensemble averaging over independent runs.
The ``measureErrors`` switch on ``dsmcVolFields`` (see
:doc:`../classes/measurements`) writes the per-cell standard
deviations alongside the averaged fields so that the statistical
confidence intervals can be reconstructed post-hoc.

Motivating the rovibronic extension
-----------------------------------

The stock DSMC internal-energy model carries, on each particle, a
continuous rotational and vibrational energy, and exchanges energy
with the translational mode through the Larsen-Borgnakke model.
Under thermodynamic equilibrium, this reproduces the Boltzmann
distribution correctly. Under strong non-equilibrium, however,
two pathologies emerge:

* The **tail** of the internal-energy distribution deviates
  substantially from the Boltzmann form in strong shocks and
  rapid expansions, yet it is precisely the tail that drives
  dissociation and radiation.
* The **electronic mode** is altogether absent in the continuous
  formulation, or at best represented by a single average
  energy, making any coupling with radiation transport solvers
  semi-phenomenological at best.

The rovibronic extension replaces the continuous internal-energy
variable by an indexed triple :math:`(n, v, J)` that selects one
level in a pre-tabulated three-dimensional database
(:doc:`rovibronic_levels`). Energies, degeneracies, and reaction
thresholds are then evaluated from Dunham-fitted coefficients
derived from ab-initio potential-energy surfaces. Concrete
benefits include non-Boltzmann-tail-resolved chemistry
[Civrais2024ChemI_f]_, [Civrais2024ChemII_f]_, and the direct
coupling with radiation transport solvers
[Civrais2024Electronic_f]_.
