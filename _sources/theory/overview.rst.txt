Method Overview
===============

The present section aims to describe the Direct Simulation Monte
Carlo (DSMC) method as implemented in this solver, and to motivate
the rovibronic extension that constitutes the novelty of the present
work.

The DSMC method [Bird1994t]_ resolves a rarefied or transitional gas
flow by following a population of simulator particles, each
representing a large number of real molecules. Motion and collisions
are decoupled on a sub-mean-collision-time step: particles are first
advected ballistically, and then binary collisions are sampled, cell
by cell, according to the selected collision model —  the Variable
Soft Sphere (VSS) model by default. The DSMC method relies on
considerations from kinetic theory, imposing that the gas be dilute,
i.e. the mean molecular diameter must be significantly smaller than
the mean free path and Boltzmann's molecular chaos assumption must
hold.

The novelty of the rovibronic extension lies in the replacement of
the usual continuous (or multi-temperature) internal-energy model by
**explicit quantum-state tracking**. Every molecular particle carries
three integers:

* the electronic level index :math:`n`,
* the vibrational level index :math:`v` (within electronic level
  :math:`n`), and
* the rotational level index :math:`J` (within the
  :math:`(n, v)` manifold).

Energies, degeneracies, and transition rates are evaluated from
pre-tabulated level tables, with Dunham-fitted coefficients derived
from ab-initio potential-energy surfaces where available.

What the rovibronic description buys
------------------------------------

The state-resolved description is more expensive than a bulk
temperature representation, but delivers three benefits that matter
in applications of interest:

* Correct non-Boltzmann tail populations in strong shocks and rapid
  expansions, where multi-temperature assumptions fail.
* Direct coupling between excited-state populations and
  state-specific dissociation, recombination, and charge-exchange
  channels [Civrais2024ChemI_t]_, [Civrais2024ChemII_t]_.
* Predictive chemistry without having to fit global rate constants
  to a particular temperature range, because the rate integrals
  are constructed directly from the spectroscopic database.

In particular, coupling between the vibrational and electronic
modes of molecular species is essential when DSMC is coupled with
radiation transport solvers [Civrais2024Electronic_t]_, or in
flows in which state-selective dissociation channels (e.g. from
electronically excited O\ :sub:`2` in re-entry plasmas) alter the
overall chemistry.

Organisation of the present guide
---------------------------------

The remainder of the Theory Guide develops, in turn:

* :doc:`rovibronic_levels` — the spectroscopic database and the
  construction of the :math:`(n, v, J)` energy tables;
* :doc:`collisions` — the VSS collision model and the no-time-counter
  (NTC) scheme;
* :doc:`chemistry` — dissociation, recombination, exchange, and
  ionisation, with particular emphasis on the parameter-free
  computation of heats of reaction from the database;
* :doc:`relaxation` — the parallel-marginal cascade that drives the
  internal modes toward equilibrium, and its validation against the
  multi-exponential Landau-Teller solution;
* :doc:`macroscopic_properties` — the inversion from state-resolved
  populations to effective temperatures.
