Method Overview
===============

DSMC resolves a rarefied or transitional-regime gas flow by following
a population of *simulator particles*, each representing a large
number of real molecules. Motion and collisions are decoupled on a
sub-mean-collision-time step: particles are advected ballistically,
then binary collisions are sampled cell-by-cell according to the
selected collision model (VSS by default).

The rovibronic extension replaces the usual continuous or
multi-temperature internal-energy model with **explicit quantum
state tracking**. Every molecular particle carries three integers:

* :math:`e_{qn}` — electronic level index
* :math:`v_{qn}` — vibrational level index (within the electronic level)
* :math:`j_{qn}` — rotational level index (within the vibrational level)

Energies, degeneracies, and transition rates are evaluated from
pre-tabulated level tables, with Dunham-fitted coefficients derived
from ab-initio potential energy surfaces where available.

What the rovibronic description buys
------------------------------------

* Correct non-Boltzmann tail populations in strong shocks and rapid
  expansions, where multi-temperature assumptions fail.
* Direct coupling between excited-state populations and state-specific
  dissociation/recombination channels.
* Predictive chemistry without fitting global rate constants to a
  particular temperature range.
