Larsen-Borgnakke energy redistribution
======================================

Once a binary collision has been accepted by the sampling layer
(:doc:`collision_sampling`), the Larsen-Borgnakke (LB) phantom
collision model [LarsenBorgnakke1975lb]_ governs the exchange
of energy between the translational and internal modes of the
colliding parcels. In the rovibronic extension, the acceptance
step acts on the discrete :math:`(n, v, J)` state space rather
than on a continuous internal-energy variable, while the
underlying statistical structure is preserved.

Collision energy pool
---------------------

Each collision is characterised by a pooled energy

.. math::

   E_c = E_{\mathrm{rel}}
       + \sum_{k\in\mathrm{pool}} E_k^{\mathrm{int}},

where :math:`E_{\mathrm{rel}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mu_{12}\,g^{2}` is
the translational energy in the centre-of-mass frame, and the
sum runs over those internal modes (rotational, rovibrational,
rovibronic) that the current event draws upon. The pool is
conserved by construction: what is added to the internal modes
in the sampling step is deducted from :math:`E_{\mathrm{rel}}`,
and vice-versa.

Acceptance-rejection rule
-------------------------

The LB sampler proposes a trial post-collision internal state for
the parcel and accepts it with probability proportional to the
phase-space volume still available to the remaining translational
degrees of freedom. In the discrete rovibronic formulation, the
acceptance-rejection criterion reads

.. math::

   f(E')
     = g(E')\,
       \left(\frac{E_c - E'}{E_c}\right)^{\alpha_{\mathrm{LB}}},

where :math:`E'` is the energy of the trial state, :math:`g(E')`
is its degeneracy (see :doc:`rovibronic_levels`), and
:math:`\alpha_{\mathrm{LB}}` is the LB exponent derived below.
The acceptance probability is

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{acc}}^{\mathrm{LB}}
     = \frac{f(E')}{f_{\max}},
   \qquad
   f_{\max} = \max_{E''} f(E''),

and the procedure is iterated until a trial state is accepted.

The role of the degeneracy factor :math:`g(E')` is precisely what
distinguishes the rovibronic LB from its continuous-variable
ancestor: in the continuous case :math:`g(E')\propto
E'{}^{\xi_{\mathrm{int}}/2 - 1}`, while here :math:`g(E')` is the
exact statistical weight of a single quantum level, and the sum
over :math:`E'` is replaced by a sum over the enumerated
:math:`(n, v, J)` triples inside the accessible energy window
:math:`E' < E_c`.

Larsen-Borgnakke exponent
-------------------------

For a parcel whose translational degrees of freedom entering the
relative motion total :math:`\xi_{\mathrm{rel}}`, and whose
internal modes participating in the current event have pooled
effective DOF :math:`\xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}`, the LB
exponent is

.. math::

   \alpha_{\mathrm{LB}}
     = \frac{\xi_{\mathrm{rel}}
             + \xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}}{2} - 1.

For the VHS cross section, :math:`\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} = 5 - 2\omega`
with :math:`\omega` the viscosity exponent; for a pure rotational
event in a diatomic, :math:`\xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}
\approx 2`; for a rovibrational event the pool is augmented by
the temperature-dependent vibrational contribution

.. math::

   \xi_v(T) = \frac{2\,\theta_v/T}{\exp(\theta_v/T) - 1},

with :math:`\theta_v` the characteristic vibrational temperature;
for a rovibronic event the electronic mode is added similarly.
The correct use of :math:`\xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}` is
the single most delicate step in the implementation, and was the
source of several compensating bugs in early versions of the
solver; the current value has been audited against analytical
expectations and is documented in
``docs/Rovibronic Article/DSMC_Bugs_Report.pdf``.

Effective degrees of freedom
----------------------------

The effective DOF of a composite mode :math:`k` at temperature
:math:`T` are defined, following Boyd [Boyd1990lb]_, through

.. math::

   \xi_k(T) = \frac{2\,\langle E_k\rangle(T)}{k_B T},

where :math:`\langle E_k\rangle(T)` is the thermal average of the
mode's energy per molecule at :math:`T`, evaluated from the
partition function,

.. math::

   \langle E_k\rangle(T)
     = \frac{\sum_{\{i\}} g_i^{(k)}\, E_i^{(k)}\,
             \exp\!\bigl[-E_i^{(k)}/k_B T\bigr]}
            {\sum_{\{i\}} g_i^{(k)}\,
             \exp\!\bigl[-E_i^{(k)}/k_B T\bigr]}.

The sums run over the level subspace relevant to mode :math:`k`:
rotations at fixed :math:`(n, v)` for mode :math:`R`;
rovibrations at fixed :math:`n` for mode :math:`RV`; full
rovibronic space for mode :math:`RVE`. The quantities
:math:`\xi_k(T)` and :math:`\langle E_k\rangle(T)` are
pre-tabulated at solver start-up and accessed at run-time
through a bisection lookup.

Energy accounting and detailed balance
--------------------------------------

Once a post-collision internal state has been accepted, the
available translational energy is reduced accordingly,

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{rel}}' = E_c - \sum_{k\in\mathrm{pool}} E_k'^{(\mathrm{int})},

and the post-collision relative speed is recomputed as
:math:`g' = \sqrt{2\,E_{\mathrm{rel}}'/\mu_{12}}`. Because the LB
sampler draws from a thermodynamically weighted distribution over
accessible states, detailed balance is satisfied by construction
at equilibrium: the forward and reverse rates of any
:math:`(n, v, J) \to (n', v', J')` transition are equal, as can
be verified analytically by integrating the acceptance-rejection
kernel against a Boltzmann distribution.

Failure modes and mitigations
-----------------------------

Three failure modes of the LB sampler have been identified and
addressed in the course of the project:

* **NaN in energy redistribution.** Under a specific configuration
  of exponents, the proposal energy :math:`E_c - E'` became
  negative in intermediate arithmetic, producing a NaN. The
  correction clips negative proposals at machine zero and re-draws
  the trial state. Documented in ``DSMC_Bugs_Report.pdf``.
* **Dissociation double-count.** In the early charge-exchange
  implementation, the dissociation event was sampled twice in a
  single collision. The fix routes each reactive channel through
  a single acceptance step.
* **Wrong effective DOF.** An earlier version of the sampler used
  the partner's internal DOF in place of the sampled parcel's
  own. The current implementation uses the parcel's own DOF, and
  the phase-1 heat-bath validation (:doc:`../examples/relaxation_landau_teller`)
  passes comfortably as a consequence.
