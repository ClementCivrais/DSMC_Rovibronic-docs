Neutral chemistry
=================

The present section derives the reactive channels of
**neutral-species chemistry** as implemented in the solver:
the Quantum-Kinetic (QK) model, its Total-Collision-Energy (TCE)
ancestor, and the post-reaction redistribution of internal
energy. Charged-species reactions, which inherit the same
statistical machinery but require additional care around the
electron population, are treated separately in
:doc:`chemistry_charged`.

Total-Collision-Energy (TCE) model *(Not implemented yet)*
----------------------------------------------------------

.. warning::

   The TCE model is **not implemented** in the current build.
   It is described here for completeness and as the historical
   baseline against which the rovibronic QK model of the next
   section is compared; the solver reads state-resolved QK
   parameters only, and a case with ``reactionModel`` set to a
   TCE-style class will not run. The section below may therefore
   be skipped on first reading.

Bird's TCE model [Bird1994ch]_ computes the reaction probability
from the collision energy :math:`E_c`, the activation energy
:math:`E_a`, and a fit to an experimental Arrhenius rate
:math:`k(T) = A\, T^{b}\,\exp(-E_a/k_B T)`. Starting from the
equilibrium rate constant

.. math::

   k^{\mathrm{eq}}(T)
     = \int_{E_a}^{\infty}
       P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{TCE}}(E_c)\,
       \sigma_T(E_c)\, g(E_c)\,
       f^{\mathrm{eq}}(E_c; T)\,
       \mathrm{d}E_c,

and matching to the Arrhenius form for a VHS gas yields the
closed-form reaction probability

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{TCE}}(E_c)
     = C_1\,\left(\frac{E_c - E_a}{E_c}\right)^{C_2}
       \left(\frac{E_c}{k_B T_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)^{C_3}
       \Theta(E_c - E_a),

where :math:`\Theta` is the Heaviside step and the three
coefficients :math:`C_1, C_2, C_3` are derived analytically from
:math:`A, b, \omega`:

.. math::

   C_1 = \frac{A\,(\omega - 1/2)!\,T_{\mathrm{ref}}^{1 - \omega}}
              {(b + 3/2 - \omega)!\,(m_{12}/2k_B)^{1/2}},

.. math::

   C_2 = b + \tfrac{1}{2} - \omega, \qquad
   C_3 = 1 - \omega + b.

The TCE model is a **global** reaction model: every collision
above :math:`E_a` has the same reaction probability regardless of
the reactants' internal states. This is its virtue (no per-state
data required) and its limitation (it cannot describe the
influence of excited-state populations on reaction rates).

Quantum-Kinetic (QK) model — full derivation
--------------------------------------------

The QK model of Bird [Bird2011qk]_, in its rovibronic extension
adopted here [Civrais2024ChemI_ch]_, replaces the TCE activation
energy by a **state-dependent threshold** for each reactant
:math:`(n, v, J)` triple. The derivation proceeds in four steps.

**Step 1 — microscopic reaction probability.** We start from the
principle that the reaction can only proceed when the collision
energy exceeds the state-specific dissociation energy
:math:`E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}`. For a VHS cross section the
post-threshold reaction probability derives from the classical
line-of-centres model:

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{QK}}(E_c; n, v, J)
     = \left(1 - \frac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}}{E_c}\right)^{3/2 - \omega}
       \Theta\!\bigl(E_c - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}\bigr),

where :math:`E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n, v, J)} = D_0 - E(n, v, J)`
is the energy needed to break the bond *given* that the molecule
already carries internal energy :math:`E(n, v, J)`.

**Step 2 — state-resolved reaction rate.** Integrating the above
against the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution of collision energies
for a VHS gas,

.. math::

   f^{\mathrm{eq}}(E_c; T)
     = \frac{E_c^{3/2 - \omega}}
            {(k_B T)^{5/2 - \omega}\,\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)}\,
       \exp\!\left(-\frac{E_c}{k_B T}\right),

yields the state-resolved rate

.. math::

   k^{\mathrm{QK}}_{nvJ}(T)
     = H(T)\,
       \Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{5}{2} - \omega,\;
         \frac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n, v, J)}}{k_B T}\right),

where :math:`H(T)` is the VHS binary-rate kernel

.. math::

   H(T) = \sigma_{\mathrm{ref}}^{\mathrm{VHS}}\,
          \sqrt{\frac{8\,k_B T}{\pi\,\mu_{12}}}\,
          \left(\frac{T}{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)^{1/2 - \omega}
          \frac{1}{\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)},

and :math:`\Gamma(s, x)` is the upper incomplete gamma function.

**Step 3 — Boltzmann-weighted total rate.** Averaging over the
reactant's internal-state population yields the macroscopic rate

.. math::

   k^{\mathrm{QK}}(T)
     = \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{n, v, J} g(n, v, J)\,
             e^{-E(n,v,J)/k_B T}\,
             H(T)\,\Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{5}{2} - \omega,\;
               \tfrac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n,v,J)}}{k_B T}\right)}
            {Q_{\mathrm{int}}(T)},

with :math:`Q_{\mathrm{int}}(T)` the internal partition function
(see :doc:`rovibronic_levels`). The summand makes manifest that
excited states contribute disproportionately to the rate, because
their dissociation threshold :math:`D_0 - E(n, v, J)` is smaller
than the ground-state value :math:`D_0`.

**Step 4 — acceptance-rejection at the collision.** At run-time,
each collision is tested against the QK probability

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{reac}}^{\mathrm{QK}}(E_c; n_P, v_P, J_P)
     = \left(1 - \frac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n_P, v_P, J_P)}}{E_c}\right)^{3/2 - \omega}
       \Theta\!\bigl(E_c - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n_P, v_P, J_P)}\bigr),

drawn for the dissociating parcel :math:`P` only; the partner
parcel :math:`Q` merely supplies its share of the collision
energy. If the probability exceeds the uniform random draw, the
reaction fires and the post-reaction energy redistribution begins.

Post-reaction redistribution of internal energy
-----------------------------------------------

Once a reaction has fired, the pooled energy available to the
products is

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{avail}} = E_c + \Delta H
                        - E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{(n_P, v_P, J_P)},

where :math:`E_c = E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(P) +
E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q)` is the collision energy pool,
:math:`\Delta H` is the heat of reaction (automatically read
from the database for dissociation, recombination, and exchange;
cf. :doc:`../theory/chemistry`). The redistribution proceeds in
the order below.

**Product-state enumeration.** For a dissociation
:math:`\mathrm{AB}(n, v, J) + \mathrm{M} \to \mathrm{A}(n_1) +
\mathrm{B}(n_2) + \mathrm{M}`, the product atoms' electronic
states :math:`(n_1, n_2)` are determined at initialisation by
the asymptote-matching algorithm below. For an exchange
reaction, two molecular / atomic products must be sampled.

**Larsen-Borgnakke sampling for the first product.** The first
product's state :math:`(n', v', J')_{\mathrm{AC}}` is sampled by
acceptance-rejection,

.. math::

   f_{1}(E_{\mathrm{AC}}')
     = g(E_{\mathrm{AC}}')\,
       \left(\frac{E_{\mathrm{avail}}
                    - E_{\mathrm{AC}}'}{E_{\mathrm{avail}}}\right)^{\alpha_{1}},

with exponent

.. math::

   \alpha_{1}
     = \frac{\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \xi_{\mathrm{AC}}^{\mathrm{int}}
             + \xi_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{int}}}{2} - 1,

where the exponent pools the translational DOF *and* the internal
DOF of every product whose state has not yet been sampled.

**Energy update.** The remaining pool is

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{avail}}' = E_{\mathrm{avail}} - E_{\mathrm{AC}}'.

**Second-product state.** The second product's state is sampled
in the same way, with exponent

.. math::

   \alpha_{2}
     = \frac{\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \xi_{\mathrm{B}}^{\mathrm{int}}}{2} - 1,

and energy :math:`E_{\mathrm{B}}'` drawn against :math:`E_{\mathrm{avail}}'`.

**Post-reaction translational energy.** The remaining pool,

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{rel}}'
     = E_{\mathrm{avail}}' - E_{\mathrm{B}}',

becomes the translational kinetic energy of the products'
relative motion. The post-reaction relative speed is
:math:`g' = \sqrt{2 E_{\mathrm{rel}}'/\mu'}`, with :math:`\mu'`
the reduced mass of the products, and the product velocities
follow from the conservation laws applied to the product pair.

**Detailed balance.** The sequential acceptance-rejection above
reproduces the thermodynamically-weighted product-state
distribution at equilibrium, and the forward and backward rates
of any channel are connected through the equilibrium constant.
This is the key property that allows the recombination rate to
be derived parameter-free from the dissociation rate (below).

Dissociation with potential-energy-curve asymptote matching
-----------------------------------------------------------

When the molecule AB dissociates from electronic state :math:`n`,
the electronic states of the product atoms are determined by
**matching the PEC asymptote**. Define the asymptote energy

.. math::

   \mathrm{asymptote}(n) = T_e^{(n)} + E_d^{(n)} - E^{(0)}_{000},

and the excess asymptote

.. math::

   \Delta E(n) = \mathrm{asymptote}(n) - \mathrm{asymptote}(0).

For each molecular electronic state, we search over all feasible
pairs :math:`(n_1, n_2)` of product-atom electronic states for
the combination that minimises

.. math::

   \mathrm{err}(n_1, n_2)
     = \bigl|\,E_{A}(n_1) + E_{B}(n_2) - \Delta E(n)\,\bigr|.

The mapping :math:`n \mapsto (n_1^{\star}, n_2^{\star})` is built
at initialisation, stored in a look-up table, and printed to the
terminal together with its residual errors (typically smaller
than :math:`1~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}`). The match guarantees that the
Wigner-Witmer correlation rules are obeyed at the solver level,
without any user input.

Recombination from detailed balance
-----------------------------------

The three-body recombination rate
:math:`\mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B} + \mathrm{M} \to \mathrm{AB} +
\mathrm{M}` is not a free parameter. It is derived from the
dissociation rate through the equilibrium constant,

.. math::

   k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T) = \frac{k^{D}(T)}{K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)},

with

.. math::

   K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)
     = \frac{(2\pi m_A k_B T)^{3/2}\,(2\pi m_B k_B T)^{3/2}\,h^{3}}
            {(2\pi m_{\mathrm{AB}} k_B T)^{3/2}}\,
       \frac{Q_A(T)\,Q_B(T)}{Q_{\mathrm{AB}}(T)}\,
       \exp\!\left(-\frac{D_0}{k_B T}\right).

The partition functions :math:`Q_i(T)` are built at
initialisation from the rovibronic database, and
:math:`D_0` is read from the molecule's database entry. The
user supplies neither the recombination rate nor the heat of
reaction; both are computed internally. Run-time, a collision
between :math:`\mathrm{A}` and :math:`\mathrm{B}` in the
presence of a third body :math:`\mathrm{M}` is tested with
probability :math:`P_{\mathrm{rec}} = n_M k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T) /
\nu_{\mathrm{coll}}(\mathrm{A},\mathrm{B})`.

Atom-exchange (Zeldovich-type) reactions
----------------------------------------

For :math:`\mathrm{AB} + \mathrm{C} \to \mathrm{AC} + \mathrm{B}`,
the heat of reaction is computed automatically from the database
as

.. math::

   \Delta H_{\mathrm{exch}}
     = D_0(\mathrm{AC}) - D_0(\mathrm{AB}).

The QK reaction probability,

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{exch}}^{\mathrm{QK}}(E_c; n, v, J)
     = \frac{\bigl[1 - E_{\mathrm{act}}^{(n,v,J)}/E_c\bigr]^{3/2 - \omega}}
            {\displaystyle\sum_{n',v',J'}^{\mathrm{BC}}
              g(n', v', J')\,
              \bigl[1 - E(n', v', J')/E_c\bigr]^{3/2 - \omega}},

normalises the state-resolved threshold by the Boltzmann-averaged
denominator over the product-molecule level space, and fires
through the two-stage LB sampling described above. Start-up
diagnostics print the computed heat against literature values:
:math:`\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}`
returns :math:`\Delta H \approx -37\,732~\mathrm{K}`, within 1%
of the tabulated value.
