Chemistry
=========

Chemistry is handled as state-specific or state-aggregated reactions
sampled during the collision phase. The reactive channels supported
by the solver are dissociation, recombination, exchange, electron-
impact ionisation, associative ionisation, and charge exchange. One
of the defining design choices of the rovibronic extension is that
**heats of reaction are extracted automatically from the
spectroscopic database**, so that the user is not required to supply
them in the input dictionary, and so that thermodynamic consistency
between the database and the reaction rates is guaranteed by
construction [Civrais2024ChemI_c]_, [Civrais2024ChemII_c]_.

Dissociation with asymptote matching
------------------------------------

When a diatomic molecule AB in electronic level :math:`n`
dissociates, the electronic states of the product atoms are
determined by matching the molecular potential-energy-curve (PEC)
dissociation asymptote. This ensures that Wigner-Witmer correlation
rules are respected and that product atoms emerge in the correct
electronic configuration.

For each molecular electronic state :math:`n` we define the
asymptote energy

.. math::

   \mathrm{asymptote}(n) = T_e^{(n)} + E_d^{(n)} - E^{(0)}_{000},

and the excess asymptote

.. math::

   \Delta E(n) = \mathrm{asymptote}(n) - \mathrm{asymptote}(0).

We then search among all feasible pairs :math:`(n_1, n_2)` of
product-atom electronic states for the combination that minimises

.. math::

   \mathrm{err}(n_1, n_2) = \bigl|\,E_{A}(n_1) + E_{B}(n_2)
                             - \Delta E(n)\,\bigr|.

The mapping :math:`n \mapsto (n_1^{\star}, n_2^{\star})` is built at
initialisation and printed to the terminal, together with the
matching error for each channel; the error is typically smaller
than :math:`1~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}`. At reaction time, the product
electronic states are looked up deterministically, and the available
energy

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{avail}} = E_{\mathrm{rel}}
                      + E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q)
                      - E_{\mathrm{diss}}(n, v, J)

is redistributed via Larsen-Borgnakke sampling between the products'
translational and internal modes.

Recombination from detailed balance
-----------------------------------

The recombination rate :math:`k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T)` is not an input
parameter but is derived from the corresponding dissociation rate
:math:`k^{D}(T)` and the equilibrium constant
:math:`K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)` through

.. math::

   k^{\mathrm{rec}}(T) = \frac{k^{D}(T)}{K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T)},

with

.. math::

   K_{\mathrm{eq}}(T) =
     \frac{(2\pi m_A k_B T)^{3/2}\,(2\pi m_B k_B T)^{3/2}\,h^{3}}
          {(2\pi m_{AB} k_B T)^{3/2}}
     \frac{Q_A\,Q_B}{Q_{AB}}\,
     \exp\!\left(-\frac{D_0}{k_B T}\right),

where the partition functions :math:`Q_i(T)` are pre-tabulated from
the rovibronic database and the dissociation energy :math:`D_0` is
read directly from the product molecule's ground-state database
entry. The dissociation rate is itself constructed by summing over
accessible rovibronic states,

.. math::

   k^{D}(T) = H(T) \sum_{n,v,J} g_{nvJ}\,
              \exp\!\left(-\tfrac{E_{nvJ}}{k_B T}\right)\,
              \Gamma\!\left(\tfrac{5}{2} - \omega,\;
                             \tfrac{E_{\mathrm{diss}}^{nvJ}
                                     - E_{nvJ}}{k_B T}\right)
              \Big/ Q_{\mathrm{int}}(T),

with :math:`H(T)` the VSS binary-rate kernel and
:math:`\Gamma(s, x)` the upper incomplete gamma function. The
resulting scheme is **parameter-free**: neither the user nor a
separate reaction dictionary supplies the recombination rate.

Exchange with automatic heat of reaction
----------------------------------------

For a Zeldovich-type exchange reaction
:math:`\mathrm{AB} + \mathrm{C} \to \mathrm{AC} + \mathrm{B}`, the
heat of reaction is computed automatically as

.. math::

   \Delta H_{\mathrm{exch}}
     = D_0(\mathrm{AC}) - D_0(\mathrm{AB}),

where both dissociation energies are read from the database. The
available energy for the reaction is then

.. math::

   E_c = E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(\mathrm{AB})
       + E_{\mathrm{int}}(\mathrm{C}) + \Delta H_{\mathrm{exch}},

and the reaction probability in the quantum-kinetic (QK) model
reads

.. math::

   P_{\mathrm{exch}} =
     \frac{\bigl[1 - E_{\mathrm{act}}/E_c\bigr]^{3/2 - \omega}}
          {\displaystyle \sum_{n,v,J}^{\mathrm{BC}} g(n, v, J)
           \bigl[1 - E(n, v, J)/E_c\bigr]^{3/2 - \omega}}.

On reaction, the product molecule :math:`\mathrm{AC}` is sampled
first in :math:`(n', v', J')` from Larsen-Borgnakke, followed by the
product atom :math:`\mathrm{B}` in its electronic state. The
remaining pool :math:`E_c' = E_c - E(n', v', J')_{\mathrm{AC}} -
E_{\mathrm{B}}` becomes the product relative translational energy.

Diagnostic output at start-up confirms the computed heats against
the literature. For instance, for
:math:`\mathrm{N_2} + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}` the
reported heat is :math:`\Delta H \approx -37\,732~\mathrm{K}`,
within 1% of the tabulated literature value.

Legacy input dictionaries in which the user explicitly provided the
exchange heat ``heatOfReactionExch`` are silently ignored, and such
keys may be removed for clarity.

Ionisation and charge exchange
------------------------------

When ``chargedParticles`` is enabled, the solver adds the ionisation
and charge-exchange channels. The ionisation threshold is **fully
state-dependent**: a molecule in an excited :math:`(n, v, J)` state
requires less energy to ionise than one in the ground state,

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{ion}}(n, v, J)
     = E_{\mathrm{IP}} - E_{\mathrm{int}}(n, v, J),

and ionisation proceeds whenever
:math:`E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q) \geq
E_{\mathrm{ion}}(n_P, v_P, J_P)`. On ionisation, the product cation
retains the internal state of the neutral, and a free electron is
created at the ion's position with zero relative kinetic energy in
the current implementation.

The heat of a charge-exchange reaction
:math:`\mathrm{A}^{+} + \mathrm{B} \to \mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B}^{+}`
is similarly derived from the database as

.. math::

   \Delta H_{\mathrm{cx}} = \mathrm{IP}(\mathrm{A})
                          - \mathrm{IP}(\mathrm{B}).

Associative ionisation
``````````````````````

The channel :math:`\mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B} \to \mathrm{AB}^{\star}
\to \mathrm{AB}^{+} + e^{-}` is treated in two steps: a hypothetical
intermediate AB\*\ is first formed and sampled in its rovibronic
state; if the available energy exceeds its state-dependent
ionisation threshold, the intermediate ionises, producing the cation
and the electron; otherwise the event reverts to an ordinary
collision. Associative ionisation is a minor channel in most air
chemistry calculations and currently requires user-supplied QK-type
rate coefficients.

Summary of design principles
----------------------------

It is useful to recapitulate the three guiding principles of the
rovibronic chemistry module:

* **Spectroscopic database as single source of truth** — level
  energies, partition functions, dissociation energies, and
  ionisation potentials are all read from the same
  ``constant/speciesCache/`` files.
* **No user-supplied heats of reaction** for dissociation,
  recombination, and exchange. Only the ionisation potentials
  remain an input, since they live outside the diatomic
  potential-energy-curve description.
* **Detailed balance enforced by construction** — forward rates
  are sampled from the tabulated levels, and backward rates are
  obtained through the equilibrium constant, built from the same
  tables.
