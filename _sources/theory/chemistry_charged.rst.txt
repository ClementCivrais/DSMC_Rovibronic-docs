Charged-species chemistry
=========================

.. warning::

   Every charged-species reaction class in the solver —
   electron-impact ionisation, atom-atom and molecule-atom
   ionisation channels, atom-ion / atom-electron ionisation,
   charge exchange, associative ionisation in forward and
   reverse forms, and the molecule-electron / molecule-ion
   dissociation-ionisation channels — is **work in progress**.
   It has been ported into the rovibronic framework but has not
   yet been reworked, verified, or validated end-to-end.
   Quantitative results from cases with
   ``chargedParticles = true`` must be treated as preliminary.

The charged-species chemistry inherits the Larsen-Borgnakke
machinery and the post-reaction redistribution rules from
:doc:`chemistry`, but requires additional care on three fronts:
the state-dependent ionisation threshold of the reacting
species, the ambipolar treatment of the electron population, and
the sub-time-stepping that accommodates the very high electron
collision rate. Each is discussed below.

State-dependent ionisation threshold
------------------------------------

Unlike the ground-state ionisation potential :math:`E_{\mathrm{IP}}`
tabulated for the bulk species, the ionisation threshold seen by
a molecule in an excited rovibronic state depends on its current
internal energy:

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{ion}}(n, v, J)
     = E_{\mathrm{IP}} - E(n, v, J).

A molecule that already carries internal energy
:math:`E(n, v, J)` requires only the complement
:math:`E_{\mathrm{IP}} - E(n, v, J)` to reach the ionisation
continuum. The consequence is that excited states contribute
disproportionately to the ionisation rate, by the same mechanism
as in dissociation (:doc:`chemistry`). Ionisation fires whenever

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q)
     \ge E_{\mathrm{ion}}(n_P, v_P, J_P),

where :math:`P` is the ionising parcel and :math:`Q` the collision
partner. The post-ionisation available energy

.. math::

   E_{\mathrm{avail}}^{\mathrm{ion}}
     = E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q)
       - E_{\mathrm{ion}}(n_P, v_P, J_P)

enters the Larsen-Borgnakke redistribution exactly as for the
neutral channels.

Charge exchange
---------------

The charge-exchange reaction
:math:`\mathrm{A}^{+} + \mathrm{B} \to \mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B}^{+}`
transfers a single electron between the two heavy partners. Its
heat of reaction is read directly from the database as

.. math::

   \Delta H_{\mathrm{cx}}
     = E_{\mathrm{IP}}(\mathrm{A})
       - E_{\mathrm{IP}}(\mathrm{B}).

The available energy is then

.. math::

   E_c = E_{\mathrm{rel}}
         + E_{\mathrm{int}}(P) + E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q)
         + \Delta H_{\mathrm{cx}},

and the two products are sampled in succession through the
two-stage Larsen-Borgnakke procedure of :doc:`chemistry`. No
user-supplied parameter is required beyond the ionisation
potentials already tabulated in the species database.

Associative ionisation
----------------------

Associative ionisation
:math:`\mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B} \to \mathrm{AB}^{\star}
\to \mathrm{AB}^{+} + e^{-}` proceeds in two steps: a
hypothetical intermediate :math:`\mathrm{AB}^{\star}` is first
sampled in a rovibronic state, and if the state energy lies
above the intermediate's ionisation threshold, the intermediate
ionises. Concretely:

#. **Intermediate formation.** Compute the pooled energy
   :math:`E_c = E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(A) +
   E_{\mathrm{int}}(B) + D_0(\mathrm{AB})` and sample an
   intermediate state :math:`(n^{\star}, v^{\star}, J^{\star})`
   of :math:`\mathrm{AB}^{\star}` from Larsen-Borgnakke against
   that pool.
#. **Ionisation test.** If :math:`E_c \ge
   E_{\mathrm{IP}}(\mathrm{AB}^{\star}) -
   E(n^{\star}, v^{\star}, J^{\star})`, the intermediate ionises
   and an electron is produced; otherwise the event reverts to
   an ordinary neutral collision.
#. **Product redistribution.** When ionisation fires, the
   available energy :math:`E_c -
   E_{\mathrm{IP}}(\mathrm{AB}^{\star}) +
   E(n^{\star}, v^{\star}, J^{\star})` is distributed between
   :math:`\mathrm{AB}^{+}` and the new electron through the same
   Larsen-Borgnakke machinery.

Associative ionisation is a minor channel in most air chemistry
calculations and currently requires user-supplied QK-type rate
parameters. The reverse channel
:math:`\mathrm{AB}^{+} + e^{-} \to \mathrm{A} + \mathrm{B}` is
implemented symmetrically.

Electron population: ambipolar diffusion
----------------------------------------

Tracking electrons as independent DSMC parcels on the same
time-step as the heavy species is prohibitively expensive:
electrons are lighter by four orders of magnitude and their
thermal speed is correspondingly higher, so their characteristic
collision time and advection step would dictate a global DSMC
:math:`\Delta t` two to three decades smaller than would be
needed for the heavy gas.

The solver therefore treats the electrons under the **ambipolar
approximation**: on the simulation timescale the plasma remains
quasi-neutral, and the electrons are dragged along by the ion
population through the self-consistent ambipolar electric field.
Concretely, the code does not advect electron parcels on the
global DSMC time-step. Each electron is instead **created,
stored, and moved alongside its parent ion**: whenever an
ionisation event produces an ion, the electron is added at the
ion's position (``dsmcCloud.H:323``: "Add an electron where an
ion is found, after the move function"). The electron's
contribution to the cell-level macroscopic fields —  number
density, mass density, momentum, kinetic energy, temperature —
is accumulated separately in the dedicated electron data
structures documented at ``dsmcCloud.H:211–229``.

The ambipolar approximation holds on length scales large
compared to the Debye length and on times long compared to the
plasma oscillation period. Both are satisfied for the hypersonic
re-entry regimes of interest, but would need to be revisited for
high-vacuum plasma-probe applications where the assumption
breaks down.

Electron collisions: sub-time-stepping
--------------------------------------

Although the electrons are advected through the ambipolar
approximation, their **collisions** are not: the collision layer
still needs to sample electron-neutral and electron-ion
interactions at a cadence consistent with the electron collision
rate, which can be one to two orders of magnitude higher than
the heavy-species rate.

This is handled through the ``noTimeCounterSubCycled`` variant
of the No-Time-Counter scheme (see :doc:`collision_sampling`).
Within each global DSMC step :math:`\Delta t`, the electron
collisions are sampled over :math:`n_{\mathrm{sc}}` internal
sub-cycles of length
:math:`\Delta t_{\mathrm{sc}} = \Delta t / n_{\mathrm{sc}}`, so
that the sampling resolves the electron collision frequency while
the heavy-species collision loop executes once. The
``nSubCycles`` key in the
``noTimeCounterSubCycledProperties`` sub-dictionary sets
:math:`n_{\mathrm{sc}}`; the reference value for re-entry
plasma cases is of the order of 30.

Two design choices are worth highlighting:

* **No electron-electron collisions.** Both
  ``noTimeCounter.C:259`` and
  ``majorantCollisionFrequency.C:272`` explicitly skip the
  electron-electron channel: the high electron-electron collision
  rate would swamp the DSMC candidate budget without materially
  changing the macroscopic behaviour under the ambipolar
  assumption (electron-electron collisions thermalise only the
  electron distribution, which is already Maxwellian in the
  ambipolar limit).
* **Energy book-keeping in electron sub-cycles.** Within the
  sub-cycled loop, the Larsen-Borgnakke machinery of
  :doc:`larsen_borgnakke` is invoked with the electron's
  ambipolar velocity rather than a freshly-drawn Maxwellian.
  This is consistent with the underlying assumption that the
  electron distribution is already equilibrated on the sub-cycle
  timescale.

Status
------

The entire charged-species block is flagged as work in progress
in :doc:`../classes/reactions`. The present page reflects the
intended physics and the current implementation; discrepancies
between the two, if any, will be resolved as the classes are
reworked and validated. The immediate next steps include:

* re-deriving the QK activation parameters for each charged
  channel from QCT or experimental rates;
* running the three-phase validation protocol on a
  re-entry-representative charged case;
* validating the ambipolar approximation against a
  fully-kinetic electron run in a controlled setting.
