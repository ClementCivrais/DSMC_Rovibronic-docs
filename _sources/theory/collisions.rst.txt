Binary Collisions
=================

Binary-collision sampling is the kinetic core of the DSMC method. We
follow the standard No-Time-Counter (NTC) scheme on a cell-by-cell
basis, with the cross section provided by the Variable Soft Sphere
(VSS) model, and we route every internal-energy redistribution
through the parallel-marginal cascade described in :doc:`relaxation`.

Collision-pair selection (NTC)
------------------------------

In each cell, the expected number of collision candidates over the
DSMC time-step :math:`\Delta t` is

.. math::

   N_{\mathrm{cand}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\, \frac{N(N-1)\, F_N\,
                       (\sigma_T g)_{\max}\, \Delta t}{V_c},

where :math:`N` is the number of particles in the cell,
:math:`F_N` is the equivalent-molecules factor
(``nEquivalentParticles`` in the input dictionary),
:math:`V_c` is the cell volume, and
:math:`(\sigma_T g)_{\max}` is a running estimate of the maximum
of the collisional cross section–speed product. Candidates are
drawn at random from the cell inventory and accepted with
probability :math:`\sigma_T g / (\sigma_T g)_{\max}`.

VSS cross section
-----------------

The total collisional cross section is modelled with the VSS law

.. math::

   \sigma_T(g) = \sigma_{\mathrm{ref}}
                 \left(\frac{g_{\mathrm{ref}}}{g}\right)^{2\omega - 1},

where :math:`\sigma_{\mathrm{ref}}`, :math:`g_{\mathrm{ref}}`,
:math:`\omega` (the viscosity exponent), and :math:`\alpha` (the
scattering exponent) are species-pair parameters tabulated in the
species database. Post-collision relative velocities follow the VSS
scattering law, parameterised by :math:`\alpha`, which interpolates
between isotropic (:math:`\alpha = 1`) and forward-peaked scattering.

Exponent conventions for the Larsen-Borgnakke sampler
-----------------------------------------------------

The Larsen-Borgnakke acceptance-rejection exponent used throughout
the solver is

.. math::

   \alpha_{\mathrm{LB}} =
     \frac{\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \sum_k \xi_k^{\mathrm{pooled}}}{2} - 1,

where :math:`\xi_{\mathrm{rel}}` is the translational degrees-of-
freedom (DOF) entering the relative motion, and
:math:`\xi_k^{\mathrm{pooled}}` denotes the effective DOF of those
internal modes that participate in the current event. It is
important to emphasise that the exponent must be built from the
DOF of the particle whose state is being sampled —  a subtle point
that was the source of two corrected bugs in earlier versions of
the solver, documented in ``docs/Rovibronic Article/DSMC_Bugs_Report.pdf``.

Reactive collisions
-------------------

Once a binary collision has been accepted, the collision pair is
tested in turn for:

#. inelastic internal-energy redistribution (see :doc:`relaxation`);
#. reactive channels: dissociation, recombination, exchange, and,
   when :math:`\mathrm{chargedParticles}` is enabled, ionisation
   and charge exchange (see :doc:`chemistry`).

Each channel draws from the same pool of available energy
:math:`E_c = E_{\mathrm{rel}} + E_{\mathrm{int}}(P) +
E_{\mathrm{int}}(Q)`, augmented by any heat of reaction. The
available-energy bookkeeping is the single unifying thread of the
collision logic and is where care with DOF and ground-state
normalisation pays off.
