Binary Collisions
=================

Each candidate collision is sampled according to the NTC (No Time
Counter) scheme in every computational cell. The collision cross
section follows the Variable Soft Sphere (VSS) model,

.. math::

   \sigma_T(g) = \sigma_{\text{ref}}
                 \left(\frac{g_{\text{ref}}}{g}\right)^{2\omega - 1},

with species-pair parameters :math:`\sigma_{\text{ref}}, \omega, \alpha`
tabulated per interaction in the species database.

For a sampled binary collision, the solver then:

#. Re-distributes translational energy using the VSS scattering law.
#. Applies internal-energy redistribution via the selected relaxation
   submodel (see :doc:`relaxation`).
#. Tests for reactive channels (see :doc:`chemistry`).

State-resolved inelastic collisions
-----------------------------------

Because the internal energy is quantised, vibrational and rotational
energy exchange draws transitions :math:`(e, v, j) \to (e', v', j')`
according to state-specific rates (or effective Landau–Teller /
Parker models, depending on ``relaxationScheme``). Detailed balance
is enforced against the level degeneracies defined in
:doc:`rovibronic_levels`.
