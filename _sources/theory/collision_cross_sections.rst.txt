Collision cross sections
========================

The present section documents the binary-collision cross-section
models implemented in the solver: the Hard Sphere (HS) reference,
the Variable Hard Sphere (VHS), the Variable Soft Sphere (VSS),
and the degenerate ``NoBinaryCollision`` model used for
free-molecular verification. All models are selected through the
``BinaryCollisionModel`` key in ``constant/dsmcProperties``; the
associated coefficients live in ``<Model>Coeffs``.

Hard sphere cross section
-------------------------

Hard sphere collisions are the oldest kinetic-theory model and
serve as the pedagogical starting point. Two molecules of
diameters :math:`d_1, d_2` are treated as solid spheres that
collide elastically when :math:`|\mathbf{r}_1 - \mathbf{r}_2| =
(d_1 + d_2)/2`. The resulting total cross section,

.. math::

   \sigma_T^{\mathrm{HS}}
     = \pi\, d_{12}^{2}, \qquad
   d_{12} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,(d_1 + d_2),

is **independent of the relative speed**. The differential cross
section is isotropic in the centre-of-mass frame. The HS model is
easy to reason about but has a major limitation: it does not
reproduce the experimental temperature dependence of gas
viscosity, which goes as :math:`\eta(T) \propto T^{\omega}` with
:math:`\omega \approx 0.75` for most diatomic species instead of
the HS prediction :math:`\omega = 1/2`.

Variable Hard Sphere (VHS)
--------------------------

The VHS model of Bird [Bird1981c]_ retains the isotropic
scattering of the hard sphere but gives the cross section an
explicit dependence on the relative speed :math:`g`:

.. math::

   \sigma_T^{\mathrm{VHS}}(g)
     = \pi\, d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}\,
       \left(\frac{g_{\mathrm{ref}}}{g}\right)^{2\omega - 1}
       \frac{1}{\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)},

where :math:`d_{\mathrm{ref}}`, :math:`g_{\mathrm{ref}}`, and
:math:`\omega` are species-pair parameters. The reference speed is
related to the reference temperature :math:`T_{\mathrm{ref}}`
(tabulated at 273 K in the reference tutorial) through the most
probable speed
:math:`g_{\mathrm{ref}} = \sqrt{2 k_B T_{\mathrm{ref}}/\mu_{12}}`
with :math:`\mu_{12}` the reduced mass of the colliding pair.

The viscosity of a VHS gas is

.. math::

   \eta^{\mathrm{VHS}}(T)
     = \frac{15}{8}\,\frac{\sqrt{\pi m k_B T}}{\pi d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}}\,
       \frac{\Gamma(4 - \omega)}{\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)\,
        \Gamma(4 - \omega)}\,
       \left(\frac{T}{T_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right)^{\omega},

which recovers the experimental :math:`T^{\omega}` scaling exactly
by construction. The angular distribution remains isotropic in the
centre-of-mass frame: the post-collision relative velocity is
drawn as

.. math::

   \mathbf{g}' = g\,\hat{\mathbf{n}}, \qquad
   \hat{\mathbf{n}} = \bigl(\cos\theta\,\hat{\mathbf{e}}_{z}
                           + \sin\theta\,\cos\phi\,\hat{\mathbf{e}}_{x}
                           + \sin\theta\,\sin\phi\,\hat{\mathbf{e}}_{y}\bigr),

with :math:`\cos\theta` uniform on :math:`[-1, 1]` and
:math:`\phi` uniform on :math:`[0, 2\pi)`.

Variable Soft Sphere (VSS)
--------------------------

The VSS model of Koura and Matsumoto [Koura1991c]_ extends VHS by
augmenting the same speed-dependent total cross section with an
**anisotropic** scattering law parameterised by :math:`\alpha`.
The differential cross section in the centre-of-mass frame is

.. math::

   \frac{\mathrm{d}\sigma^{\mathrm{VSS}}}{\mathrm{d}\Omega}
     = \frac{\sigma_T^{\mathrm{VSS}}(g)}{4\pi\,\alpha}\,
       \left[\cos^{2(\alpha - 1)}\!\tfrac{\chi}{2}\right]
       \left[\frac{1}{\cos^{2}(\chi/2)}\right],

which concentrates the scattered flux in the forward direction
when :math:`\alpha > 1`. The scattering angle is sampled from

.. math::

   \cos\chi
     = 2\,U^{1/\alpha} - 1,

with :math:`U` uniform on :math:`[0, 1]`; the limit
:math:`\alpha = 1` recovers the isotropic VHS scattering.
Simultaneously fitting :math:`(d_{\mathrm{ref}}, \omega, \alpha)`
to the gas viscosity *and* the binary diffusion coefficient
removes the Schmidt-number inconsistency present in the VHS model.
For common diatomic species the fitted :math:`\alpha` lies
between 1.0 and 1.5.

Reference parameters for air-chemistry species are tabulated in
``constant/speciesCache/<species>.dat`` and are documented in
:doc:`../classes/species_database`.

Post-collision velocities
-------------------------

Whatever the choice of cross section, the post-collision
velocities follow from momentum conservation and the sampled
relative velocity:

.. math::

   \mathbf{v}_{P}' = \mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{cm}}
                    + \frac{m_Q}{m_P + m_Q}\,\mathbf{g}', \quad
   \mathbf{v}_{Q}' = \mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{cm}}
                    - \frac{m_P}{m_P + m_Q}\,\mathbf{g}',

with :math:`\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{cm}} = (m_P \mathbf{v}_P +
m_Q \mathbf{v}_Q)/(m_P + m_Q)` the centre-of-mass velocity. The
magnitude of :math:`\mathbf{g}'` equals :math:`g` for an elastic
collision; it is smaller for an inelastic one after Larsen-
Borgnakke sampling has transferred energy to the internal modes
(see :doc:`larsen_borgnakke`).

Elastic versus inelastic variants
---------------------------------

The solver distinguishes between collision models that include
internal-energy exchange and those that do not:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 40 60

   * - Class
     - Internal-energy treatment
   * - ``VariableHardSphere``
     - Purely elastic; internal state unchanged on every
       collision. Useful for verification against analytical
       free-molecular results.
   * - ``VariableSoftSphere``
     - Purely elastic; anisotropic scattering.
   * - ``LarsenBorgnakkeVariableHardSphere``
     - VHS cross section + LB internal-energy redistribution on
       the rovibronic state space. **Default** production model.
   * - ``LarsenBorgnakkeVariableSoftSphere``
     - VSS cross section + LB internal-energy redistribution.
   * - ``NoBinaryCollision``
     - Binary collisions disabled. Used to isolate boundary-
       driven effects in free-molecular tests.

Coefficient dictionaries
------------------------

For the VHS/VSS elastic variants, ``<Model>Coeffs`` is essentially
empty beyond the reference temperature:

.. code-block:: cpp

   VariableHardSphereCoeffs { Tref 273; }
   VariableSoftSphereCoeffs { Tref 273; }

For the LB variants, the same block carries the three relaxation
collision numbers :math:`Z_r, Z_{rv}, Z_{rve}` that govern the
parallel-marginal cascade (:doc:`relaxation`):

.. code-block:: cpp

   LarsenBorgnakkeVariableHardSphereCoeffs
   {
       Tref                                    273; // [K]
       rotationalRelaxationCollisionNumber     2;
       rovibrationalRelaxationCollisionNumber  10;
       rovibronicRelaxationCollisionNumber     10;
   }
