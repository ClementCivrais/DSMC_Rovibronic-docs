Binary collision models

Source: src/lagrangian/DSMC/collisions/derived/.

Role

Binary collision models prescribe two things: the cross section used to weigh accepted collision candidates, and the post-collision scattering and internal-energy redistribution that is applied once a collision has been accepted. They are selected through the BinaryCollisionModel key in constant/dsmcProperties and configured through the associated <Model>Coeffs sub-dictionary.

Implemented classes

Class

Purpose

LarsenBorgnakkeVariableHardSphere

Variable Hard Sphere (VHS) cross section with Larsen-Borgnakke (LB) energy redistribution; the default and most frequently used model.

LarsenBorgnakkeVariableSoftSphere

Variable Soft Sphere (VSS) cross section with LB energy redistribution; adds the anisotropic scattering exponent \(\alpha\), which interpolates between isotropic and forward-peaked scattering.

VariableHardSphere

Plain VHS without LB: purely elastic collisions, no internal-energy exchange. Useful for debugging, or for cases in which internal degrees of freedom are not of interest.

VariableSoftSphere

Plain VSS variant of the above.

NoBinaryCollision

Disables binary collisions entirely. Used for free- molecular verification cases and for isolating boundary-driven effects.

VHS cross section

The Variable Hard Sphere cross section depends on the relative velocity \(g\) through the viscosity exponent \(\omega\):

\[\sigma_T^{\mathrm{VHS}}(g) = \pi\, d_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}\, \left(\frac{g_{\mathrm{ref}}^{2}}{g^{2}}\right)^{\omega - 1/2} \frac{1}{\Gamma(5/2 - \omega)},\]

where \(d_{\mathrm{ref}}\), \(g_{\mathrm{ref}}\), and \(\omega\) are species-pair parameters tabulated in dsmcSpeciesDatabase — spectroscopic & VSS data. The VHS law is isotropic in the centre- of-mass frame.

VSS cross section and scattering

The Variable Soft Sphere model augments VHS with an anisotropic scattering law parameterised by \(\alpha\). The differential cross section is

\[\frac{\mathrm{d}\sigma}{\mathrm{d}\Omega} \propto \cos^{2(\alpha - 1)}\!\left(\tfrac{\chi}{2}\right),\]

where \(\chi\) is the centre-of-mass scattering angle. The limit \(\alpha = 1\) recovers the isotropic VHS scattering; values \(\alpha > 1\), which are commonly fitted to viscosity and diffusion data, yield forward-peaked scattering consistent with the Lennard-Jones interaction of diatomics.

Larsen-Borgnakke energy redistribution

On each accepted collision, the LB variants sample a new internal energy for the participating parcels, using the acceptance- rejection criterion

\[f(E') = g(E')\, \left(\frac{E_c - E'}{E_c}\right)^{\alpha_{\mathrm{LB}}}, \qquad \alpha_{\mathrm{LB}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(\xi_{\mathrm{rel}} + \xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}\bigr) - 1,\]

where \(E_c\) is the pooled collision energy, \(g(E')\) is the degeneracy of the candidate state, and \(\xi_{\mathrm{int}}^{\mathrm{pool}}\) is the effective number of internal degrees of freedom that enter the current event. The rovibronic extension evaluates \(g(E')\) and \(E'\) on the quantised state space rather than on a continuous internal-energy variable; the three composite-mode cascades (rotational, rovibrational, rovibronic) that build on top of this acceptance rule are documented in Internal-energy relaxation.

Coefficient sub-dictionary

For the LB variants, the <Model>Coeffs block holds the reference temperature and the three relaxation collision numbers \(Z_r,\,Z_{rv},\,Z_{rve}\):

LarsenBorgnakkeVariableHardSphereCoeffs
{
    Tref                                    273; // [K]
    rotationalRelaxationCollisionNumber     2;   // [-]
    rovibrationalRelaxationCollisionNumber  10;  // [-]
    rovibronicRelaxationCollisionNumber     10;  // [-]
}

The plain VHS/VSS variants do not expose the collision-number entries, since they do not redistribute internal energy.
