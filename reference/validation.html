Validation

We collect in the present page the canonical checks that are exercised whenever the rovibronic machinery is modified. The scope is deliberately lightweight — the goal is to assemble a coherent baseline against which regressions can be detected (see the planned Regression tests (roadmap)), rather than to revisit every published validation case.

Heat-bath relaxation (Landau-Teller)

The four-phase isothermal heat-bath protocol is the primary validation of the parallel-marginal relaxation cascade with the Nizenkov Z-correction. For each phase only one channel is active (the other relaxation collision numbers are set to \(10^{6}\)), so the DSMC relaxation of the active mode can be compared directly against the analytical Landau-Teller (LT) curve — no fit is used; the reference equilibrium \(E_{\mathrm{eq}}\) is taken from the rovibronic database restricted to the state space actually reachable by the sampler.

* Phase 3 shows a small mid-decay residual relative to the single- \(\tau\) LT curve; this is the bi-exponential physics signature of spin-conserving relaxation — fast \((v,J)\) equilibration inside \(n=0\) followed by slow access to the A’³Δu and A³Σu+ triplet states at \(\sim\!50\,000\)K. Phase 4 does not show this because the low-lying singlets a¹Δg (∼11,400K) and b¹Σg+ (∼19,000K) equilibrate on the same timescale as \((v,J)\) inside \(n=0\).

Key ingredients that make the validation succeed:

  • Window-select dispatch in dsmcCloud::postCollisionInternalEnergy replaces the earlier nested conditional cascade. A single uniform \(R\) selects exactly one of \(\{E_0,\,E_1,\,E_2,\,E_3\}\) with marginal probabilities \(P_k = 1/(Z_k \cdot C_k)\). This is what enables clean phase-by-phase isolation.

  • State-consistent pre-tabulation in dsmcCloud::buildZCorrTables:

    • meanRotEnergyTable_[tid][n][v][iT] — J-Boltzmann per \((n,v)\) ladder;

    • meanRovibEnergyTable_[tid][n][iT] — \((v,J)\)-Boltzmann at each electronic state;

    • meanIntEnergyTable_[tid][\mathrm{spinIdx}][iT] — \((n,v,J)\)-Boltzmann restricted to each spin class of the species.

    Each parcel looks up its own \((n_p, v_p)\) / \(n_p\) / spin class in the relevant table — the DOF used in the LB exponent is therefore consistent with the sub-manifold being sampled.

  • Species-general cell reference in dsmcCloud::updateZCorrections: cell-level \(\langle E_k\rangle(T)\) is a parcel-count-weighted mixture over every molecular species in the cell; no “first molecular species” shortcut.

The derivation of \(C_k\) per composite mode and the protocol itself are documented in doc/LT_Correction_MultiMode.pdf in the source tree.

Two-temperature recombination and three-body channel

The 2-T recombination path, together with the three-body recombinePQ channel, has been exercised in zero-dimensional and one-dimensional canonical cases. Two historical failure modes have been isolated and fixed in the course of validation:

  • a Larsen-Borgnakke NaN, traced to unbounded inelastic energy sampling under a specific configuration of reaction exponents;

  • a dissociation double-count bug associated with the wrong branch of the collision index in the early charge-exchange implementation.

The full list of bug-audit findings, together with the corresponding fixes and short justifications, is collected in docs/Rovibronic Article/DSMC_Bugs_Report.pdf in the source tree.

Heats of reaction

Startup diagnostics print the automatically-computed heats of reaction \(\Delta H = D_0(\mathrm{products}) - D_0(\mathrm{reactants})\) for each registered exchange and recombination channel. Representative values for air chemistry agree with the literature to within 1%:

  • \(\mathrm{N}_2 + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}\): \(\Delta H \approx -37\,700~\mathrm{K}\) (lit.) vs. \(-37\,732~\mathrm{K}\) (database).

  • \(\mathrm{O}_2 + \mathrm{N} \to \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{O}\): \(\Delta H \approx +16\,000~\mathrm{K}\) (lit.) vs. \(+16\,150~\mathrm{K}\) (database).

Shock profiles

One-dimensional normal-shock profile benchmarks against the Park and Macheret reference data, as published in [Civrais2024Shock_v], are tracked as separate cases under run/. Re-running these after a substantive change to the collision or chemistry modules is the recommended next-level validation beyond the heat-bath test.
